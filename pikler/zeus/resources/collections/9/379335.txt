Freeport, Nova Scotia    freeport , nova scotia freeport , nova scotia is a village in digby country , nova scotia and it is located on long island in the bay of fundy . located at the end of digby neck on long island , freeport is a working fishing village built up around a tidal basin . this area is great for bird watching , beach combing , hiking trails , birds , whale watching , gift shops , accommodations , restaurants , and unique flora and fauna . loyalists founded freeport in 1784 establishing fishing and trading industries . prior to this it had been inhabited by the mi ’ kmaq indians . place names such as petit passage , grand passage and the saint mary ’ s bay can be attributed to the french explorer , champlain , who first visited the area in 1604 .
