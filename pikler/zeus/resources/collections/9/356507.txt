Type site     in archaeology , a type site ( or type-site , or typesite ) is a site that is typical of a particular archaeological culture . for example , the type site of the pre-pottery neolithic a culture is jericho , in the west bank , while the type site of the pre-celtic/celtic bronze age hallstatt culture is the lakeside village of hallstatt , austria . a type site contains artifacts which are typical of that culture . type sites are often the first or foundational site discovered about the culture they represent . in geology the term is used similarly for a site considered to be typical of a particular rock formation etc . the use of this term is similar to that of the type specimen in biology
