Lipopolysaccharide     lipopolysaccharides ( lps ) , also known as lipoglycans , are large molecules made of a lipid and a polysaccharide joined by a covalent bond . they occur in the outer membrane of gram-negative bacteria , and act as triggers for the innate immune system . their presence is sensed by toll-like receptors on dendritic cells . this causes a strong immune response in animals .
