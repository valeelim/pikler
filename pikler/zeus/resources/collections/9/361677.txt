Scarlet tiger moth     the scarlet tiger moth ( `` callimorpha dominula '' , formerly `` panaxia dominula '' ) is a colourful dayflying moth of europe and the near east . it belongs to the tiger moth family , arctiidae . the caterpillars feed mostly on comfrey ( `` symphytum officinale '' ) . the moth imago flies by day , and has both cryptic colouring on the front wings , and warning colour on the hind wings . if disturbed it jinks the front wings forward to show the red warning flash on the hind wings . it can occur in rare colour morphs , one with yellow hindwings and body and one with extended black on hindwings . yellow , black and red are common warning colours in insects and other animals . the three morphs occurring in the population at the cothill reserve in oxfordshire have been studied for many years , including research by e.b . ford and r.a. fisher . mcnamara ( 1998 ) describes how amateurs can rear this species .
