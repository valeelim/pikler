Albert Namatjira     albert namatjira ( 28 july 1902 â€“ 8 august 1959 ) , born elea namatjira , was an indigenous australian artist . he is one of australia 's most well-known painters . he is best known for his watercolour paintings of the australian outback landscape . his works were not in the traditional style of aboriginal art . but they became important to forming the style of modern indigenous art in australia . namatjira was an arrernte man from the western macdonnell ranges area . he was born at hermannsburg , a lutheran mission near alice springs . he was born as elea , but once baptised , his parents changed his name to albert . he had a western-style upbringing on the mission . when he turned 13 , though , he returned to the bush for his initiation . he was made a member of the arrernte community and was introduced to his traditional heritage . namatjira said this is how he came to understand the kinship with his land that is seen in his works . he eventually become an elder of the arrernte community .
