Cayo District     cayo district is a district in the west of the nation of belize . the district capital is the town of san ignacio . the cayo district is the largest in belize . it is on the western side of the country which borders guatemala . the nation 's capital , belmopan , is in the district . the cayo district has the pre-columbian maya ruins of xunantunich , cahal pech , chaa creek , and caracol . major rivers in the district include the macal river and the mopan river . the cayo district is mostly an agricultural district . the chief crops are citrus , ( oranges , grapefruit , and tangerine ) , as well as bananas . recently oil was found in spanish lookout and is now in production . in addition to the ruins listed above there are several other important nature reserves including two national parks , blue hole ( park ) and guanacaste national park . ecotourism is also an important part of the region 's economy . according to the 2000 census , cayo district had a population of 53,715 people .
