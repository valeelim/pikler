Atheris     atheris is a genus of venomous vipers found only in rain forest areas of sub-saharan africa . there are currently eight species found . atheris are commonly known as `` bush vipers '' . they are all arboreal ( meaning they live in trees ) , but they can sometimes be seen on the ground . bush vipers are small snakes , with adults growing to the size of around 40 to 78 cm . they all have broad , triangular heads with large eyes and a slender body . the bush viper lives in rain forest areas of sub-saharan africa , far from humans , but some bush vipers are threatened because of habitat loss . bush vipers eat small amphibians like frogs , and toads , reptiles like lizards and other snakes , rodents and birds . they ambush their prey by hanging on to a branch of a tree , waiting for a prey to come close , then jump on the prey and inject venom , and then swallow it . some bush vipers in zoos only eat mice and small birds , but there have been reports on some eating each other . all bush vipers are viviparous , meaning they give live birth . they mate between september and november , and females give to their young in march or april .
