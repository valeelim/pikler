Small Smiles Dental Centers     small smiles dental centers ( called church street health management and previously called forba ) is a company that manages dental offices in the united states for poor people . small smiles has 70 centers in 22 states and washington , dc . it has its headquarters in nashville , tennessee . the clinics are called many names , including children 's dental clinic , indian springs dental clinic , oklahoma smiles , small smiles dentistry , texas smiles , and wild smiles . the clinic comes from a dental clinic in pueblo , colorado that was started in 1928. the derose family and dr. william mueller owned the company . in 2003 michael derose , one of the deroses , bought a very expensive house in pueblo for $ 3.4 million . when they owned the clinics , they put children in `` papoose boards '' to stop them from moving so they could quickly do dental work . the state of colorado made a law to limit how much they could use the papoose boards . the offices also did lots of dental work on the children and put a lot of crowns on the children 's teeth , so the colorado government said that the clinics could only place a specified number crowns for each day . in 2006 the derose family sold the company to new owners .
