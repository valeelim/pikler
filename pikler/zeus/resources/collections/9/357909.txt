Muscadine     muscadine is a grapevine species . it is in the southeastern united states . they grow well in a warm and humid climate . muscadine berries are bronze to dark purple to black in color when ripe . however , many wild varieties stay green . their skin is very tough . to eat the raw fruit a person bites a small hole in the skin to suck out the pulp inside . muscadines are not only eaten fresh , but also are used in making wine , juice , and jelly .
