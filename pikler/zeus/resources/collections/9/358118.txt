Adèle Dumilâtre     adèle alphonsine dumilâtre ( 30 june 18214 may 1909 ) was a french ballerina associated with the romantic ballet . she was born in paris to michel dumilâtre , an actor in the comédie-française . he hoped she would become a tragedienne , but her dance talent led her to the ballet of the paris opéra . she performed from 1840 to 1848. she rose quickly from minor parts to principal roles . at eighteen she danced `` la sylphide '' . she created the roles of myrtha in `` giselle '' and the title role in `` lady henriette '' . rich gentlemen sustained her power at the opéra . she danced in milan and london where she created the title role in `` the marble maiden '' ( 1845 ) by arthur saint-léon . she married louis drake y del castillo in 1848 and bore him three children . she died in paris and was buried in the montmartre cemetery . her older sister sophie was also a prominent parisian ballerina .
