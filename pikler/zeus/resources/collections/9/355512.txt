Pediatric acute-onset neuropsychiatric syndrome (PANS)    pediatric acute-onset neuropsychiatric syndrome ( pans ) pediatric acute-onset neuropsychiatric syndrome ( pans ) is the name of a disease that normally begins in childhood . it causes obsessive-compulsive disorder ( ocd ) and/or tics . the symptoms begin very suddenly . if it is not treated at the beginning the disease can be there also in adults . the reason for pans can be a bacteria called `` group a streptococcus '' ( pandas type ) or an immunologic reaction to other pathogens like certain bacteria or viruses . ( pitands type ) . patients suffering from pans show sudden begin ocd and/or tics and at least two of these symptoms : 1. obsessive-compulsive disorder , or finding it hard to eat . this begins very suddenly and very intensely . 1. at the same time , more neuropsychiatric symptoms begin . these are just as quick and severe . the symptoms are from at least two of these categories : 1. fear with or without a reason ( anxiety ) 1. problems with feelings and/or sadness ( depression ) 1. aggression 1. behavioral regression ( acting like a younger child )
