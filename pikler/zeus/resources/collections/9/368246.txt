Robert FitzRoy     robert fitzroy ( 5 july 1805 â€“ 30 april 1865 ) was a british seaman , admiral , meteorologist and hydrographer . he was the captain of hms beagle in the voyage of the english naturalist charles darwin . he was governor of new zealand from 1843 to 1845. in 1851 , fitzroy was elected to the royal society . in 1854 , on the recommendation of vice admiral francis beaufort and the president of the royal society , fitzroy was appointed as chief of a new department to deal with the collection of weather data at sea , with the title of `` meteorological statist to the board of trade '' and a staff of three . this was the forerunner of the modern meteorological office .
