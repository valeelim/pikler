Egyptian Plover     the egyptian plover is a wader . it is the only member of the genus `` pluvianus '' . it is sometimes called the crocodile bird . this is because of a story dating to herodotus . in the story the crocodiles lie on the shore with their mouths open . the plovers fly into the crocodiles ' mouths to feed on bits of meat between the crocodiles ' teeth . there is no evidence of this actually happening . the egyptian plover lives in tropical sub-saharan africa . it breeds on sandbars in large rivers . its two or three eggs are buried in warm sand . it feeds by pecking for insects . the call is a high-pitched `` krrr-krrr-krrr '' . other websites . - egyptian plover at encyclopedia.thefreedictionary.com
