Rogier van der Weyden     rogier van der weyden or roger de la pasture ( 1399 or 1400 – 18 june 1464 ) was an early flemish painter . his surviving works are mainly religious triptychs , altarpieces and commissioned single and diptych portraits . he was very successful and internationally famous in his lifetime . his paintings went to italy and spain . by the end of the 15th century , he had become more popular than jan van eyck . his fame lasted only until the 17th century . due to the loss of archives in 1695 and again in 1940 , there are few certain facts of van der weyden 's life . rogelet de le pasture ( roger of the pasture ) was born in tournai in 1399 or 1400. his parents were henri de le pasture and agnes de watrélos . he married around 1426 , to elisabeth goffaert . he was made town painter of brussels in 1436. he changed his name from the french to the dutch format , becoming 'van der weyden ' .
