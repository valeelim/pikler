Alkali Ridge     alkali ridge , also known as alkali point , is a set of widely scattered archaeological remains of the earliest forms of puebloan architecture . they represent a period of change from scattered , pit-style dwellings to a settled agricultural lifestyle . these multi-story buildings and kivas have yielded high-quality ceramics . they form the type location for the pueblo ii period ( `` ca . '' 900 ce - `` ca . '' 1100 ce ) . it was declared a national historic landmark in 1964. other websites . - blm page on the alkali ridge area
