Bearded vulture     the bearded vulture ( `` gypaetus barbatus '' ) , also known as the lammergeier or lammergeyer , is a bird of prey . it is the only member of the genus gypaetus . an old world vulture , it forms a minor line of the accipitridae with the egyptian vulture , its closest living relative . they are not much more closely related to the old world vultures proper than to hawks . they differ from old world vultures by their feathered neck . it eats mainly carrion and lives and breeds on crags in high mountains in southern europe , the caucasus , africa , the indian subcontinent , and tibet . the females lay one or two eggs in mid-winter that hatch at the beginning of spring . populations are resident .
