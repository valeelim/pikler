Four Freedoms     the four freedoms is an important concept of american president franklin d. roosevelt . he presented them in the `` state of the union '' of january 6 , 1941 , which is therefore also called the `` four freedoms speech '' . roosevelt made the `` four freedoms '' public just eleven months before the american participation at world war ii . the concept played an important role in the american propaganda during the war . after roosevelts death in 1945 , his wife eleanor continued being an important advocate for including the `` four freedoms '' in the universal declaration of human rights . 1. the first is freedom of speech and expression—everywhere in the world . 1. the second is freedom of every person to worship god in his own way—everywhere in the world . 1. the third is freedom from want which , translated into world terms , means economic understandings which will secure to every nation a healthy peacetime life for its inhabitants—everywhere in the world . 1. the fourth is freedom from fear which , translated into world terms , means a world-wide reduction of armaments to such a point and in such a thorough fashion that no nation will be in a position to commit an act of physical aggression against any neighbor—anywhere in the world .
