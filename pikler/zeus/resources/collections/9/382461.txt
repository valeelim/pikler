Charioteer of Delphi     the charioteer of delphi is a greek bronze sculpture from the early classical period , about 477 bc . it stands 1.8 m tall . it was discovered in the sanctuary of apollo at delphi during excavations by french archaeologists in 1896. with the statue were found fragments of a sculpted chariot , sculpted horses , and a small , sculpted person , presumably the charioteer 's groom . all would have been part of a large monument . the charioteer monument was commissioned by polyzalos , a sicilian tyrant , to commemorate his victory in the chariot races about 477 bc . at some point , according to greek practice , the statue was buried , probably because of irreparable damage .
