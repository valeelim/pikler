Jōkyō calendar     , also called teikyō-reki , was a japanese lunisolar calendar ( `` genka reki '' ) . it was used from 1684-1753. it was made official in 1685. the `` jōkyō-reki '' system was the work of shibukawa shunkai . he recognized that the length of the solar year is 365.2417 days . shirakawa discovered errors in the traditional chinese calendar which had been in use for 800 years . this older system was also known as the `` semmyō '' calendar or 'semmei-reki '' . it was also known as the `` xuanming '' calendar . related pages . - japanese calendar - sexagenary cycle - jōkyō - hōryaku calendar - kansei calendar - tenpō calendar other websites . - national diet library , japanese calendar ''
