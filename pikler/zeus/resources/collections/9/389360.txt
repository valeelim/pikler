Elizabeth Fry     elizabeth `` betsy '' fry ( 21 may 1780 – 12 october 1845 ) , `` née '' gurney , was an english prison reformer and social reformer . she was also a quaker and a christian philanthropist . she has sometimes been referred to as the `` angel of prisons '' . fry was very important in the creation of legislation to make the treatment of prisoners more humane . she was supported in her efforts by the monarch at the time , queen victoria . she was especially interested in women and children . fry also wrote a book about prisons in scotland and northern england . since 2001 , she has been shown on the bank of england £5 note . fry was born in the gurney court which is in norwich , norfolk . the gurneys were a quaker family . her family home was called the earlham hall . this is now part of the university of east anglia . john gurney ( her father ) worked in the gurney 's bank . catherine ( her mother ) was part of the barclay family . they were the founders of the barclay 's bank . elizabeth 's mother died when she was only 12 years old .
