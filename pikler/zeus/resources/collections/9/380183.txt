Charlecote Park     charlecote park is a house and park owned by the national trust . it is located 4 miles east of stratford-upon-avon , and 6 miles south of warwick on the b4086 stratford/banbury road . the house and park were owned by the lucy family from the 13th century to 1945 , when they were presented to the national trust . sir thomas lucy tore down an earlier structure to make way for the present house in 1558. he was a man of wealth . he entertained queen elizabeth i at breakfast in 1572 as she made her way to kenilworth . lucy is better known for having fined the young william shakespeare over a poaching incident in 1583. this incident prompted shakespeare to flee warwickshire for london . charlecote was inherited in the direct male line until george lucy died in 1786. this man improved the house . he hired capability brown to improve the park about 1769. in the early years of the 19th century , another george lucy continued improvements , leaving little unchanged except for the gatehouse .
