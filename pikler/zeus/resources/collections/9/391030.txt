Division of Bendigo     the division of bendigo is an australian electoral division in victoria . the division was created in 1900 first federal election in 1901. it is named for the city of bendigo . in the early years was covered only bendigo , but on later boundaries the seat has included towns such as echuca , castlemaine , maryborough and seymour . it now includes bendigo , castlemaine , kyneton and maldon . bendigo has often been held by both the australian labor party and the conservative parties . bendigo has had 15 members , the second-highest number ( with denison ) of any federal electorate . famous members have been its first member , sir john quick , who was a leading federalist , and prime minister billy hughes . hughes , who came from sydney , represented bendigo for two terms at a time when the federal parliament met in melbourne . john brumby , who held the seat from 1983 to 1990 , later became premier of victoria . election results . - 2013 election results - 2010 election results - 2007 election results
