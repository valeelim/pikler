Élie Ducommun    élie ducommun élie ducommun ( 19 february 1833 – 7 december 1906 ) was a swiss peace activist . he won the 1902 nobel peace prize , which he shared with charles albert gobat . ducommun was born in geneva switzerland on december 19 , 1833. ducommun worked as a tutor , language teacher , journalist and a translator for the swiss federal chancellery ( 1869–1873 ) . in 1867 , he helped to found the ( league for peace and liberty ) , though he continued working at other positions , including secretary for the jura-simplon steel company from 1873 to 1891. that year , he was appointed director of the newly formed ( international peace office ) , the first non-governmental international peace organization , based in bern . he refused to accept a salary for the position , stating that he wished to serve in this capacity solely for reasons of idealism . his keen organizational skills ensured the group 's success . he was awarded in the nobel peace prize in 1902 with charles albert gobart . he served as director of the organization until his death .
