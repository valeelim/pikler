Malakunanja II     malakunanja ii is a rock shelter in arnhem land , in the northern territory of australia . it is about 50 kms from the coast . archaeologists discovered that people have been living in the protection of the rock for at least 60,000 years.. this is the oldest site showing the presence of humans in australia . more than 1500 items have been found in the shelter , including stone tools and grinding stones , ochre , and charcoal . some of these were buried more than 2.5 metres below the surface . the shelter also includes faded paintings on the walls . the paintings are drawn with ochre .
