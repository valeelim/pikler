Clarias cavernicola     the golden cave catfish , `` clarias cavernicola '' , is a species of freshwater catfish . they are only known to live in one place : an underground lake in aigamas cave , which is near otavi , in the kalahari desert of namibia . the lake is in area and about deep . the water level has been steadily dropping because it is extracted by humans . the lake is a major water supply in an otherwise very dry area . because of this , the species is considered critically endangered . it is estimated that there are about 150 individuals living in the lake . the fish live in the shallow areas of the lake in open , clear water . they feed on detritus ( bat droppings and carcasses ) and insects that fall into the lake . not much is known about how the species reproduces . attempts to breed the fish in captivity have failed . the fish grow up to long . having evolved in the darkness of the cave , they have no pigment . the fish is white in colour . it has a long eel-like body , with long dorsal and anal fins . the head has a rounded snout with four pairs of thin barbels . the fish have very small eyes , which are covered with skin . they are blind , and find food using taste and other senses on the barbels .
