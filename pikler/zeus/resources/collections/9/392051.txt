White-headed capuchin     the white-headed capuchin ( `` cebus capucinus '' ) is a medium-sized new world monkey of the family cebidae , subfamily cebinae . it is also known as the white-faced capuchin or the white-throated capuchin . it comes from the forests of central america . it also lives in the extreme northwestern part of south america . the white-headed capuchin is important to rain forests because of its role in dispersing seeds and pollen . in recent years the species has become popular in north american media . it is a very intelligent monkey and has been trained to assist paraplegic people . the monkey is medium-sized . it can weigh up to . the color is mostly black but they have a pink face . they also have white on the front part of the body . it has a distinctive prehensile tail that is coiled up and is used to help support the monkey on a branch . in the wild , the white-headed capuchin can live in many different types of forest . they can eat many different types of food , like fruit , other plants , insects , and small vertebrates . it lives in troops ( groups ) that can have more than 20 animals , both males and females . it is noted for its tool use . they can rub plants over their fur in using herbal medicine . they can also use tools as weapons and for getting food . the monkey has a maximum recorded age of over 54 years .
