Jiroemon Kimura     jiroemon kimura ( 木村 次郎右衛門 , 19 april 1897 – 12 june 2013 ) was a japanese supercentenarian , who has been listed by the guinness world records as the oldest living person in the world since the death of dina manfredini on december 17 , 2012. kimura became the oldest man ever verified by the guinness world records after his lifespan became longer than that of christian mortensen on december 28 , 2012. he was the last living man who was born before the 1900s , as well as the oldest and last verified person ever to have been born in the year 1897. kimura was born as kinjiro miyake ( 三宅 金治郎 miyake kinjirō ) in the fishing village of kamiukawa . after he finished school at the age of 14 , he worked for local post offices for over 45 years until he retired in 1962 at the age of 65. in the 1920 's , he also worked as a government communications worker in korea , which was at the time a colony of japan . after he returned from korea , he married yae kimura and changed his name to jiroemon kimura , since his wife 's family lacked a male heir . they were married until her death in 1978 and had seven children , five of which are still living . he lives in kyōtango , kyoto prefecture , japan with his eldest son 's widow and grandson 's widow .
