John Dryden     john dryden ( 9 august 1631 – 1 may 1700 ) was an english poet laureate . he was the oldest of fourteen children born to erasmus dryden and wife mary pickering . as a boy dryden lived in the nearby village of titchmarsh , northamptonshire . in 1644 he was sent to westminster school . in the late twentieth century a house at westminster was founded in his name . in 1650 dryden went up to trinity college , cambridge . after graduating , he went to london . dryden got work with the secretary of state , john thurloe . on 1 december 1663 dryden married the royalist sister of sir robert howard—lady elizabeth . they had three sons . his first play , `` the wild gallant '' was in 1663. it was not successful . but from 1668 on he was contracted to make three plays a year for the king 's company . books about dryden . - editions - biography - modern criticism
