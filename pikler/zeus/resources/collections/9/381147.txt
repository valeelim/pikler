The Dancing Class     the dancing class is an oil painting by edgar degas painted about 1870. it was the first of his ballet pictures . it depicts a dance class at the paris opéra . when he painted this picture , degas did not yet have privileges to go backstage at the opéra . in the late 1870s , degas said , `` i have done [ painted ] so many of these dance examinations without having seen them that i am a little ashamed of it . '' joséphine gaujelin ( or gozelin ) is the dancer in the center of the picture . she waits for the starting note from the ballet master . the watering can ( to wet down the rosin on the floor ) , the top hat as music holder , and the empty violin case are props that degas would continue to use in his ballet pictures . degas would re-use the poses in this picture many times .
