Heraia     the heraia were footraces held in honor of the goddess hera in olympia , greece since early ancient times . the races were said to have been created by hippodameia in gratitude for her marriage to pelops . only virgins from elis , greece competed . they wore a short tunic that fell just above the knees . the right shoulder was bare to the breast . competitors ran only about 160 meters in the stadium of olympia . this was somewhat short of the stadium 's complete length . the judges were 16 eleian women . every four years , they wove hera 's sacred `` peplos '' ( dress ) , and took it to her temple . the judges had two special dances : the hippodameia and the physkoa . the latter was dedicated to a local nymph with whom dionysos fell in love . winners of the race received an olive branch wreath and a portion of the cow 's meat sacrificed to hera . they had the right to dedicate their statues to the temple of hera . the heraia was not held during the years of the men 's olympic games . about two years separated the events .
