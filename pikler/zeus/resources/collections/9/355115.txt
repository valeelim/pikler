Kottabos (Kleophrades Painter)    kottabos ( kleophrades painter ) kottabos player is an attic red-figure terracotta kylix said to have been painted by someone close to the kleophrades painter . it dates to the greek archaic period , about 500 bc . the kylix 's height with handles is 3 3/16 in . ( 8.1 cm ) and its width with handles is 10 1/16 in . ( 25.5 cm ) . the diameter of the bowl is 7 5/8 in . ( 19.3 cm ) . the kylix is in the collections of the metropolitan museum of art in new york city . the figure is a symposiast playing a party game called kottabos . he is about to fling the last of the wine in his cup at a lamp stand in an attempt to knock the lamp stand 's finial off . the winner was he who did so .
