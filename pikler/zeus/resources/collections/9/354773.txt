Brandenburg Gate (Potsdam)    brandenburg gate ( potsdam ) the brandenburg gate ( ) on the `` luisenplatz '' in potsdam , should not be confused with the gate of the same name on berlin 's `` pariser platz '' , it was built in 1770/71 by carl von gontard and georg christian unger by order of frederick ii of prussia . it stands at the western end of `` brandenburger straße '' , which runs in a straight line up to the church of st. peter and st. paul . previously , from 1733 , there was another , simpler gate on the same spot , which resembled a castle gateway . together with the city wall , a form of toll barrier , and the other gates it was intended to prevent desertion and smuggling . towards the end of the seven years ' war , frederick the great had the old gate demolished and built , in its stead , this new brandenburg gate , as a symbol of his victory . since the city wall was demolished around 1900 the brandenburg gate has been a free-standing structure . source . - paul sigel , silke dähmlow , frank seehausen und lucas elmenhorst , architekturführer potsdam , dietrich reimer verlag , berlin 2006 , isbn 3-496-01325-7 .
