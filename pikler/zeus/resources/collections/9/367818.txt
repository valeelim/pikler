Common crane     the common crane ( `` grus grus '' ) , also known as the eurasian crane , is a bird of the family gruidae , the cranes . it is a medium-sized species , and classified as of least concern . it is the only crane commonly found in europe apart from the demoiselle crane . in europe , the common crane breeds in boreal and taiga forest and mixed forests , from sea-level to . it also lives on treeless moors , on bogs , or on dwarf heather habitats , usually where small lakes or pools are also found . in sweden , breeders are usually found in small , swampy openings amongst pine forests while , in germany , marshy wetlands are used . breeding habitats in russia are similar , though they can even be found nesting in the steppe and in semi-desert , so long as water is near . mostly , common cranes are found breeding in wooded swamps , bogs and wetlands . they seem to need quiet , peaceful enivrons with little human interference . they live in low density , from 1 to 5 pairs per . during winter , the birds move to flooded areas , shallow sheltered bays , and swampy meadows . during the flightless moulting period there is a need for shallow waters or high reed cover for concealment . later , after the migration period , the birds winter regularly in open country , often on cultivated lands and sometimes also in savanna-like areas , for example on the iberian peninsula .
