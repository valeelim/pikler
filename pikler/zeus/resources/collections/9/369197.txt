Farid-ud-din Attar     abū hamīd bin abū bakr ibrāhīm better known by his pen-name farīd-ud-dīn attār ( i.e . 'farid-ud-din the perfumer ' ) , was a persian poet , sufi and muslim theoretician . little is known about his life and he was probably born in nishapur , persia , sometime in the 1140s . he died in 1220 ad . his most famous works are his long allegorical poem , `` mantiq at -tayr '' ( the conference of birds ) and his account of the lives of many sufi saints , the `` tazkirat el auliya '' .
