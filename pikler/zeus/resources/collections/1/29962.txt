Ming dynasty     the ming dynasty was the ruling dynasty of china for 276 years ( 1368–1644 ad ) after the collapse of the mongol-led yuan dynasty . china was then known as the . the ming was described as `` one of the greatest eras of orderly government and social stability in human history '' . it was the last imperial dynasty in china ruled by ethnic han chinese . the ming 's main capital of beijing fell in 1644 to a rebellion led by li zicheng , who established the shun dynasty . this was soon replaced by the manchu-led qing dynasty . regimes loyal to the ming throne – collectively called the southern ming – survived until 1683 .
