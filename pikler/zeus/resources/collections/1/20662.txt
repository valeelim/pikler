Composite number     a composite number ( or simply a `` composite '' ) is a natural number , that can be found by multiplying prime numbers . for example , the number `` 9 '' can be found by multiplying `` 3 '' by `` 3 '' , and the number `` 12 '' . you get it by multiplying `` 3 '' , `` 2 '' and `` 2 '' . all natural numbers ( greater than 1 ) can be put in one of the two classes below : - the number is prime . - the number is not prime . it can be found by multiplying together other primes . the same prime number can be used several times , as in the example with 12 above . this is known as the fundamental theorem of arithmetic . other pages . - perfect number
