Ulster Volunteer Force     the ulster volunteer force ( uvf ) is a loyalist paramilitary ( civilians trained as soldiers ) group based in northern ireland . it was formed in 1966. it takes its name from the uvf of world war i. the ulster volunteer force was started as a protestant/unionist militia ( citizen military force ) in 1912 to oppose the home rule campaign for a separate irish parliament . many uvf members formed the 36th ( ulster ) division that fought with the british army in world war i. the progressive unionist party is the political wing of the outlawed ulster volunteer force and red hand commando . although the pup is called a loyalist fringe party , it has more moderate unionist views than any of the traditional unionist parties . during the troubles in northern ireland ( 1969-1998 ) , the uvf carried out a number of atrocities : - in 1971 , they bombed mcgurk 's bar in belfast which was situated in a predominantly catholic area of the city . the blast killed 15 catholics and levelled the building .
