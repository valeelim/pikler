Diaphragm     the diaphragm is a muscle that is at the bottom of the ribcage of mammals . when breathing in , the diaphragm pulls down so that the size of the lungs increases , allowing air to enter the lungs . when breathing out , the diaphragm rests and goes up making a dome shape , decreasing the size of the lungs and pushing air out . this increases the surface area of the air so that there is more oxygen that the lungs can hold . other websites . - science aid : lungs
