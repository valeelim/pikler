Soap bubble     a soap bubble is a very thin film of soap water . their shape is a hollow , round ball , or sphere . they have a colorful surface . soap bubbles do not last very long . sometimes they break in the air . when they touch something else , they usually break . when people talk about soap bubbles they think about them being pretty but short-lived . so , they are a symbol for other things that are pretty but short-lived or unimportant . children enjoy playing with bubbles . sometimes , adults enjoy seeing art done with soap bubbles . they get their colors from iridescence . artists . - tom noddy , author of `` bubble magic '' , - keith michael johnson , - fan yang , - louis pearl , - reinhold leppert , - craig glenday , - john erck .
