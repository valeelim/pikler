Giant isopod     giant isopods are big crustaceans that live at the bottom of the ocean . they can grow up to 45 cm long and weigh up to 1.7 kg . currently about 9 different species are known . giant isopods usually eat dead whales , fish and squid . they may also be active scavengers , and hunt slow-moving prey , such as sea cucumbers , sponges , nematodes . perhaps they even catch live fish . they live in depths between 170m to about 2200m . over 80 % can be found between 365 m and 730 m of depth . they probably prefer a muddy or clay ocean floor environment , and lead solitary lives .
