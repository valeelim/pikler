Hibiscus syriacus     hibiscus syriacus is one of the common flowering shrubs found in gardens , a species of `` hibiscus '' . common names for the same plant include rose of sharon ( but it is not a rose ) , rose mallow , shrub-althaea , syrian hibiscus , syrian ketmia , and st joseph 's rod . the part of the name `` syriacus '' seems to say that the origin of this plant is from syria , but the exact origin is so far unknown . historically it was grown in ancient china and then it became a popular plant in korea . today the flowers are national symbols of korea . in japan , the flowers are often shown at tea ceremonies for decoration . there are many variations of flowers in gardens , because gardeners of the past were able to find different colors and shapes of flowers , and grow their seeds .
