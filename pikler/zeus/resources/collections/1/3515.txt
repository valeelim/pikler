Fertilizer     a fertilizer is a chemical that helps plants to grow . it is used to replace the mineral salts taken by plants or removed by rain . common fertilizers : - ammonia - manure ( animal waste ) which was the first fertilizer . - compost - urea the important things in fertilizer are : - nitrogen ( n ) - phosphorus ( p ) - potash ( k ) when fertilizers are offered for sale , the percentage of n , p , and k must be written on the bags or boxes , but for historcal reasons , p is shown as % p2o5 and k is shown as % k2o . e.g . :9-23-30 which means : 9 % n , 23 % p2o5 and 30 % k2o . in australia the pecent of elemental sulfur must also be shown . in the uk , the elemental composition ( in percentages ) may also be shown alongside the mandatory traditional system , provided the numbers are put inside square brackets . leafy plants need lots of n. flowering plants need lots of p and k. a soil test can tell how much n , p , and k is needed .
