Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth     the polish–lithuanian commonwealth ( or union , after 1791 the commonwealth of poland ) was a state of poland and lithuania ruled by a common monarch . the commonwealth was an extension of the polish-lithuanian union , a personal union between those two states that had existed from 1386. it was the largest and one of the most populous countries of 16th- and 17th-century europe . the polish-lithuanian commonwealth covered more lands than the present lands of poland and lithuania . the lands of the commonwealth also covered all the lands of present-day belarus ; a large part of ukraine and latvia ; and western part of present-day russia . the polish–lithuanian commonwealth had high levels of ethnic diversity and by religious tolerance . the amount of religious freedom varied over time . after several decades of prosperity , it entered a period of political , military and economic decline . it ended with the final partitions of poland in 1795. its growing weakness led to it being divided by its more powerful neighbors , austria , prussia and the russian empire .
