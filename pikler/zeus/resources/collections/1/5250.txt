Venus (mythology)    venus ( mythology ) in roman mythology , venus is the goddess of love and beauty . she was the roman version of the greek aphrodite and etruscan turan . her cult began in ardea and lavinium , latium . on august 18 , 293 bc , the first temple to her was built . august 18 was then a festival called the vinalia rustica . on april 1 , the veneralia was celebrated in honor of venus verticordia , the protector against vice . on april 23 , 215 bc , a temple was built on the capitol dedicated to venus erycina to commemorate the roman defeat at lake trasum . venus was also married to vulcan , who was the god of fire , volcanoes and blacksmiths . julius caesar introduced venus genetrix as a goddess of fatherhood and domesticity . related pages . - aphrodite - greek mythology version of venus .
