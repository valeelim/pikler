Crewkerne     crewkerne is a town in somerset , england where about 6,700 people live . it is in the south somerset district . the first time it is mentioned in history is 899 , when king alfred the great left the manor to his son ethelweard . the name `` crewkerne '' comes from the saxon words `` cruce '' ( cross ) and `` earne '' ( cottage ) , because of the early church there . there was also a mint where coins were made between 978 and 1035. in 1066 , the manor belonged to edith , mistress of harold godwinson . after the norman conquest , it belonged directly to william the conqueror . other websites . - archaeology of crewkerne ( .pdf file )
