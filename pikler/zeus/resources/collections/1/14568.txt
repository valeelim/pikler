Köbes     köbes is a word from the rhineland from local ripuarian and limburgish-bergisch languages . it has two meanings : 1. literally , it is a translation of the name `` jacob '' 1. informally , it usually means a specialized type of waiter . a köbes is the waiter who serves the kölsch beer in cologne or who serves the alt beer of düsseldorf and krefeld . one finds him only in brewery outlets and some specialized pubs . traditionally , a köbes is dressed in blue , with a big leather belt and leather purse . if a guest sits down , the köbes supplies beer , there is no order required . a köbes continually replaces every empty glass with a full one , usually without asking . one must ask for the bill , or cover the glass , if you do not want more beer . expect köbes to engage in short conversations or occasionally to tell , or make , jokes . everyone can be subject of such jokes , the köbes himself , his customers or other guests . if a köbes includes them in jokes they do not hesitate to joke back on him . books . - michael scuffil : `` drink doch ene mit ! der kölsche köbes '' , published by `` verlag emons '' in cologne , edition 1 , april 2007 , 47 pages , in five languages , hardbound , isbn 978-3-89705-505-6
