Kalua     kalua is a hawaiian cooking method . it means `` da pit . '' the hole . the oven is a hole in the ground called an imu . kalua is most often used to cook pig . kalua pig is a popular food at luaus . ancient hawaiians used rocks from rivers to cook pig . they heated the rocks in the imu and the pig cooked with steam . they covered the pig with banana leaves , ti leaves , old mats and then dirt to keep the heat inside . this produced a unique taste that can not be reproduced . people today kalua pig in the same way that ancient hawaiians did . kalua pig is still served at luaus . however , it is illegal to sell kalua pig if it has been cooked underground . hawaiians today still enjoy kalua pig that has been cooked in an imu at baby luaus and parties . some companies try to imitate the flavor using liquid smoke to make kalua pig , but if you have had the real thing braddah , you can tell the difference ! other websites . - hawaiian luau : no-imu kalua pig - emeril legasse 's recipe for kalua pig .
