Census of Marine Life     the census of marine life is a ten-year survey of life in the oceans , starting in 2000. its head is ron o'dor of dalhousie university in halifax , nova scotia , canada . it uses data from researchers all over the world . more than 70 nations are involved and over a billion us dollars will be spent on it . it is a major work of marine ecology . it is trying to do what the world wide fund for nature ( wwf ) and national geographic society did for terrestrial ecoregions - say exactly what is alive on the planet . in this case , in the seas . other websites . - census of marine life website
