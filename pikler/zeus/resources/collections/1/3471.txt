Denarius     the denarius was a small silver coin used by the roman empire and roman republic . the denarius weighed about 3 to 4.5 grams . it was the main coin of ancient rome . it became the most common coin produced for circulation but was slowly debased in weight and silver content . the coin was then sometimes made of copper and painted silver in color . during the empire the front side usually had a picture of the emperor on it . the denarius was introduced in 211 bc , and was last issued in 275 ad , by which time it was made of bronze .
