Caspian tiger     the caspian tiger ( `` panthera tigris virgata '' ) is an extinct sub species of the tiger . it was the most western sub species of the tiger , very close to europe . they became extinct in the 1970s . they were found in turkey , armenia , iraq , azerbaijan , iran , mongolia , and georgia . recent research by scientists looking at the tiger 's dna , have found that it is almost identical to the siberian tiger . they now believe that the two species of tiger may not have become separate until the 1900s . the caspian tiger was the second largest of the tiger species . it had a bigger , muscular body , with longer strong legs . it had large paws and more claws . it had small , short ears . the color was different from other tiger subspecies , with yellow to gold fur with light to dark brown stripes . the chest and stomach area are white with yellow stripes . in winter it grew longer fur . it was the furriest of all the tiger subspecies .
