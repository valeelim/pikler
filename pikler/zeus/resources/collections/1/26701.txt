Atomic mass unit     an atomic mass unit is a unit of measurement that is used to measure the mass of atoms . the atomic mass unit ( abbreviated : amu or u ) is roughly equal to the mass of 1 proton or 1 neutron . the unified atomic mass unit and the dalton are different names for the same thing . the dalton name is used more over time . the unit is named after john dalton , an 18th-century naturalist and teacher .
