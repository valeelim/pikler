Dam     a dam is a large wall or barrier that blocks or stops the flow of water , forming a reservoir or a lake . most dams have a section called a spillway or weir over which , or through which , water flows , either sometimes or always . dams generally serve the primary purpose of retaining water . the tallest dam in the world is the 300 meter high nurek dam in tajikistan . some of the first dams were built in mesopotamia up to 7,000 years ago . modern dams are built with arched walls of steel rod-reinforced concrete . the arched wall is a very strong shape which helps the wall to withstand the huge weight of the water . there are a number of reasons that people build dams . some countries build dams to control the flow of water in the downstream river systems , and prevent flooding . other countries build dams to generate electricity using hydroelectric turbine generators . other countries use dams to store water for use in irrigation ( for farming ) or drinking by people .
