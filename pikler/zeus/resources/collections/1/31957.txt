Gustav Holst     gustav holst ( 21 september 1874 - 25 may 1934 ) was a famous english composer . he was born in cheltenham , gloucestershire . he studied composition at the royal college of music in london . he also learned to play the trombone . he became director of music at st paul ’ s girls ’ school , hammersmith , ( london ) . some of his music was written for the pupils at this school : for example , the popular `` st paul ’ s suite '' ( 1912-1913 ) for string orchestra . his most famous work is `` the planets '' ( 1918 ) . this is a suite of seven movements for orchestra , each about a different planet ( the earth is not included , and pluto had not yet been discovered ) . the music does not really describe the planets , it describes the roman gods after which the planets are named . mars , for example , is the “ bringer of war ” . it has a very exciting rhythm with five beats in a bar . jupiter has a tune which has become famous as the hymn “ i vow to thee , my country ” . he died of heart failure in london . other websites . - www.gustavholst.info
