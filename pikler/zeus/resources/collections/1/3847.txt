Para rubber tree     a para rubber tree ( or simply , rubber tree ) is the tree which naturally produces rubber . it is native to tropical areas from south america , in the amazon ( brazil ) , but was spread by european farmers to the far east . para rubber trees belong to the euphorbiaceae family . in the wild they may reach heights of 100 to 125 ft ( 30-38 m ) with large cylindrical trunks with or without buttresses . crop trees reach a width of about 20 in . ( 50 cm ) , usually with a short bole , and with a sloped taper . most rubber plantations are in south asia and southeast asia . when trees reach 5-6 years old , they are harvested . their trunks are cut just deep enough to tap the vessels without harming the tree 's growth , and the sap is collected in small buckets . this process is known as rubber tapping . older trees produce more latex , but they stop producing it after 26-30 years .
