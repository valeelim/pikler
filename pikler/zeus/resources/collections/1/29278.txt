Guru Granth Sahib     the guru granth sahib , also called adi granth , is the holy book of the religion sikhism . it contains prayers , hymns and songs of sikh religion . sikhs believe the guru granth sahib to be a living guru , hence the guru granth sahib has its own place also commonly known as 'sach khand ' ( the pure heaven ) . the name of the book is made up of three words . the first word is guru , and a guru in sanskrit language means a teacher . granth is a word of hindi , and this means a book . the third word sahib is a word of urdu language and it means master . thus , the guru granth sahib means `` a master book by the teacher '' . the guru granth sahib , is a collection of hymns of the first five `` sikh gurus '' , the `` ninth guru '' , and various saints , or `` bhagats '' sikhs and others such as baba farid . the book is usually placed at the end of the temple , high up under a canopy .
