D-Day     d-day is a term used in military planning to mean the actual day a major operation or event is to begin . the days leading up to a d-day are called d-1 , d-2 , d-3 , etc . this allows scheduling a sequence of events before the start date is chosen . the days after a d-day are d+1 , d+2 , d+3 , and so on . several different events in military history were called d-day such as the invasion of normandy ( operation overlord ) . the most famous d-day was on the morning of june 6 , 1944 , when the largest naval attack in military history took place . it was in france during world war ii . this attack was code named `` operation overlord '' , led by american general dwight d. eisenhower . the allies ( britain , canada , and america in this case ) , breached the normandy coastline , via the german outpost , bunker , and fortification chain , the atlantic wall , which was a turning point in world war ii , though over 3,500 men were lost . less than a year later , the allies entered nazi germany , and germany surrendered to the allies .
