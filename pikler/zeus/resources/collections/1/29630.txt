Lemma (mathematics)    lemma ( mathematics ) in mathematics , a lemma is a `` mini-proof '' ; a small hypothesis which is proved and then provides a part of a more important theorem . a good small thing can lead to many big things . some powerful results in mathematics are known as lemmas , such as bézout 's lemma , dehn 's lemma , euclid 's lemma , farkas ' lemma , fatou 's lemma , gauss 's lemma , greendlinger 's lemma , itō 's lemma , jordan 's lemma , nakayama 's lemma , poincaré 's lemma , riesz 's lemma , schur 's lemma , schwarz 's lemma , urysohn 's lemma , yoneda 's lemma and zorn 's lemma . while these results originally seemed too simple to get independent interest , they have turned out to be important to the theories in which they occur .
