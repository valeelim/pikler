Soprano     a soprano is a female singer with a high voice . young boys who sing , and also girls , are usually called trebles . the word “ soprano ” can also refer to the top line of choir music . this would be sung by sopranos or trebles . soprano is also used as a name for high pitched instruments , such as a soprano saxophone . in opera there are different kinds of soprano voices : - a dramatic soprano will sing big , dramatic roles such as aida in verdi 's opera `` aida '' . - a coloratura soprano will have a light voice which can bounce up to very high notes ( the queen of the night in mozart ’ s `` magic flute '' goes up to top f ( 2 ½ octaves above middle c ) . - a soubrette is usually the maid or a young girl who flirts . - a lyric soprano role needs a beautiful smooth voice , e.g . mimi in puccini 's `` la boheme '' . - a heavy dramatic soprano is needed in many of wagner 's operas , e.g . isolde in `` tristan und isolde '' or brunnhilde in `` der ring des nibelungen '' . - in the 17th and 18th centuries many of the soprano roles were written for male sopranos . called a castrato , they were men who had their testicles removed before puberty so that their voices would remain high pitched .
