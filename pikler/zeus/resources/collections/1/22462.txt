Bonfire     a bonfire or balefire is a large controlled outdoor fire made from bales of straw or wood . the word is believed to come from `` bone fire '' . in the time of the celts , there were midsummer festivals where animal bones were burnt to ward off evil spirits . in great britain , bonfires are particularly associated with `` guy fawkes night '' . this night is also known as `` fireworks night '' or `` bonfire night '' . on this night , people celebrate that the gunpowder plot was discovered . this discovery took place on 5 november 1605. they do this celebration each year . ( it is called an `` annual celebration '' . ) in northern ireland , `` bonfires '' are associated with celebrations on the anniversary of the battle of the boyne . this battle took place on 12 july 1690. in japan , large fires called bon-bi are set to welcome the return of the spirits of the ancestors . though the two terms are not etymologically or historically related , they serve similar purposes and indicate the universal importance of large fires .
