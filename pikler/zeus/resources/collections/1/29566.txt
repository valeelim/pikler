Charon (mythology)    charon ( mythology ) in mythology , charon was the ferryman of hades . he was the son of erebos and nyx . charon took the newly dead people across the river acheron to the greek underworld , if they paid him three obolus ( a greek silver coin ) . because of this , people in ancient greece were always buried with a coin under their tongue and one on each eye to pay charon . those who could not pay him had to wander the banks of the acheron for one hundred years . the largest moon of the planet pluto is named charon after this mythological person .
