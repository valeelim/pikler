Cardinal number     cardinal numbers ( or cardinals ) are numbers that say `` how many '' of something there are , for example : one , two , three , four , five , six . they are sometimes called counting numbers . the cardinality of a set is the cardinal number that tells how many things are in the set . in mathematics , people also study infinite cardinal numbers . the first infinite cardinal number was named formula_1 ( pronounced aleph-zero or aleph-naught ) by georg cantor . formula_1 is the amount of numbers that are in the group 0 , 1 , 2 , 3 , ... keep going forever . cantor proved that there are many different infinite cardinal numbers that are bigger than formula_1 . a famous theorem of cantor is that the cardinality of the real numbers is larger than the cardinality of the natural numbers . other pages . - ordinal number
