Kookaburra     kookaburras are birds of the genus dacelo and are native to australia and new guinea . an adult kookaburra is generally 28–42cm from the head to the tip of the tail . taxonomy . - genus `` dacelo '' - laughing kookaburra ( `` dacelo novaeguineae '' ) – native to eatern mainland australia , introduced to tasmania , flinders island , kangaroo island and western australia . some were also introduced to new zealand between 1866 and 1880 ; but only those that were freed by sir george grey on kawau island are still there today . - blue-winged kookaburra ( `` dacelo leachii '' ) – native to northern australia and southern new guinea . - spangled kookaburra ( `` dacelo tyro '' ) – found only on the aru islands and trans fly savanna and grasslands of southern new guinea . - rufous-bellied kookaburra ( `` dacelo gaudichaud '' ) – widely distributed through forests of lowland new guinea and has also been seen on saibai island in queensland . kookaburras usually eat small animals : small mammals ( like mice ) , insects , small birds , lizards and snakes .
