Foolscap     foolscap is paper cut to the size of , sometimes . this was a traditional paper size used in europe and the british commonwealth , before the adoption of the international standard a4 paper , the most common standard size in the world . foolscap was so called because , in the 18th century , folio-sized paper had the watermark of a fool 's cap on it . basically , it is taller than a4 . in north america , foolscap is lined , legal-size paper . in some south american countries it is called `` oficio '' , because it is or was used for official documents . paper of the size is now very rarely used , being superseded by a4 ( 297 x 210 ) . however , some ring binders , lever arch files , suspended files , and other filing related things still use the size .
