Parvati     according to hinduism and hindu mythology , parvati is the wife of shiva . shiva is one of the main gods of the hindu religion . parvati is considered to be a mother goddess by many people . parvati is a word in the sanskrit language that means `` daughter of the mountain '' . hindu teachings call parvati the daughter of the himalayas mountains , where she is said to live . she has two sons , ganesha and kartikeya . when shown alone , parvati has four arms . in her arms she carries prayer beads , a mirror , a bell and citron . but when she sits by the side of her husband shiva , parvati has only two arms . in her form with two arms , parvati holds her right hand up in blessing and holds a lotus flower in her left hand . parvati 's conveyance ( vahana ) is a lion or tiger . this means that parvati rides a lion or a tiger . in hindu scriptures , parvati is mentioned many times . she is called by many other names . some of them are : - ambika , meaning the mother - gauri , meaning the fair one - shyama , meaning the dark one
