Vitamin C     vitamin c , also known as ascorbic acid , is a vitamin . it is found in fresh fruits , berries and green vegetables . it is one of the water soluble vitamins . vitamin c is important in wound healing . without enough vitamin c , a person can get a sickness called scurvy . lack of vitamin c was a serious health problem on long ocean trips where supplies of fresh fruit were quickly used up . many people died from scurvy on such trips . most animals make their own vitamin c. some mammals can not . those that can not include the main suborder of primates , the haplorrhini : tarsiers , monkeys and apes , including humans . others are bats , capybaras and guinea pigs . vitamin c was first discovered in 1928. in 1932 , it was proved to stop the sickness called scurvy . through history the need for people to eat fresh plant food to help them get through long sieges or long sea trips was known by some wise people but was often forgotten . the first attempt to prove this idea was by a ship 's doctor in the british royal navy called james lind , who at sea in may 1747 gave some crew members lemon juice as well as their normal ships food , while others continued on normal food alone .
