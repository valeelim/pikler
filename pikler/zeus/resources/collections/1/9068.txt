Vulture     vultures are large birds of prey that usually feed on carrion ( dead or dying animals and rocks ) . vultures use their large wings to soar in the air for many miles without having to flap . vultures are also called buzzards . `` vultures '' from the old world ( europe , asia , and africa ) are not related to the vultures of the new world ( north and south america ) . they look quite similar , though . both have heads and necks with no feathers . `` old world vultures '' are related to eagles and hawks and use sight to find their food . `` new world vultures '' are related to storks and use their sense of smell to find their food . vultures symbolize death in literature . king vultures use rocks to open eggs the pick up a rock with their beak and simply drop or throw the rock onto the egg and breaks it . new world vultures is a name which is used for a number of species in the americas . the best known of these are probably the andean condor and the black vulture .
