Glass rod     glass rods are sometimes used as stirrers in laboratory environments . glass rods are often used to spread liquids evenly onto something . an example would be to coat glass surfaces with liquids to look at them under a microscope . glass rods rubbed with silk or fur were used in early demonstrations of electrical energy . glass rods are commonly manufactured in diameters of 3 mm , 6 mm , and 10 mm . glass is used for these rods because it does not react with most laboratory chemicals .
