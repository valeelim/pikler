Gyeonggi-do     gyeonggi-do ( 경기도 ) is the province in which the most people live in south korea . over 12 million people live in it . its provincial capital is suwon . its name , gyeonggi , means 'surrounding the capital ' and it surrounds seoul , the capital of south korea . it is also next to incheon . gyeonggi-do has been important since king onjo of baekje ( one of the three kingdoms of ancient korea ) built wiryeseong and founded the government in it . after king taejo of goryeo made the capital in gaegyeong , ( now kaesong ) , the province became much more important , and since king hyeonjong 's period it has been called gyeonggi . gyeonggi-do 's population increased rapidly after the modernization of south korea because the capital , seoul , the economic and cultural center of south korea , is very close to it . now the population of gyeonggi-do is 12,649,894. gyeonggi province consists of 28 cities and 3 counties . gyeonggi province 's transport is well-developed because it is very close to seoul—south korea 's capital—and incheon , where korea 's biggest international airport is located . it is easy to access the capital and easy to go abroad by plane . much of gyeonggi province is served by the seoul metropolitan subway .
