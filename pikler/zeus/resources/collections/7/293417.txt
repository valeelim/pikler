Glycerol     glycerol ( or glycerin , glycerine ) is a simple organic molecule . it is a colorless , odorless , thick liquid that is widely used during the last step of preparing prescription drugs . glycerol has three hydrophilic ( water-loving ) hydroxyl ( oxygen-hydrogen ) groups that are responsible for its solubility in water and its hygroscopic nature ( which means it attracts water from the air ) . the glycerol backbone is central to all lipids known as triglycerides . it is used is in making nitroglycerin .
