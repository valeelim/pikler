Tinamou     the tinamous are a family of birds . there are 47 species of tinamou in central and south america . they are one of the most ancient living groups of bird , and are related to the ratites . generally ground dwelling , they are found in a range of habitats . of gondwanan origin , tinamous are related to the ratites . although the fossil record in south america is poor , the known tinamiform fossil record goes back 10 million years . together with the ratites , they make up palaeognathae ( `` old jaws '' ) , while all other living birds are members of neognathae ( `` new jaws '' ) . unlike the other palaeognaths , tinamous do have a keeled sternum . like the other palaeognaths , they also have a distinctive palate ( thin bony plate at top of mouth ) . recent phylogenetic studies place tinamids as the sister group of australasian/oceanian ratites . those are the ( cassowaries , emus , and kiwi ) . south american ratites ( rheas ) and african ratites ( ostriches ) are also related , but more distantly . tinamous themselves are monophyletic .
