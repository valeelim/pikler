Hypertrophy     hypertrophy ( from greek ὑπέρ `` excess '' + τροφή `` nourishment '' ) is the increase in the volume of an organ or tissue due to the enlargement of its component cells . it should be distinguished from `` hyperplasia '' , in which the cells remain approximately the same size but increase in number . although hypertrophy and hyperplasia are two distinct processes , they frequently occur together , such as in the case of the hormonally-induced proliferation and enlargement of the cells of the uterus during pregnancy . one of the two most common and visible forms of organ hypertrophy occurs in skeletal muscles in response to strength training or bodybuilding . other websites . - university of california muscle physiology home page : hypertrophy
