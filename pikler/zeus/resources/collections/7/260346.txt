Golden, British Columbia    golden , british columbia golden is a city in the canadian province of british columbia and is nicknamed `` kicking horse country '' . golden was first known as the cache and kicking horse flats . frederick w aylmer heard about a development west of banff , alberta being called `` silver city '' , thought to call this community golden city . `` city '' was dropped when the cpr arrived in 1885. the current mayor of golden is christina benty . the mayor in 2009 , aman virk died suddenly of a surpise heart attack while vacationing in india . the population is 3,811 from the 2006 census . golden is home to the longest freestanding timber frame bridge in canada called the `` kicking horse pedestrian bridge '' . nhl player doug barrault was born in golden . other websites . - official city website
