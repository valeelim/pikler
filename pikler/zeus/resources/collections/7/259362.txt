Chemical stability     chemical stability refers to the resistance of a chemical to change in a chemical reaction . some chemicals , like gold , are very stable and resistant to change ; but sodium is unstable and corrodes rapidly in the presence of air . when an atom has an extra electron or a missing electron , it is unstable . when that atom loses or gains the electron , it becomes stable . stable atoms have lower energy than unstable ones . related pages . - chemical compound - chemical equation - chemistry
