Andromeda (constellation)    andromeda ( constellation ) andromeda is a constellation in the northern sky . it is named after the princess andromeda in greek mythology . it is sometimes called `` the chained lady '' or `` the chained woman '' in english . it has also been called `` persea '' ( `` perseus 's wife '' ) or `` cepheis '' ( `` cepheus 's daughter '' ) . the astronomer named ptolemy listed andromeda when he made a list of 48 constellations . it is also one of the 88 constellations made by the international astronomical union . the andromeda galaxy is inside andromeda , so it has the same name . it is the closest spiral galaxy to earth . the brightest star in andromeda is called alpha andromedae , which is a binary star .
