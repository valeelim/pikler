Silver chloride     silver chloride is a chemical compound . its chemical formula is agcl . it contains silver in its +1 oxidation state . it also contains chloride ions . it is a white crystalline solid . it does not dissolve in water . when it is exposed to light , it turns back into silver and chlorine . it dissolves in several solutions , such as ammonia . it can be made by reacting a common chloride such as sodium chloride with silver nitrate . it is used in photographic film . it darkens when exposed to light , which makes a picture . it is also used in electrochemistry . it is used as a disinfectant in bandages . it is also used to make stained glass . it can be used when someone is poisoned with mercury . it can irritate skin . it can also stain the skin . it is a weak oxidizing agent . related pages . - silver bromide - silver iodide
