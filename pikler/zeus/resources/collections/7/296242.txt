April 25–28, 2011 tornado outbreak    april 25–28 , 2011 tornado outbreak in late april 2011 , there was a major tornado outbreak , the worst in u.s history . 3 ef5 tornadoes dropped down along with 13 ef4 tornadoes and 23 ef3 tornadoes . that was a major tornado outbreak . on the evening of april 25 , dangerous supercells started forming across arkansas , northeast texas , and southeast oklahoma . the supercells then merged into a massive squall line of severe thunderstorms . the severe thunderstorms produced tornadoes . then , heavy rain formed northwest of the line of severe thunderstorms and tornadoes . an ef2 tornado struck vilonia , arkansas , and an ef3 tornado struck hot springs village , arkansas . later , the severe thunderstorms and tornadoes moved into tennessee and mississippi . on april 26 , the severe thunderstorms and tornadoes moved back into texas , oklahoma , and arkansas . on the evening of april 26 , dangerous supercells formed over texas . those supercells produced dangerous tornadoes , like ef2 or ef3 . then , those supecells moved into oklahoma and arkansas . the squall line of severe thunderstorms moved back into tennessee and mississippi .
