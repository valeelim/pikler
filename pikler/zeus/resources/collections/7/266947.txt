Synod of Hippo     the synod of hippo was the synod of 393 that was held in hippo regius in northern africa during the early christian church . other synods were held in 394 , 397 , 401 and 426. some were attended by st. augustine . the synod of 393 is best known for two things . first , for the first time a council of bishops listed and agreed a canon of sacred scripture that is the same as the modern orthodox and roman catholic canon . the canon was later agreed at the council of carthage . references . - new advent catholic encyclopedia - african synods - schaff - the code of canons of the african church a.d. 419
