Stagecoach     a stagecoach is a type of horse drawn coach for passengers and goods . they were one of the first forms of organized public transport and originated in the 1600s in great britain . before railways were invented in the first part of the nineteenth century they were the fastest way to travel long distances . stage coaches were operated across europe and north america . the coach would travel between stops called `` stages '' at which passengers could obtain food and drink and the horses that pulled the coach would be changed for fresh ones . regularly changing horses meant the coach could travel much faster than other vehicles . they were mostly of light weight construction which also allowed them to go faster . their speed meant they were used to carry mail and often called a mail coach . the name is sometimes shortened to simply a `` stage '' .
