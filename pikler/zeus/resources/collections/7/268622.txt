Dysgraphia     dysgraphia or agraphia is the inability to write texts , even though the person has the necessary motoric skills . people with dysgraphia can move their hand enough , and are also capable of understanding how to write . dysgraphias occur because of damage done to the brain . they often occur with other illnesses , such as speech problems . many people who suffered a stroke may have lost the ability to move parts of their body . there are different forms of dysgraphia : - lexical agraphia : words that are hard to tell apart can not be written down properly - phonological agraphia : words can be written down correctly , but they can not be pronounced - semantic agraphia : words with a certain meaning can not be pronounced - apractical agraphia : the motor skills can no longer be used for writing
