Appin, New South Wales    appin , new south wales appin is a town in new south wales , australia in wollondilly shire . it is situated about 16 kilometres south of campbelltown and 35 kilometres north west of wollongong . in 2006 there were 1,423 people living in appin . the fifth village settled in australia , it is now on the fringe of the sydney urban area . the town was named by governor lachlan macquarie in 1811 after a small village on the coast of scotland where his wife was born . one of the first settlers was john hume , whose son hamilton hume became a famous australian explorer . hamilton hume explored the local area and was given a grant of 300 acres of land at appin as a reward . when he made his famous journey to victoria with william hovell in 1824 , they started the journey from appin . appin is the birthplace of john fuller ( 1830-1865 ) , the australian bushranger also known as dan morgan . cataract gorge is about 10km south east of appin . it is now has a dam built in 1906 which is the oldest dam in the sydney water supply system . on 17 april , 1816 the british army killed many aboriginal men , women and children , of the dharawal nation at the gorge . they had been fighting the european settlers who were moving into the area .
