Dysaethesia Aethiopica     dysaethesia aethiopica was a term to describe a mental illness which was believed to be why afro-american slaves were lazy . american physician samuel a. cartwright described the condition in 1851. today , dysaethesia aethiopica is considered an example of pseudoscience . many people think it is an example of scientific racism . cartwright wrote that the illness caused people to break and mishandle everything they touched . they walked about as if they were in a daze , or half asleep . they often argued with people in charge and caused disturbances . they did not feel pain , and did not react when physically punished . it affected people who did not have white ( or european ) people to look after them .
