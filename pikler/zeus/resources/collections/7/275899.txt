Antimony pentachloride     antimony pentachloride , also known as antimony ( v ) chloride , is a chemical compound . its chemical formula is sbcl . it has antimony and chloride ions in it . the antimony is in its +5 oxidation state . antimony pentachloride is a colorless or yellow liquid . it releases toxic and odorous ( bad odor ) fumes in air . it melts at 2.8°c and boils at 140°c . it reacts with water to make antimony pentoxide and hydrochloric acid . it dissolves in hydrochloric acid . it dissolves in chloroform . it absorbs water to form a solid hydrate . it is a strong oxidizing agent . it is made by reacting liquid antimony trichloride with chlorine . it is used in a test for caesium . it is also used in the making of chemicals and dyes . related pages . - antimony pentafluoride - antimony trifluoride - bismuth pentafluoride
