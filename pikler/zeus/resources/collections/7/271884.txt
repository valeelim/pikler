Post-transition metal     the post-transition metals , also known as the poor metals , is a group of metals on the periodic table . it is to the right of the transition metals . the group 12 elements are sometimes included . sometimes germanium , antimony , and polonium are included , although they are normally considered metalloids . they normally have higher electronegativities than the transition metals . their melting points are lower , too . they are generally softer than other metals . they include lead , tin and other softer metals . examples . - aluminium - gallium - lead - tin - thallium - indium - bismuth
