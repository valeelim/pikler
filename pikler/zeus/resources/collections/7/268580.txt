Leavening agent     a leavening agent is a substance used to make a baked good rise . most people like baked goods that are risen , so leavening agents are popular . yeast is a common leavening agent . it is a fungus that converts sugar to ethanol and carbon dioxide . the carbon dioxide makes the bread rise . this is slower and makes the bread more soft . most of the ethanol evaporates when the bread is baked . the yeast is also killed when the bread is baked . yeast takes longer time to rise than chemical leavening agents . it also can be killed by heat or salt . yeast is normally sold in small packages . these contain yeast fungi . `` main articles : baking powder and baking soda '' there are several chemical leavening agents . for acidic foods , baking soda ( sodium bicarbonate ) can be used . it reacts with the acid to make carbon dioxide gas , which makes the bread rise . for non-acidic foods , baking powder is used . baking powder is a mix of a powdered acid and sodium bicarbonate . they react when dissolved in water to make carbon dioxide gas . baking powder is more common .
