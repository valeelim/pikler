Telephone numbering plan     a telephone numbering plan is a system used in telecommunications to give telephone numbers to customers and to route telephone calls in a telephone network . a closed numbering plan imposes a fixed length to numbers , such as in north america ( 10-digit ) . an open numbering plan may have telephone numbers that vary in length . a dial plan establishes the type of telephone number in a given area . this includes country codes , access codes , area codes and all combinations of digits dialed . for instance , the north american public switched telephone network ( pstn ) uses a phone numbers with a 3-digit area code and a 7-digit telephone number .
