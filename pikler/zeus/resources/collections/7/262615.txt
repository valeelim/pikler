Supercalifragilisticexpialidocious     supercalifragilisticexpialidocious ( ) is an english word , with 34 letters , that was in the song with the same title in the disney musical movie `` mary poppins '' . the song was written by the sherman brothers , and sung by julie andrews and dick van dyke . it also appears in the stage show version of mary poppins . the writers of the `` oxford english dictionary '' think that the word `` supercalifragilisticexpialidocious '' was first used in the 1940s . the word is a compound word , and said by richard lederer in his book `` crazy english '' to be made up of these words : super- `` above '' , cali- `` beauty '' , fragilistic- `` delicate '' , expiali- `` to atone '' , and -docious `` educable '' , with all of these parts combined meaning `` atoning for educability through delicate beauty . '' according to the movie , in which the word became widely used , the word is `` something to say when you have nothing to say '' . however , a lot of people think the word means `` extraordinarily good '' or `` wonderful '' because all mentions of the word in the movie can be thought of as good .
