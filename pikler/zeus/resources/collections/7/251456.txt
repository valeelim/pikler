Weaver ant     weaver ants ( also known as tailor ants or green ants ; genus `` oecophylla '' ) are eusocial insects of the formicidae family . they make their nests from living leaves , still attached to the tree . instead of needles and thread , they use a rare type of silk , made in the mouths of their own grubs . the grubs are passed to and from between the leaves , to sew them together . tailor ants may be found in the rainforest of asia . colonies of weaver ants can be very large , containing more than half a million workers . the colonies can be made up of more than a hundred nests across several trees . other websites . - weaver ants - national geographic , may 2011 - antweb - ants of the world - tree of life - oecophylla
