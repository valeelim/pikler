Denial Bay, South Australia    denial bay , south australia denial bay is the name for both a small bay on the coast of south australia , and a small fishing town built at the bay . the bay was named by explorer matthew flinders on 7 february 1802. flinders had hoped that bay was bigger and would have provided better access into the inland of south australia . one of the first european settlers was a sheep farmer , william mckenzie , who built a house in the area in 1889. the town was known as mac 's town , and in 1910 the government officially named it mckenzie . in 1940 it was named denial bay .
