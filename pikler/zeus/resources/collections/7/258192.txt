Chloralkali process     the chloralkali process is a process used to create sodium hydroxide , chlorine , and hydrogen from sodium chloride and water . it is normally conducted in a tank separated by a membrane , although there are other methods , such as using mercury . if potassium chloride or calcium chloride is used instead , potassium hydroxide and calcium hydroxide are produced . when the membrane is not in place , hypochlorites are formed . if it is heated , chlorates are formed . the process is fueled by electrolysis . it can be done in the laboratory . other pages . - chlorine - sodium hydroxide - electrolysis
