Geomorphology     geomorphology is the scientific study of landforms and the processes that shape them . more broadly , it studies the evolution of the topography of a planet . geomorphologists seek to understand why landscapes look the way they do , to understand landform history and dynamics , and to predict future changes . they use a combination of field observation , physical experiment , and numerical modeling . geomorphology is part of geography , geology , geodesy , engineering geology and archaeology . this range of interests leads to a wide variety of research styles and interests in the field .
