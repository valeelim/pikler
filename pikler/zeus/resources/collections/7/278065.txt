Mérida, Venezuela    mérida , venezuela santiago de los caballeros de mérida , venezuela , is the capital of the municipality of libertador and the state of mérida , and is one of the principal cities of the venezuelan andes . it was founded in 1558 , forming part of nueva granada , but later became part of the captaincy general of venezuela , and played an active role in the war of independence . mérida has more than 200,000 inhabitants and a metropolitan area containing some 350,000 people . it is the main center for education and tourism in western venezuela , the home of the prestigious university of the andes , and the location of the highest and second longest aerial tramway in the world . mérida is at an altitude of approximately 1,600 meters ( 5,249 ft ) . the city sits on a plain in the valley of the chama river , which runs from one end to the other . the backdrop of mérida 's skyline is the country 's highest summit , pico bolívar .
