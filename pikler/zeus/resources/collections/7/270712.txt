Delisle scale     the delisle scale is a scale to measure temperature . it was introduced by french astronomer joseph-nicolas delisle in 1732. delisle used a thermometer that worked with mercury . the unit is abbreviated `` de '' or `` d '' . 0°de is equal to the boiling point of water under normal pressure . delisle then measured the volume change of the mercury . at the start , the delisle scale had 2400 degrees . josias weitbrecht recalibrated the theromometer in 1738 , so that the melting point of ice was taken into account . ice melts at 150°de . the deslile scale counts downwards , from the boiling point to the melting point . it was widely used in russia in the 19th century .
