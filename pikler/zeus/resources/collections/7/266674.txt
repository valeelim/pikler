Strong acid     in chemistry , a strong acid is an acid which ionizes ( splits ) completely in a solution of water . it always loses a proton ( a h ) when put in water . a weak acid does not always lose a proton , though ; it can lose its proton , but not all the time they also have a very low ph , usually between 1 and 3. many strong acids have a negative pka value , which means they are very strong . the six common strong acids are : ( acronym : cbspin ) 1. hydrochloric acid hcl 1. hydrobromic acid hbr 1. sulfuric acid h₂so₄ 1. perchloric acid hclo 1. hydroiodic acid hi 1. nitric acid hno almost strong acids include : 1. chloric acid hclo
