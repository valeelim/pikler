Golpar     heracleum persicum , known as golpar or persian hogweed , is a flowering plant . it grows wild in mountains . golpar seeds are used as a spice in persian cooking . these seedpods are slightly bitter . they are usually sold in powder and are often sold as `` angelica seeds . '' the powder is sprinkled over beans and potatoes . golpar is also used in soups and stews . it is often sprinkled over pomegranate seeds . golpar is also mixed with vinegar into which lettuce leaves are dipped before eating .
