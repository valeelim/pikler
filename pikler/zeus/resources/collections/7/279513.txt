Yaws     yaws is a tropical infection of the skin , bones and joints . it is caused by the spirochaete bacterium `` treponema pallidum pertenue '' . the disease begins with a round , hard swelling of the skin , two to five centimeters in diameter . the center may break open and form an ulcer . this usually heals after three to six months . after several weeks , or even years later , the joints and bones may become painful , fatigue may develop , and new skin sores may appear . the skin of the palms of the hands and the soles of the feet may become thick and break open . the bones ( especially those of the nose ) may change shape . after five years or more large areas of skin can die . this leaves large scars . yaws is spread by touching the fluid from the sores of a person with the disease . the disease is most common among children , who spread it by playing together . other similar diseases are caused by different strains of the same bacterium , such as syphilis ( `` treponema pallidum pallidum '' ) . once the lesions have appeared doctors are able to tell it is yaws . polymerase chain reaction ( pcr ) is the best method of diagnosis .
