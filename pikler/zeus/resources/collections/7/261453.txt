Canon (church)    canon ( church ) a canon in a church is an official . he may or may not be a priest . the chapter of a cathedral is an official group made up of the dean and chapter of canons , who meet in a chapterhouse . they run the cathedral , especially the business affairs of the cathedral . they are the body which has legal responsibility for the cathedral , and for electing the ( arch ) bishop . originally , a canon was a cleric who lived in the precinct of the cathedral . the word meant a priest who was under ( ruled by ) a bishop . in the anglican church , some canons are laypersons ( not ordained ) . the title is also given to some senior or retired priests as an honorary title .
