Nichiren     nichiren ( 16 february 1222 - 13 october 1282 ) was a buddhism priest who lived in japan . he is often called `` nichiren '' , `` nichi '' means `` sun '' and `` ren '' means `` lotus '' . the type of buddhism he founded is known as nichiren buddhism . nichiren 's actions and writings are the basis of the modern buddhist organization soka gakkai international ( sgi ) . according to sgi 's interpretation of buddhism , it is through faith , practice and study that people can tap into their buddha nature , become truly happy , and build a peaceful society . faith refers to the belief in the concept known as `` the mystic law of the universe . '' practice means to chant every day the phrase `` nam-myo-ho-renge-kyo '' and sections from the lotus sutra while sitting in front of the gohonzon , a sacred scroll that hangs in the butsudan ( an altar ) . practice also entails telling other people about nichiren buddhism . study means reading the writings of nichren and discussing the readings with other people .
