The Andrews Sisters     the andrews sisters were an american singing group in the 1930s , the 1940s and the 1950s . they were from minneapolis , minnesota . their names were laverne ( july 6 , 1911 – may 8 , 1967 ) , maxene ( january 3 , 1916 – october 21 , 1995 ) and patty andrews ( february 16 , 1918 – january 30 , 2013 ) . they broke up in 1967 after the death of laverne , but their music is still played over certain radio stations in the united states . the andrews sisters were notable for their song `` the boogie woogie bugle boy '' . that song was covered and recorded by multiple singers over the years . the andrews sisters were born in minnesota , to a greek father and norwegian mother . they grew up in minneapolis . maxene was married to jack kapp from 1941 until his death in 1949. they had two adopted children . andrews died on october 21 , 1995 in cape cod , massachusetts , from a heart attack , aged 79. laverne was married to louis a. roger from 1948 until her death in 1967. andrews died on may 8 , 1967 in brentwood , california from cancer , aged 56 .
