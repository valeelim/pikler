Fasciola hepatica     fasciola hepatica is the common liver fluke . it is a parasitic trematode ( fluke or flatworm ) in the phylum platyhelminthes . it infects the livers of various mammals , including humans . the disease caused by the fluke is called `` fascioliasis '' . it has been classified as a neglected tropical disease . `` f. hepatica '' is distributed worldwide , and has been known as an important parasite of sheep and cattle for hundreds of years . it causes great economic losses in sheep and cattle . because of its size and economic importance , it has been the subject of many scientific investigations and may be the best-known of any trematode species . to complete its life cycle , `` f. hepatica '' needs a freshwater snail as an intermediate host , where the parasite can reproduce asexually .
