Fauvism     les fauves ( french for `` the wild beasts '' ) was the name of a modern art movement in the early 20th century , led by the painters henri matisse and andré derain . the fauves were a group of french painters who were influenced by the bright colours used by the post-impressionists vincent van gogh and paul gauguin . the bright colourful style of painting that they used began around 1900 and continued beyond 1910 , but the group was only together for three years , 1905–1907 . in that time they had three exhibitions together . their influence has continued until the present day , and was adopted or influenced many artists who are not usually thought of as fauvists . aristarkh lentulov is one good example . related pages . - impressionism - post-impressionists - pointillism - cubism - expressionism
