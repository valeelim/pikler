King in Prussia     king in prussia was the title used by the rulers of prussia from 1702 to 1772. the rulers , who were also princes and margraves of brandenberg wanted to be kings , but knew that the holy roman emperor would not allow them the title . in return for support in the war of the spanish succession emperor leopold i allowed frederick to call himself king in prussia . the duchy of prussia was outside the empire , and he was only regarded as king outside the empire . in 1772 frederick ii annexed most of poland and decided to call himself king of prussia . the holy roman emperor was not as strong as they had been , and in 1806 the empire ceased to exist .
