Grumman G-21 Goose     the grumman g-21 goose is an amphibious aircraft , which means it can land and take off on water . it was designed as an eight-seat `` commuter '' plane for businessmen in the long island area . the goose was grumman â€™ s first monoplane to fly . it was also grumman 's first twin-engined aircraft as well as their first aircraft to provide commercial service . during world war ii , the goose was used to transport the us military ( including the coast guard ) . it served with many other air forces . during battles , the goose took on an increasing number of combat and training roles . it was also used after the war . in 1936 , some rich residents of long island , such as e. roland harriman , asked grumman to build them an aircraft . they would use this aircraft to fly to new york city . the g-21 was designed after this . it was an all-metal , high-winged monoplane powered by two pratt & whitney r-985 wasp jr. nine-cylinder , air-cooled radial engines mounted on the leading edge of high-set wings . the deep fuselage served also as a hull and had hand-cranked retractable landing gear . first flight of the prototype took place on may 29 , 1937 .
