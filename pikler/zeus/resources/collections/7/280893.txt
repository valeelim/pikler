Genetic counselling     genetic counselling , or `` counseling '' , is the process by which clients are advised about genetic disorders . genetic disorders , by definition , can be passed on in heredity . example : many descendents of queen victoria carried one or both alleles for haemophilia . it was a hereditary disease in european royal families . clients with a disorder may be interested in the probability of developing or transmitting a genetic disorder to their children . they may ask what the options are to prevent , avoid or ameliorate it . facilities and rules vary in different countries . in the united states the national society of genetic counselors defines genetic counselling as : - interpretation of family and medical histories to assess the chance of disease occurrence or recurrence . - education about inheritance , testing , management , prevention , resources and research . - counselling to promote informed choices and adaptation to the risk or condition . a genetic counsellor is an expert with a master of science degree in genetic counseling . in the united states they are certified by the american board of genetic counseling . most enter the field from biology , genetics , nursing , psychology , public health and social work . genetic counsellors need to be expert educators , skilled in translating ideas into terms that are easy to understand .
