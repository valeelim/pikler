Residency (medicine)    residency ( medicine ) residency is a level of medical training . a resident physician or resident ( called senior house officer or registrar in the united kingdom , depending on the level of training ) is a person who has gotten a medical degree and who practices medicine under supervision of other physicians , usually in a hospital . in the united states , doctors must complete residency before they can practice medicine without supervision . residency programs train doctors to work in a specific specialty , or field within medicine . for example , there are different residency programs for medicine , surgery , and psychiatry . in the us , the length of a residency program is different based on specialty . residency in specialties like family medicine and psychiatry is usually three years long , but a residency program in neurosurgery ( brain surgery ) may be 7 years long .
