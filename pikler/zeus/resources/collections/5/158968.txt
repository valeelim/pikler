2006 Central Pacific cyclone     the 2006 central pacific cyclone , also called 91c.invest or storm 91c , was a strange weather event in 2006. forming on october 30 from a mid-latitude cyclone in the north pacific mid-latitudes , it moved over waters warmer-than-normal . it got some characteristics more normal for subtropical or even tropical cyclones . however , as it came closer to western north america , it fell apart . moisture from its remains then caused a lot of rainfall in british columbia . a cut-off extratropical cyclone over the central north pacific moved into a place with sea surface temperatures as high as 2 Â°c above normal for two days . by october 31 the system had gotton convection , a warmer-than-normal core , and an eye-like characteristic . during this time it had moved east , then northeast , and then northwest . on november 1 , the system had estimated winds of 100 km/h ( 60 mph ) and its most developed convection . after that , it slowly weakened , moved counter-clockwise , and headed east towards the west coast of north america . on november 2 , wind shear started affecting the storm , and all convection was gone by the next day , when it was located roughly 520 mi ( 840 km ) off the coast of oregon .
