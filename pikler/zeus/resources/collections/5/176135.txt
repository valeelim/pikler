Bougainvillea     bougainvillea is a genus of flowering plants . bougainvilleas first came from south america . they are climbing plants grown for decoration in tropical countries , and in the southern united states . farther north , the plants are grown in greenhouses . the bougainvillea is named for the french navigator louis-autoine de bougainville . bougainvillea , a tropical vine known for the beauty of the large , colorful bracts ( modified leaves ) which surround its flowers . the flowers are small and plain . the bracts may be magenta , purple , yellow , or crimson , and are one inch ( 2.5 cm ) or more long . one common type of bougainvillea has a climbing stem that sometimes reaches a length of 100 feet ( 30 m ) .
