Lake Eyre     lake eyre ( pronounced `` air '' ) is the lowest point in australia , at about below sea level . when it fills with water , it is the largest lake in australia , being long and wide . it is also in the driest part of australia , getting only about of rain each year . it is in the north of south australia , about north of adelaide . the lake was named after the explorer , edward eyre , who was the first europen to see it in 1840. it is the centre of the lake eyre national park . lake eyre is in the driest desert region of central australia . it is a large endorheic lake system , which means that the water flows in , but does not flow out . as the water evaporates , the lake dries up leaving a dry salt lake . the lake does not usually completely dry up , there is often water in small areas on the lake surface . the rate that the water dries up is per year , that is about 20 times more than the rainfall in the area . during the wet season the rivers from queensland flow towards the lake through the channel country . these rivers drain about one sixth of australia , an area the size of spain . how much water reaches the lake depends on how rain falls in queensland . since 1885 the lake has filled in 1886/1887 , 1889/1890 , 1916/1917 , 1950 , 1955 and 1974-1976. in 1974 lake eyre was deep . local rain filled lake eyre to in 1984 and 1989. very heavy rain in january 2007 took about six weeks to reach the lake but put only a small amount of water into it . in february 2009 , water from flooding rains in queensland were flowing along the creeks to the lake covering about 40 km per day . the lake fills to about every three years , to , every ten years , and fills up about four times every 100 years .
