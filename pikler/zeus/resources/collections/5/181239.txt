Susan B. Anthony     susan brownell anthony ( february 15 , 1820 - march 13 , 1906 ) was an american women 's rights leader . at 17 , she became a schoolteacher , but soon became involved in activism regarding slavery and drinking . by the 1850s , she was working closely with elizabeth cady stanton for women 's rights , including the vote . she edited a newspaper called `` the revolution '' for several years . in 1872 , she was arrested for trying to vote . for many years , she was the leader of the national women 's suffrage association , which wanted women to have the right to vote . the 19th amendment ( change to the u.s. constitution ) was passed in 1920 , 14 years after anthony 's death on march 13 , 1906. anthony later became the first woman on a u.s. coin . this coin was a dollar coin .
