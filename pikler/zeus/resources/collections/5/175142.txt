Ocean current     an ocean current is a continuous movement of ocean water from one place to another . ocean currents are created by wind , water temperature , salt content , and the gravity of the moon . the current 's direction and speed depend on the shoreline and the ocean floor . they can flow for thousands of miles and are found in all the major oceans of the world . one major example of an ocean current is the gulf stream in the atlantic ocean . ocean currents can be found on the water surface and deeper down . - currents on the surface often depend on wind . they travel clockwise in the northern hemisphere . they travel counterclockwise in the southern hemisphere . they are found up to below the surface of the ocean . - deeper currents depend on water pressure , temperature , and salt content . major ocean currents of the world . arctic ocean . - east greenland current - norwegian current - beaufort gyre ( water or ice flow ) - transpolar drift ( water or ice flow ) atlantic ocean . - agulhas current - angola current
