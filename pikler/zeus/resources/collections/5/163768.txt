Baobab     baobab is the common name of a genus of trees ( adansonia ) . there are nine species . six species live in the drier parts of madagascar , two in mainland africa , and one in australia . the baobab is the national tree of madagascar . other common names include 'boab ' , 'boaboa ' , 'bottle tree ' , 'the tree of life ' , 'upside-down tree ' , and 'monkey bread tree ' . the trees reach heights of 5 to 30 metres ( 16 to 98 ft ) and trunk diameters of 7 to 11 metres ( 23 to 36 ft ) . its trunk can hold up to 120,000 litres of water . for most of the year , the tree is leafless , and looks very much like it has its roots sticking up in the air . baobabs are one of the largest and most important trees in all of where they grow ! you can use them for shelter , wood , and more . the trees are long-lived , but just how long is disputed . the owners of sunland farm in limpopo , south africa have built a pub called `` the big baobab pub '' inside the hollow trunk of the high tree . the tree is 47 m ( 155 ft ) in circumference , and is said to have been carbon dated at over 6,000 years old .
