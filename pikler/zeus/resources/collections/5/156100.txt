Muncie, Indiana    muncie , indiana muncie is a city of indiana in the united states . it is known as `` middletown usa '' because many sociological studies were conducted there because it was said to be the typical american city . the population of muncie in 2011 is 67,430 people . muncie is in delaware county , about 60 miles northeast of indianapolis . the county was named after the delaware indians , a native american people , who had moved west from ohio in the 1770 's . they settled along the banks of the white river . one of the settlements was named muncietown . in 1818 the delaware signed a treaty with the us government and moved out of the area to go further west . sociologists , robert s. lynd and his wife , helen merrell lynd , studied muncie in the 1920s . they wrote two books about life in muncie called `` middletown : a study in modern american culture '' , and `` middletown in transition '' . they wanted to study the typical small american city , which of course can not exist . they wanted to see how life in a city had changed from 1890 to 1925. they were going to study a city they way an anthropologist would study a primitive tribe . they decided to look at six parts of living in a city :
