Guinea (British coin)    guinea ( british coin ) a guinea is a coin that was minted in the kingdom of england between 1663 and 1813. it is not used any more . although guinea coins had not been made for a long time , prices often continued to be shown in guineas until british prices became decimal in 1971. a guinea was worth £1,1s0d ( one pound and one shilling ) . this is the same as £1.05p in modern money . because a guinea was close to a pound , putting prices in guineas made the price seem less . a price of 39 guineas might look like `` about £39 '' . in fact it is nearly £41 . prices in guineas were traditionally used for luxury goods before decimalisation . saville row tailors , bespoke boot makers , fine jewelers , and expensive motorcars were always priced in guineas .
