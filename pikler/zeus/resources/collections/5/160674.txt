British Rail Class D3/7     british railways class d3/7 were diesel shunters built from may 1939 through to july 1942 by the london , midland and scottish railway at their derby works . like the earlier british rail class d3/6 , they had the english electric 6k diesel engine of . but a big change was having a `` jackshaft '' type of connection between engine and wheels ( like a diesel mechanical shunter ) . these were given lms numbers 7080–7119 . ten were loaned to the war department in 1941 , and sold to the wd the following year . all ten survived the war ; six were then sold to egyptian railways and four to italian state railways ( ferrovie dello stato—fs ) .
