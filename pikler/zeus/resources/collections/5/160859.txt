InterCity (British Rail)    intercity ( british rail ) intercity ( or , in the earliest days , the hyphenated inter-city ) was introduced by british rail in 1966 as a brand-name for its long-haul express passenger services ( see british rail brand names for a full history ) . after the privatisation of british rail , intercity trains were divided up into several franchises . the caledonian sleeper are transferred to scotrail , now first scotrail .
