Circe     in greek mythology , circe ( ; greek κίρκη `` kírkē '' `` falcon '' ) is a minor goddess of magic . sometimes , she is described as a nymph , witch , enchantress or sorceress . in the `` odyssey '' homer describes her as 'the loveliest of all immortals , ' living on the island of aeaea , famous for her part in the adventures of odysseus . in most descriptions , circe was the daughter of helios , the god of the sun , and perse , an oceanid , and the sister of aeetes , the keeper of the golden fleece , perses , and pasiphaë , the wife of king minos and mother of the minotaur . other accounts make her the daughter of hecate . circe transformed her enemies , or those who offended her , into animals through the use of magical potions . she was known for her knowledge of drugs and herbs . that circe also purified the argonauts for the death of apsyrtus , as told in `` argonautica '' , may be an early tradition .
