Alexandre Guilmant     alexandre guilmant ( born boulogne-sur-mer , 12 march 1837 ; died meudon 29 march 1911 ) was a french organist and composer . alexandre guilmant was born in boulogne-sur-mer . his father was an organist and organ builder , and gave young alexandre lessons . otherwise alexandre was largely self-taught . he did study with belgian teacher jacques-nicolas lemmens , but by that time he was already a very good organist . from the 1860s he travelled widely in europe , playing organs and becoming famous . later he also visited the united states . in 1871 he became organist of the trinité church in paris . he kept this job until 1901. guilmant became well known in france as a teacher . together with two other musicians , charles bordes and vincent d ’ indy , he started the schola cantorum , a private college where students could study music . he taught the organ there . earlier he had succeeded charles-marie widor as organ teacher at the paris conservatoire . his conservatoire pupils included joseph bonnet , nadia boulanger and marcel dupré .
