Gerrymandering     gerrymandering is when a political group tries to change a voting district to create a result that helps them or hurts the group who is against them . it is named after elbridge gerry ( 1744-1814 ) . gerrymandering works by wasting votes . it puts more votes of winners into the district they will win so the losers win in another district . if we have group a and group b both trying to win in a district . group a has 40 votes and group b has 50. in another district group a knows group b will win for sure . group a changes the voting district so that 11 votes are moved to the other district . now they will win 40 votes to 39 .
