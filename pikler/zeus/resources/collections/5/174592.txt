Convex set     in euclidean space , a region is a convex set if the following is true . for any two points inside the region , a straight line segment can be drawn . if every point on that segment is inside the region , then the region is convex . the point is that a convex curve forms the boundary of a convex set . so , any shape which is concave , or has a hollow , can not be a convex set . related pages . - convex function
