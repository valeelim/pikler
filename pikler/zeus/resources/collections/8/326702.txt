19th G7 summit     the 19th g7 summit was a meeting in 1993 for the leaders of canada , the european commission , france , germany , italy , japan , the united kingdom and the united states . the international group of leaders were together in japan from july 7-9 , 1993. the meetings were held at the state guesthouse ( akasaka palace ) in tokyo . the tokyo summit of the group of seven ( g7 ) was the 19th meeting in a series which began in 1976. two previous g7 summit was hosted by japan at tokyo ( 1979 and 1986 ) . the g8 and the summit are part of a consultation process . the g8 is not an international organization . it is an informal group . the participants were the `` core members '' of the group : this was the first international summit for prime minister campbell and for president clinton . the g7 leaders agreed to work on common strategies for global economic growth ; and they discussed - strengthening the un - regional problems - strengthening efforts related to nuclear nonproliferation the group of leaders called for an `` international agreement '' to `` protect forests . '' there is little evidence of follow-up .
