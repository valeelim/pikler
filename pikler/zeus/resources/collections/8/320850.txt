Tamba Province     , also known as tanba , was an old province of japan in the area of hyōgo prefecture on the island of honshū . along with tango province , it was sometimes called . the province had borders with harima , ōmi , settsu , tajima , tango , wakasa , and yamashiro provinces . the ancient capital city of the province was in the area of modern kameoka . in 713 ( `` wadō 6 , 3rd month '' ) , tamba was separated from tango . in the meiji period , the provinces of japan were converted into prefectures . the maps of japan and tamba province were reformed in the 1870s . `` izumo daijinjū '' was the chief shinto shrine ( `` ichinomiya '' ) of tamba . related pages . - provinces of japan - prefectures of japan - list of regions of japan - list of islands of japan other websites . - murdoch 's map of provinces , 1903
