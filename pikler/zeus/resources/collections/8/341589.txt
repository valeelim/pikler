Mutunus Tutunus     in ancient roman religion , mutunus tutunus was a phallic marriage deity . he is often associated with the greek god priapus . his shrine was located on the velian hill , supposedly since the founding of rome , until the 1st century bc . during marriage rites , roman brides are supposed to have straddled the phallus of mutunus to prepare themselves for intercourse , according to church fathers who interpreted this act as an obscene loss of virginity . other pages . - priapus - greek mythology version of mutunus tutunus
