Omagh     omagh ( or ; – ) is the county town of county tyrone in northern ireland , situated where the rivers drumragh and camowen meet to form the strule . the town , which is the largest in the county , had a population of 19,910 at the 2001 census . omagh also contains the headquarters of omagh district council and the western education and library board . the town is twinned with east kilbride ( scotland ) and l'haÿ-les-roses ( france ) . in 1768 , omagh replaced dungannon as the county town of county tyrone . omagh acquired railway links to derry in 1852 , enniskillen in 1853 and belfast in 1861. the military barracks were built in 1881. the ulster transport authority closed the omagh-enniskillen railway line in 1957 and the portadown-derry main line in 1965 , leaving the town with no rail service . the town 's large military barracks , st lucia barracks , closed on 1 august 2007. related pages . - omagh bombing
