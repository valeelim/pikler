Ganbaru     , also romanized as gambaru , is a widely-used japanese cultural concept . it roughly means to slog on or stick to it through tough times . `` ganbaru '' implies a kind of hard work which is defined by japanese culture . the word `` ganbaru '' is often translated to mean `` doing one ’ s best '' . in practice , it means doing more than one 's best . the word emphasizes working with continued effort despite difficulties , failure , or distraction . the term means `` toughing it out '' . `` ganbaru '' means to commit fully to a task and to bring that task to an end . `` ganbaru '' means an active process of doing something . it contrasts with a passive process . the term `` ganbaru '' developed from the related concept of `` gaman '' . after the 1995 kobe earthquake , the slogan `` gambaro kobe '' was used to encourage the people of the kobe region . after the 2011 tōhoku earthquake and tsunami , `` ganbaru '' was one of the most commonly heard expressions . related pages . - gaman ( term ) - shikata ga nai
