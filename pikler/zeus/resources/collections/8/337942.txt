Bar-headed Goose     the bar-headed goose ( `` anser indicus '' ) is a type of goose that lives in wetland areas of central asia . it has a light grey body , broad wings , and a white face and neck . its name comes from the two stripes ( or `` bars '' ) of dark feathers that wrap around the back of its head . like other geese , its feet are webbed . they can get up to 2.5 feet long and weigh 6,5 pounds . they eat mainly grass , wheat , barley and rice .
