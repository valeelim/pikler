Electric ray     the electric rays are a group of rays , flattened cartilaginous fish with enlarged pectoral fins . they are the order torpediniformes . they can produce an electric discharge , ranging from as little as 8 volts up to 220 volts depending on species . there are 69 species in four subfamilies . these rays have battery-like electric organs on both sides of their heads , which can make a powerful electric shock . they use this shock to stun their prey and for their own self defense . electric rays are found from shallow coastal waters down to at least depth . they are sluggish and slow moving , propelling themselves along with their tails , rather than using their disc-shaped bodies , as other rays do . they feed on invertebrates and small fish . they lie in wait for prey below the sand or other substrate , using their electricity to stun and capture it . the electric rays are regarded as monophyletic , though in detail the group has been classified in several ways . this is one way the order can be classified .
