Ubangi River     the ubangi river , also spelled oubangui , is a river of central africa . it is the largest right-bank tributary of the congo river . it begins where the mbomou and uele rivers come together . it flows west , then bends to the southwest and passes through bangui . then the river flows south to the congo at liranga . the ubangi 's length is about . the ubangi 's drainage basin is about . the basin includes parts of the democratic republic of the congo , central african republic , and republic of congo . together with the congo river , it important for transportation for river boats between bangui and brazzaville . some people want the water from the ubangi to go to the chari river . the chari river empties into lake chad . they say this will help with fishing and make agriculture better .
