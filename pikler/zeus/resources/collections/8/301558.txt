Teasel     teasel is a plant in the genus dipsacus . there are about 15 different species of teasel . these flowering plants are tall and leafy ( herbaceous ) . they are biennial plants and are native to ( come from ) europe , asia , and northern africa . they can grow to be tall . these plants have a prickly stem and leaves . its flowers are purple , dark pink , or lavender and grow directly on the end of the stem . after flowering , the head of the plant dries out and holds small seeds . these seeds are important food in the winter for birds , especially the european goldfinch . teasels are grown in gardens and nature reserves to attract these goldfinches . in the united states , teasel is an invasive species . it is not native to the continent and takes over the land of native plant species . some of the most common species of teasel are : - `` dipsacus ferox '' - spiny teasel - `` dipsacus fullonum '' - wild teasel , common teasel , fuller 's teasel - `` dipsacus japonica '' - japanese teasel , chinese teasel - `` dipsacus laciniatus '' - cut-leaved teasel
