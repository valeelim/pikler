Cullman County, Alabama    cullman county , alabama cullman county is a county of the u.s. state of alabama . its name is in honor of colonel john g. cullmann . as of 2010 , 80,406 people lived there . its county seat is the town of the same name , cullman , alabama . the historic cherokee and choctaw lived here at the time of european encounter . the echota cherokee are a recognized tribe still here . cullman county was formed by german american immigrants from cincinnati , ohio , in 1877. according to the 2000 census , the county has a total area of .
