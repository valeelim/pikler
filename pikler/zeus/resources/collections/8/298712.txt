The Alchemist Discovering Phosphorus     the alchemist discovering phosphorus is a painting by joseph wright of derby which was finished in 1771 , then redone 1795. the full title of the painting is `` the alchymist , in search of the philosopher ’ s stone , discovers phosphorus , and prays for the successful conclusion of his operation , as was the custom of the ancient chymical astrologers '' . it is currently on show at the derby museum and art gallery . the picture shows an alchemist trying to produce the rare philosopher ’ s stone , which could turn metal into gold , but instead , to his surprise , he finds phosphorus . the picture has provoked many different understandings . the picture moved with wright to italy in 1773-1775 , came back to england , was redone in 1795 , but was only sold four years after his death . a story was often printed in popular chemical books in wright ’ s lifetime , and was widely known .
