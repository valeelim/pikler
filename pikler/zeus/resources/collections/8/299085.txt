Janko keyboard     the jankó keyboard is a musical keyboard layout for a piano designed by paul von jankó in 1882. it is a 6-plus-6-keyboard with six rows . so there is the same fingering in all scales and the thumb has its own rows for a better moving on the keyboard . for an 88 note ( full size ) keyboard , there would be 264 keys in total , with each note playable by 3 keys in vertical alignment . in the picture , the white keys have been coloured to show how the keys are interconnected . now the jankó keyboard is used for an electronic keyboard , the chromatone 312. other websites . - the lippens keyboard - the chromatone 312 - daskin uniform keyboard
