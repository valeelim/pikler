Shiretoko National Park     the word `` shiretoko '' is comes from an ainu word `` '' . it means `` end of the earth '' . shiretoko national park was established in 1964. in 2005 , unesco named the area a world heritage site . unesco also suggested that japan should work with russia to make an international `` world heritage peace park '' including the kuril islands . both japan and russia claim these islands as part of their countries . shiretoko is hard to get to . visitors must go to most places on foot or by boat . there is a hot springs waterfall called . `` '' means `` water of the gods '' in ainu . the park forests are temperate and subalpine . there are several different types of trees . the most common trees here are sakhalin fir , erman 's birch , and mongolian oak . the park has japan 's largest mammal species of ezo brown bears .
