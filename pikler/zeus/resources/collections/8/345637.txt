Ektara     ektara ( literally `` one-string '' , also called iktar , ektar , yaktaro gopichand ) is a one-string instrument . it is most often used in traditional music from bangladesh , india , egypt , and pakistan . the ektara was a regular string instrument of wandering bards and minstrels from india . it is plucked with one finger . the ektara usually has a stretched single string . an animal skin is over a head made of dried pumpkin/gourd , wood or coconut . the neck is a pole or split bamboo cane . pressing the two halves of the neck together loosens the string . this lowers its pants . the sound can be changed many ways by slight changes in pressure . the various sizes of ektara are soprano , tenor , and bass . the bass ektara , sometimes called a `` dotara '' often has two strings .
