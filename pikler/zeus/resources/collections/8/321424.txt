Bizen Province     the province had borders with mimasaka , harima , and bitchū provinces . the ancient capital city of the province was near okayama . in 713 ( `` wadō era 6 , 3rd month '' ) , the land of bizen '' -no kuni '' was separated from mimasaka province ( 美作国 ) . in the meiji period , the provinces of japan were converted into prefectures . the maps of japan and bizen province were reformed in the 1870s . `` kitbitsuhiko jinja '' was the chief shinto shrine ( `` ichinomiya '' ) of bizen . related pages . - provinces of japan - prefectures of japan - list of regions of japan - list of islands of japan other websites . - murdoch 's map of provinces , 1903
