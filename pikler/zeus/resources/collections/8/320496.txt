Tenchō     events of the `` tenchō '' era . - 824 ( `` tenchō 1 '' ) : this summer was entirely dry ; and prayers for rain were offered by the buddhist priest kūkai ( also known as kōbō-daishi ) . those prayers seemed to be answered when it did rain . - 824 ( `` tenchō 1 , 7th month '' ) : the former-emperor heizei died at age 51 . - 825 ( `` tenchō 2 , 11th month '' ) : the former-emperor saga celebrated his 40th birthday . - 826 ( `` tenchō 3 , 11th month '' ) : kūkai advises the emperor to build a pagoda near to-ji in kyoto . this era is part of `` kōnin-jōgan '' , which is a historical period from 810 to 877. the importance of buddhism and the arts was notable during this time . related pages . - heian period other websites . - national diet library , `` the japanese calendar '' -- historical overview plus illustrative images from library 's collection
