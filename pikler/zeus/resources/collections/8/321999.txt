Shinano Province     the ancient capital city of the province was near matsumoto . the boundaries of mutsu were formally established during the reigns of empress genshō and empress kōken . in 713 , the road between mino province and shinano province was widened . there were increasing numbers of travelers on this road . in the meiji period , the provinces of japan were converted into prefectures . maps of japan and shinano province weres reformed in the 1870s . the world war ii–era japanese aircraft carrier `` shinano '' was named after this old province . `` suwa taisha '' was the chief shinto shrine ( `` ichinomiya '' ) of shinano . related pages . - provinces of japan - prefectures of japan - list of regions of japan - list of islands of japan other websites . - murdoch 's map of provinces , 1903 - hokusai , `` a view of mount fuji across lake suwa , '' circa 1831
