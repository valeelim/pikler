Indigo bunting     the indigo bunting , `` passerina cyanea '' , is a small seed-eating bird . it is migratory . its range is from southern canada to northern florida during the breeding season , and from southern florida to northern south america during the winter . it often migrates by night , using the stars to navigate . its habitat is farmland , brush areas , and open woodland . the indigo bunting is closely related to the lazuli bunting . the indigo bunting is a small bird , with a length of 11.5–13 cm ( 4.5–5 in ) . the male is a vibrant blue in the summer and a brown color during the winter months , while the female is brown year-round . the male displays brightly colored plumage during the breeding season to attract a mate . nest-building and incubation are done solely by the female . the diet of the indigo bunting is insects during the summer months and seeds during the winter months .
