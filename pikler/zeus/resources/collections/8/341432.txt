Turbatrix aceti     turbatrix aceti ( also called vinegar eels ) is a species of nematode . they feed on the acidic bacteria from vinegar and other things , like fermented apples . they range from 1mm to 10mm long or larger . these nematodes go through a six-stage life cycle . the stages are : an egg , four larval stages , and reaching adulthood . they live about 10 months . the nematode 's reproductive system is sexual . female vinegar eels have ovaries and produce eggs . their reproductive system is in the form of a tube . the females also have a short sac-like seminal receptacle that stores sperm . a fertilized egg hatches in the uterus before leaving the womb . vinegar eels give birth to as many as 45 babies every 8-10 days . a male vinegar eels ’ reproductive system is smaller than the female ’ s . they have testis and a vas deferens . the testes open into a wide sperm duct and then into a muscular duct which can push out the sperm . the duct empties into a cloaca . nematodes , such as the vinegar eels , have no circulatory system . through their body ’ s wall , gas and excretion waste are diffused . oxygen from the outside environment is diffused into the body , and carbon dioxide is diffused out of the body . vinegar eels have to live in liquids that have enough oxygen so that it can diffuse into their bodies .
