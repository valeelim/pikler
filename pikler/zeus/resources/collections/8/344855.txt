Flag of Bahrain     the national flag of bahrain is a white band on the left , with a red area on the right split by five triangles . red is the traditional colour for flags of the arab states of the persian gulf . the five points are for the five pillars of islam . it is very similar to the flag of qatar . the oldest known flags of bahrain were plain red . in 1820 , bahrain made a treaty with the united kingdom . a white stripe was added to show the truce . in 1932 , a serrated edge was added to make the flag of bahrain different from its neighbours . the flag first had twenty-eight white points . this was changed to eight in 1972. in 2002 the number was changed to five . this was so that each of the points could stand for one of the five pillars of islam .
