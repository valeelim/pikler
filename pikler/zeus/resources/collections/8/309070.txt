Aestivation     aestivation , also spelled estivation in the usa , is a state of animal dormancy . it is somewhat similar to hibernation . the animals are inactive and have a lowered metabolic rate . the state is entered in response to high temperatures and dry conditions . lungfish have been doing this since the devonian period . it takes place during times of heat and dryness , the hot dry season , which is often but not necessarily the summer months . a wide range of animals aestivate , including the nile crocodile and many snails and lady beetles . invertebrate and vertebrate animals are known to enter this state to avoid damage from high temperatures and the risk of drying out . both land- and water-living animals can undergo aestivation . related pages . - torpor - dormancy - hibernation
