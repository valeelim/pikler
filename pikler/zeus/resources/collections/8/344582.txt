Rose Hill, New South Wales    rose hill , new south wales rose hill , new south wales was the name of an australian colonial settlement . rose hill was established on the parramatta river on november 2 , 1788. it was developed by governor arthur phillip and the first fleet . after sydney cove , this was the second settlement in australia . the fortified camp was named rose hill in honour of george rose , who was a british politician and member of parliament . rose had helped influence the process which led to phillip 's appointment as leader of the colony . the first crop of grain in australia was grown at rose hill . the area became part of the town of parramatta . related pages . - john caesar
