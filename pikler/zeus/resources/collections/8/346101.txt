State Duma     the state duma ( ) ; the lower house of the parliament and is a part of the legislative branch of the government of russia . the state duma was first introduced in 1906 and was russia 's first elected parliament . in 1922 it was replaced with the soviet parliament , and was re-established in 1993. it makes laws , elects the prime minister , supervises the work of the government , and has the power to remove the president from office and to call new elections . the duma has de jure authority to make and change any laws without being overturned . it can pass any law by a simple majority . there are 450 members in the duma .
