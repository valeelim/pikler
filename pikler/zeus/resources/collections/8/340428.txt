Djoudj National Bird Sanctuary     the djoudj national bird sanctuary ( ) is a world heritage site in senegal . it is on the senegal river . it has different wetland areas that are used by many migrating birds . some birds use the wetland after they cross the sahara desert . there are almost 400 kinds of birds . some of the most common are pelicans and flamingos . many different kinds of animals also live in the park . references . - wcmc natural site data sheet - official unesco website entry - ministère de l ’ environnement , de la protection de la nature , des bassins de rétention et des lacs artificiels : parcs et réserves , 13 october 2005 .
