Peter Warburton     peter egerton warburton ( 1813—1889 ) was an english naval and military officer who became famous as an explorer of australia . he served as the commissioner of police in south australia . he was employed by the south australian government to replace benjamin herschel babbage , because they felt babbage was moving to slowly . warburton led several trips into the desert between 1856 and 1874. he discovered a gap through the ring of salt lakes north of adelaide in south australia , which had blocked northward exploration . he discovered the artesian springs which he named coward springs . he also explored the davenport range which he named after sir samuel davenport , a south australian politician . in 1872 he became the first person to cross australia from the centre , at alice springs to the coast of western australia . he had to cross the great sandy desert , a trip which left him blind in one eye . he was so ill he completed the trip tied to his camel . they were forced to eat most of their camels . he said they only reason the group survived was through the skills of charley , their indigenous australian guide , charley . warburton was made a cmg and given £1000 as a reward in 1875 .
