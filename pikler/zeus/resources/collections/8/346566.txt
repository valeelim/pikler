William H. Seward     william henry seward ( may 16 , 1801–october 10 , 1872 ) was an american politician . he was the 12th governor of new york , and later a united states senator . he was also the united states secretary of state for two presidents , abraham lincoln and andrew johnson . he was very much against slavery . he was an important person in the republican party when it was starting . many people thought he would be nominated by the republican party to run for president of the united states in 1860 , but that did not happen . on the night that president abraham lincoln was killed , someone also tried to kill seward . for the rest of his life , seward had scars on his face from the attack . later , the man who attacked him was caught and put to death . when seward was secretary of state , he arranged for the united states to buy alaska from russia . people made fun of him for doing that . they called it `` seward 's folly '' . when asked what he thought was the most important thing he did as secretary of state , seward said `` the purchase of alaska—but it will take the people a generation to find it out '' .
