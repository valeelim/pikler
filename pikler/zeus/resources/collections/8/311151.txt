Transitivity (grammar)    transitivity ( grammar ) transitivity in grammar is about whether a verb has an object or not . a transitive verb has an object ; an intransitive verb does not . examples : - transitive : - `` we really enjoyed the trip '' . - `` she read the book '' . - `` what did you throw ? '' - intransitive : - `` she hurried home '' . - `` she visits often '' . - `` she felt sick '' a transitive verb is an action verb . it expresses something doable ( something possible to do ) . the direct object is something or someone who is the receiver of the action ( verb ) . in the first two examples above , the 'trip ' and the 'book ' are the direct objects . 'enjoyed ' and 'read ' are the transitive verbs .
