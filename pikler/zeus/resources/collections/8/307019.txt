Fabergé egg     a fabergé egg is one of the jewelled eggs made by peter carl fabergé and his company between 1885 and 1917. the most famous are those made for the russian tsars alexander iii and nicholas ii . they were easter gifts for their wives and mothers , and are called the 'imperial ' fabergé eggs . the house of fabergé made about 50 eggs , of which 43 have survived .
