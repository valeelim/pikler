Durham Cathedral     the cathedral church of christ , blessed mary the virgin and st cuthbert of durham ( usually known as durham cathedral ) is a cathedral in the city of durham , england , the seat of the anglican bishop of durham . the bishopric dates from 995 , with the present cathedral being founded in ad 1093. the cathedral is known as one of the finest examples of norman architecture . it is a unesco world heritage site . the present cathedral replaces the 10th century `` white church '' built as part of a monastic foundation to house the shrine of saint cuthbert of lindisfarne . the treasures of durham cathedral include relics of st cuthbert , the head of st oswald of northumbria and the remains of the venerable bede . durham cathedral occupies a strategic position on a promontory high above the river wear . the cathedral is a major tourist attraction , the central tower of 217 feet ( 66 m ) giving views of durham and the surrounding area . there are many famous people who are interred ( buried ) in the cathedral and in the surrounding grounds including bishop alfred tucker .
