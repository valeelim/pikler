Wren     the wrens are passerine birds in the mainly new world family troglodytidae . there are about 80 species in about 20 genera . originally , the word 'wren ' was ( and still is ) used for the species found in europe , western asia and north africa . the word came into english after a long history in the germanic languages . the bird 's scientific name is `` troglodytes troglodytes '' . when european biologists got to the americas , they soon realised that the family of wrens has many species , and is mainly an american family of birds . most wrens are about 10 centimetres ( 4 inches ) long , brown ( both sexes ) , and has a short bill that curves slightly down . its wings are round and it has a short tail . its song is a gushing burst of sweet music , loud and emphatic . it has an enormous voice for its size , ten times louder , weight for weight , than a cockerel . its song may sometimes be confused for the dunnock , which has warble that is shorter and weaker . the wren 's song also has trill sounds , which the dunnock does not . the male has a long and complex song , with a series of tinkling trills one after the other for seconds on end .
