St Dennis, Cornwall    st dennis , cornwall st dennis is a parish and village in cornwall , united kingdom . the village is on the b3279 road between newquay and st austell . st dennis had a population of 2,696 in the 2001 census . the village is famous for its church built on the site of an iron age hillfort . the area is full of tips and clay pits of china clay mining but st dennis village itself is designated an 'island settlement ' which prevents encroachment by the china clay industry . much of the parish is up to 500 feet above sea level , and the countryside is moorland with small fields enclosed by 'cornish hedges ' . the church is on top of a hill overlooking the village and has views to the sea at newquay . it stands on the site of an iron age fort . the tower is the oldest part of the church , and the newer church has been repaired after being destroyed by fire in 1985. the village also has a strong history of methodism and once had three methodist chapels , two of these are still in use .
