Katsuō-ji     , also known as `` katsuo-dera '' , is a japanese buddhist temple in minō , which is in the mountains of northern osaka prefecture . katsuō-ji is one in a grouping of buddhist temples which are pilgrimage sites in the kansai region . the temple site was first named miroku-ji in 765. the name `` katsuō-ji '' was given by the emperor seiwa . in 1184 , the temple was destroyed by fire , but it was re-built . daruma dolls are often returned to the temple and left somewhere on the grounds . other websites . - katsuō-ji website
