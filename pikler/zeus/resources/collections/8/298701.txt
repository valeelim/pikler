St Blazey     st blazey is a small town in cornwall , united kingdom , in the civil parish of st blaise . the name st blaise is also used by the town council . the population in the 2001 census was 6632. st blazey is east of st austell . once an important engineering centre for the local mine and railway industries , the parish is now dominated by the eden project . historically copper and tin were mined in and around the parish , whilst more recently china clay has been the principal commodity mined . the port of par harbour , which is in the parish , was built to make transporting these minerals easier . the cornwall minerals railway , which had a depot and station in the town , was also used to move them . the eden project is within the civil parish , and about from the centre of the town . the large number of visitors this attracts has led to the development of tourism in the town .
