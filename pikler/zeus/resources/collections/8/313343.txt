Baku Metro     baku metro ( ) , is an electrified metro railway system which is a part of the public transportation system of baku , the capital of azerbaijan . it was opened on 6 november 1967. at that time , azerbaijan was part of the soviet union until the end of the soviet union in 1991. the baku metro has the features found in most other ex-soviet metro systems . these include very deep metro stations , and detailed decorations that blend traditional azerbaijani national designs with soviet ideology . the metro system has three lines and has 36.7 kilometers of double railway tracks with 25 stations , all of which is underground . it is run by the `` bakÄ± metropoliteni '' . in 2015 , it carried 222.0 million passengers , which means an average ridership of approximately 608,200 passengers each day . at present , there are several projects , only two of which are under construction . in 2011 , the chief executive of the baku metro , taghi ahmadov , announced plans to construct 70 new stations by 2040. these will serve the new bus complex as well as heydar aliyev international airport .
