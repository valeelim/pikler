Ajkaceratops     ajkaceratops ( pronounced `` oi-ka-se-ra-tops '' ) is a genus of ceratopsian dinosaur described in 2010. it lived in the cretaceous period in what is now europe , in what was back then the tethyan archipelago . it is a ceratopsian , and is beaked . `` ceratops '' means `` horned face '' in greek . the generic name , `` ajkaceratops '' , honors ajka , a town in hungary where the fossils where found . the ajkaceratops has a skull that looks a lot like a protoceratops skull , except that it has a hump near the beak . the body is slender and long , compared to the skull . the tail is very short for its size .
