Charles Doolittle Walcott     charles doolittle walcott ( march 31 , 1850 – february 9 , 1927 ) was an american geologist and invertebrate paleontologist . he became known for his discovery in 1909 of well-preserved fossils in the burgess shale of british columbia , canada . walcott became director of the u.s. geological survey 1894–1907 , and secretary of the smithsonian institution , 1907–1927 . he discovered a number of important fossil sites . at the walcott quarry , the lowest ( earliest ) of the burgess shale deposits , walcott collected 65,000 specimens between 1910 and 1924. the site dates to the middle cambrian , and is in the canadian rockies . the walcott-rust quarry is near russia , new york . this site has produced some of the best preserved trilobites ever found , including enrolled specimens with soft body parts .
