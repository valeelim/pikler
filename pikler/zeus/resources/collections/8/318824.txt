Tōhoku region     the is one of japan 's traditional regions . the japanese conventions of geography and history divide the nation into eight regions , including the tōhoku region . these have been used since 1905 as basic units for description and comparison and as cultural markers . the regions of japan are a fusion of historical divisions and modern administrative needs . the significance of the region in japan is geographical , cultural and administrative . in the late 7th century , the `` tōsandō '' region were identified as one of the eight largest administrative areas of the imperial system ( `` ritsuryo seido '' ) . `` gokishichidō '' is an ancient system of names for parts of the country , including `` tōsandō '' . `` tōhoku '' covers the northern area of the island of honshū in roughly the same area as the traditional `` tōsandō '' . in the meiji period , the modern regional system was made by imperial decree . japan was divided into regions ( `` chihō '' ) , including the tōhoku region . a regional council ( `` chihō gyōsei kyōgisai '' ) was headed by the governor of the most powerful prefecture in the regional grouping . the council also included regional chiefs of central government ministries .
