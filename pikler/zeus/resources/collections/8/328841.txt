Japanese clock     the japanese clock is a device made to tell the time according to japanese tradition . these clocks were introduced to japan via jesuit missionaries in the 16th century . in 1612 , the clockmaker of philip ii of spain created a working timepiece which became a gift for tokugawa ieyasu . one of the major features of the japanese clock is that it has a mechanism for measuring unequal temporal hours . usual clock tells hours at same intervals , but in the japanese traditional timekeeping practice , a day had six daytime units from sunrise to sunset and six night units from sunset to sunrise . then the japanese clock was designed to adapt to this practice .
