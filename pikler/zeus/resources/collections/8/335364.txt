Provinces of South Korea     provinces are the first-level division within south korea . governors for these provinces are elected every four years . a province ( , ) is the highest-ranked administrative divisions in south korea . provinces have equal status to the korean special cities . a special autonomous province ( , ) is a province with more autonomy over its economy and more powers are given to the provincial government . jeju is the only special autonomous province . note : daegu is excluded because it is the capital but is not legally part of the province .
