Gallipoli     the gallipoli peninsula ( ; ; ) is in turkish thrace , the european part of turkey . to the west there is the aegean sea and to the east there are the dardanelles straits . after an earthquake in 1354 , the greek city of gallipoli was abandoned , then occupied by turks from anatolia . this made it the first ottoman position in europe . the peninsula was part of the byzantine empire . the ottoman empire slowly conquered it from 13 century to the 15 century . by the 19th century , thirty thousand people lived in the city : greeks , turks , armenians and jews . it was a big military camp for the british and french forces during the crimean war ( 1854 ) . it was also a stopping-off point on the way to constantinople . in world war i , gallipoli was the site of the gallipoli campaign . the name is from greek `` καλλίπολις '' ( `` kallipolis '' ) , meaning `` beautiful city '' . in antiquity , it was called the thracian chersonese ( , ) .
