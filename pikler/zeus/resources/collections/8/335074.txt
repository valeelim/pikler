Chilton County, Alabama    chilton county , alabama chilton county is a county of the u.s. state of alabama . in 1942 , the u.s. navy commissioned a new vessel , the uss `` chilton '' , in honor of chilton county . in 2010 , the population was 43,643. the county seat is clanton . it is a dry county which means alcoholic drinks can not be sold . the county is known for its peaches and its unique landscape . it is home to swamps , prairies and mountains due to the foothills of the appalachians . it also has the coosa river basin and is close to the black belt prairie . baker county was formed on december 30 , 1868. it was named for alfred baker , with its county seat at grantville . residents of the county asked the alabama legislature for the renaming of their county . in 1874 , they chose the name chilton county . william parish chilton , sr.
