Triodia     triodia is a large genus of grass endemic to australia . they are often called spinifex , but they are not a part of the genus `` spinifex '' , which is only found near the coast . there are 64 known species . triodia is a perennial grass that grows in dry areas . they have spikey , pointed leaves , about 30-40 centimetres long . the leaf tips can break off in people 's skin , causing infections . spinifex has been used by australian aborigines in many things . the seeds are collected for food . spinifex resin is used as a glue in spear-making . the grasses were also burned to make smoke signals , to communicate with groups a long way away . some species are used for building shelters . others are used to make traps for catching fish . references . - lazarides , m.
