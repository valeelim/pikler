Mâcon     mâcon is a `` commune '' in the saône-et-loire department in the bourgogne-franche-comté region in east-central france . it is the `` préfecture '' ( capital ) of the department . even if mâcon is the capital of the department , it is only the second largest city in population after chalon-sur-saône . the celts from the aedui ( people that lived between the saône and loire rivers ) had an oppidum and a river port in the present place of mâcon . this settlement was called matisco by julius caesar . mâcon is the southernmost capital city in the region of bourgogne-franche-comté . it is at north of lyon and from paris . the city lies on the western ( left ) side of the saône river , on the border with the ain department . it has an area of . its average altitude is ; at the city hall , the altitude is . since 1972 , the `` commune '' of mâcon is divided in two parts : the southern part , including the old city , and the northern part . both parts are surrounded by the `` communes '' : the köppen climate classification type for the climate at charnay-lès-mâcon , less than to the west of mâcon and where is the local airport , is an `` oceanic climate '' ( also known as maritime temperate climate ) and of the subtype `` cfb '' .
