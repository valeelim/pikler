Glaucous     glaucous is a colour that is bluish-green or bluish-grey . in botany it refers to a pale bluish waxy or powdery layer on a surface such as a leaf or a fruit . in birds it refers to a pale bluish grey colour of the feathers , legs or some other part of the bird . `` glaucous '' appears in the name of some plants and birds . for example , - glaucous gull , `` ( larus glaucescens ) '' or glaucous macaw , `` ( anodorhynchus glaucus ) '' . in botany there is the glaucous sedge , `` ( carex flacca ) '' and the glaucous michelmas daisy , `` ( symphyotrichum laeve ) '' . often grapes , plums and other fruit have a glaucous waxy or powdery layer on the surface of their skin . that layer protects the fruit from water . it can be easily rubbed off .
