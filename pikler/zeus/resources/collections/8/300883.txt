Arecibo message     the arecibo message was a short radio message sent into space to celebrate the remodeling of the arecibo radio telescope in puerto rico in 1974. it was aimed at the globular star cluster m13 , about 25,000 light years from earth . m13 was chosen because it was the right size , and was in the sky at the right time and place for the ceremony . dr. frank drake , then at cornell university and creator of the famous drake equation , wrote the message , with help from carl sagan , among others . the message was in seven parts which show : 1. the numbers one ( 1 ) to ten ( 10 ) 1. the atomic numbers of the elements hydrogen , carbon , nitrogen , oxygen , and phosphorus , which make up deoxyribonucleic acid ( dna ) 1. the formulas for the sugars and bases in the nucleotides of dna 1. the number of nucleotides in dna , and a graphic of the double helix structure of dna 1. a figure of a human , the size of an average man , and the human population of earth 1. the solar system 1. the arecibo observatory and the diameter of the transmitting antenna dish
