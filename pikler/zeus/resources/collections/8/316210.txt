Kuni-kyō     , also called kuni no miyako , was an ancient imperial capital of japan between 740 and 744. it was in present-day kizugawa in kyoto prefecture . the new capital of emperor shōmu was built by tachibana no moroe . the palace ( `` kuni no miya '' ) was built in the former yamashiro province . in what became the town kizu . the city of kuni-kyō was not completed . the court and capital moved to naniwa-kyō ( osaka ) after only four years . studies of the archaeological site suggest the location of the main buildings . the city was laid out in a grid . the area of palace is estimated to have been 500+ meters wide and 700+ meters long . related pages . - capital of japan
