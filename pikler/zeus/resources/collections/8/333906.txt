Districts of Japan     districts are not all the same size . they are roughly equivalent to a county in the united kingdom or the united states . they are smaller than prefectures and larger than towns and villages . districts were established in the 8th century . until the 10th century , the chief government official of a district ( `` gun '' ) was called the `` gunji '' . during the edo period , the head of a district ( `` gun '' ) was called the `` gundai '' . between 1878 and 1921 , the meiji period and taish≈ç period , districts were given specific powers and responsibilities which are now diffused . related pages . - list of regions of japan - list of islands of japan - municipalities in japan
