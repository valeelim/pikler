St Mawes     st mawes is a small town opposite falmouth , on the roseland peninsula on the south coast of cornwall , united kingdom . it lies on the east bank of the carrick roads into which the river fal flows . the town is in the civil parish of st just in roseland . it was once a busy fishing port , but the trade declined during the 20th century . the town of st mawes is a popular tourist location . there is a ferry service to falmouth , which is less than a mile away by boat , but about 30 miles away by road . the town takes its name from the celtic saint maudez ( mawe ) , who may have come from ireland . st mawes castle is a well-preserved coastal fortress from the time of henry viii , built to resist invasion from europe .
