Geiger–Marsden experiment     the geiger–marsden experiment ( also called the gold foil experiment or the rutherford experiment ) was a scientific experiment done by hans geiger and ernest marsden in 1909. they were instructed by ernest rutherford , and the experiment happened at the physical laboratories of the university of manchester . the results of the experiment were surprising . they showed scientists that the atomic nucleus exists . this disproved the plum pudding model of the atom , and led to the rutherford model ( also called the planetary model ) .
