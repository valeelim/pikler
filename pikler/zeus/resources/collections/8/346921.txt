Gordian knot     the gordian knot is a legend of associated with alexander the great . it is often used as a metaphor for a problem which has no easy or obvious solution . in phrygia ( modern turkey ) , there was a legendary prophesy about a unique knot which could only be loosened by the man who would rule all of asia . it was called the `` gordian '' knot because it was preserved in gordium . it supposed to have been created by a man named gordius . the knot was complex and tangled . this mythical knot was tied around the part of a wooden cart or wagon . according to traditional version of the story , alexander the great cut the knot with his sword . in another version of the story , alexander took away the wooden part which was at the center of the knot ; and in this way , the knot was undone . alexander solved the problem in a way no one had expected . then he went on to lead greek armies which conquered much of the known world . today the phrase implies `` make it happen '' or `` get things done '' . `` cutting the gordian knot '' has come to mean resolving a difficult problem with one forceful action .
