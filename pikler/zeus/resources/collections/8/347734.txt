Isaac Levitan     isaac ilyich levitan ( ; august 30 , 1860 â€“ ) was a lithuanian-russian landscape painter of jewish descent . levitan was born in 1860 in kibarty , lithuania . his father worked for a railroad company . from 1873 to 1885 , levitan went to school at the moscow college of painting , sculpture and architecture in moscow , russia . he learned painting form the painters savrasov and polenov . starting in 1884 his paintings went on display with the society for circulating art exhibitions . he became a member of the society in 1891. levitan taught landscape painting at the moscow college of painting , sculpture and architecture starting in 1898. he died in moscow in 1900. in his career as a painter , levitan painted many landscapes which were later thought of as masterpieces of russian art . levitan painted simple landscapes from russia . early in his career he painted scenes from around moscow . next he painted scenes from around the volga river . his later paintings came from vladimir , vyshny volochek , and the tver region of russia .
