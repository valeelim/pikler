Edward Upward     edward falaise upward ( 9 september 1903 â€“ 13 february 2009 ) was an english novelist and short story writer . at the time of his death , he was considered the uk 's oldest living author . he was born on 9 september 1903 in romford , essex , to harold and louisa upward . his father was a doctor . upward graduated from corpus christi college , oxford in 1925 with a degree in english . he became a teacher which also allowed him time to write . in 1931 he became interested in the communist party . he joined the party in 1934. he wrote for several left-wing journals . these included `` new country '' , `` the left review '' and `` new writing '' . upward 's first novel was `` journey to the border '' , published in 1938. at that time upward was better known for a 1937 essay called `` sketch for a marxist interpretation of literature '' . he wrote a trilogy called `` the spiral ascent '' . the second part had trouble getting published . by the time the third part was published , it had to be underwritten by the action council . the council only agreed after pressure from upward 's friends . the three were titled `` in the thirties '' ( 1962 ) , `` the rotten elements '' ( 1969 ) , and `` no home but the struggle '' ( 1977 ) . the last two were almost completely ignored at the time .
