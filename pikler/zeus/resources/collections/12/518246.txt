Typeface     in typography , a typeface is a family of fonts . every glyph in a typeface shares certain design features . typefaces have names , such as `` times new roman '' , `` garamond '' , `` baskerville '' , and so on . every font of a typeface has specific details ( such as size and weight ) and is used in a particular way . the reason for having different fonts is to give the designer or printer choices which suit various needs . the most basic of all needs is to have the text legible and readable . there are many versions of typefaces which are out of copyright . `` itc garamond '' is a different typeface from `` adobe garamond '' or `` monotype garamond '' . these are all versions of the typeface garamond , originally created in the 16th century . there are thousands of different typefaces in existence , with new ones being developed constantly . the art and craft of designing typefaces is called `` type design '' . in digital typography , type designers are sometimes also called `` font developers '' or `` font designers '' . every typeface is a collection of glyphs , each of which represents an individual letter , number , punctuation mark , or other symbol . there are typefaces tailored for special applications , such as map-making or astrology and mathematics .
