Insurance Institute for Highway Safety     insurance institute for highway safety ( iihs ) is a non-profit organization funded by at least 75 insurance companies . iihs was started in 1959 with their headquarters in arlington , virginia . their goal is to reduce deaths , injuries and property loss from automobile es on us highways . it researches and rates popular passenger vehicles . iihs also rates consumer products such as child safety seat . they also research road design and traffic regulations . it carries out research and produces ratings for popular passenger vehicles as well as for certain consumer products such as child safety seats . it also conducts research on road design and traffic regulations , and has been involved in promoting policy decisions . according to their own bylaws iihs does not lobby the united states congress and other lawmaking bodies to change laws . they educate lawmakers and by invitation . the insurance institute for highway safety was established by insurance companies in 1959 to advise other organizations about highway safety . in 1966 the national traffic and motor vehicle safety act was enacted by congress . it set up what is now called the national highway traffic safety administration . in 1968 when richard nixon became president , william haddon , jr. , was replaced as head of the agency . he was hired by the iihs to reorganize and set up a crash testing program . in the 1970s the iihs was involved in the then controversial airbag standard .
