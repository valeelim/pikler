Comet Swift–Tuttle     comet swift–tuttle ( 109p/swift–tuttle ) is a periodic comet with an orbital period of 133 years . it fits the classical definition of a halley-type comet with a period between 20 and 200 years . it was independently discovered by lewis swift and horace parnell tuttle in july 1862. it has a well-known orbit and has a comet nucleus 26 km in diameter . chinese records show that in 188 ad the comet reached apparent magnitude 0.1. in 1862 , the comet was as bright as polaris . the comet made a return appearance in 1992 , and became visible with binoculars . in 2126 it will be a bright naked-eye comet reaching about apparent magnitude 0.7. it is the parent body of the perseid meteor shower , perhaps the best known shower and among the most reliable in performance . a close encounter with earth is predicted for the comet 's return to the inner solar system in the year 4479 , around sept. 15 ; the closest approach is estimated to be 0.03–0.05 au , with a probability of impact of 1 , or 0.0001 % .
