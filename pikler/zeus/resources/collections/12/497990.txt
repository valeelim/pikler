Photios I of Constantinople     photios i was born c. 810 or 820 in constantinople and died 891 in bordi ( armenia ) . he was the ecumenical patriarch of constantinople from 858 to 867 and from 877 to 886. in the eastern orthodox churches he is called st. photios the great . photios a powerful and influential patriarch of constantinople . as a scholar , photios possessed a tremendous knowledge of greek literature . he was a leading intellectual of his time , `` the leading light of the ninth-century renaissance '' . he was a central figure in both the religious conversion of the slavs to christianity and the photian schism .
