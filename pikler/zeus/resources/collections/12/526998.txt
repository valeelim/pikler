Qualia     qualia is a term philosophers use for bits of perception or subjective , conscious experience . it is in the field called the philosophy of psychology , or philosophy of mind . examples of qualia include the pain of a headache , the taste of wine , or the perceived redness of an evening sky . it is a separate bit of experience , different from thinking about propositions or stream of consciousness thought . daniel dennett ( b . 1942 ) , american philosopher and cognitive scientist , says `` qualia '' is `` an unfamiliar term for something that could not be more familiar to each of us : the ways things seem to us '' .
