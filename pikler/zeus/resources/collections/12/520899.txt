Meningococcal disease     meningococcal disease , also called meningococcal meningitis , is infections caused by the bacterium meningococcus ( `` neisseria meningitidis '' ) . if left untreated , the death rate is high . however , it can be prevented by vaccines . the disease is best-known for causing meningitis . it also causes blood infection , which then leads to sepsis . there are over 2,600 cases of bacterial meningitis in the united states per year . in developing countries , there are over 333,000 cases per year . the case death rate is between ten and twenty percent . meningococcal disease is not as spreadable as the common cold . however , it can be passed between people through saliva .
