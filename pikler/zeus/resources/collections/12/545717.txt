Iron ore     iron ores are rocks and minerals from which metallic iron can be economically extracted . iron ( fe ) is one of the most abundant rock-forming elements . it makes up about 5 % of the earth 's crust . it is the second most abundant and widely distributed metal ( aluminium is the most common ) . people have used it for more than 3,000 years . however , its use only became widespread in the 14th century , when smelting furnaces ( the forerunner of blast furnaces ) began to replace forges . the ores are usually rich in iron oxides and vary in colour from dark grey , bright yellow , deep purple , to rusty red . iron ores . - magnetite ( , 72.4 % fe ) - hematite ( , 69.9 % fe ) - goethite ( , 62.9 % fe ) - limonite ( ) - siderite ( , 48.2 % fe )
