Offa's Dyke    offa 's dyke offa 's dyke ( welsh : clawdd offa ) is a large earthwork that roughly follows the current border between england and wales . the structure is named after offa , the 8th century king of mercia . he is believed to have ordered its construction . its original purpose is not known for sure . it followed the border between anglian mercia and the welsh kingdom of powys . the dyke was up to 65 feet ( 20 m ) wide ( including its flanking ditch ) and 8 feet ( 2.4 m ) high . it ran over low ground , hills and rivers . today the earthwork is protected as a scheduled monument . offa 's dyke path is a 176 mile ( 283 km ) footpath which runs between prestatyn in the north ( liverpool bay ) and sedbury , near chepstow ( the severn estuary ) in the south . this is from north of offa 's dyke to south of it , and runs near the dyke in its central part . it is also thought to be constructed under the orders of offa . it is a britsh national trail . some sections of the dyke are defined as sites of special scientific interest , and areas of outstanding natural beauty . most of the line of offa 's dyke is designated as a public right of way , including those sections which form part of the offa 's dyke path .
