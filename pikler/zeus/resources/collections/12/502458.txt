Ste-Anne Church (Ottawa)    ste-anne church ( ottawa ) ste-anne is a catholic church in the lowertown neighbourhood of ottawa , canada . it was built in 1873 by architect j.p. lecourt . it is built like churches in quebec even though it is in ontario . ste-anne is the home of st. clement parish , a bilingual parish community that celebrates the mass and other sacraments in latin using the 1962 roman missal . in the 1870s , the french catholics in ottawa were meeting at notre-dame cathedral . this church was too small for them , so bishop joseph-bruno guigues decided to make a new church . the church was designed by j.p. lecourt and built by pierre rocque . bishop guigues laid the cornerstone on may 4 , 1873. in april 2009 , part of the roof fell down . the church was closed for 18 months while the roof was fixed . when the church was opened again , not many people were meeting there anymore , so the archdiocese of ottawa closed it again . archbishop terrence prendergast gave the church to st. clement parish , who started to meet there on june 3 , 2012 .
