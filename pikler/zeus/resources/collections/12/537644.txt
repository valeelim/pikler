Sodomy laws in the United States     sodomy laws in the united states were laws that made certain kinds of sexual activity illegal . in the past , there were federal laws against sodomy . every state also had a a sodomy law , even in the 20th century . starting in the 1960s , some states began to repeal ( throw out ) their sodomy laws . in 2003 , in a case called `` lawrence v. texas '' , the supreme court of the united states ruled that sodomy laws were unconstitutional . this means no sodomy law in the united states can be used to charge a person with a crime . most sodomy laws in the united states made both oral sex and anal sex illegal . however , state laws did not agree on `` who '' could not do these things . every state law made sodomy between homosexual couples illegal . other state laws also made sodomy illegal between heterosexual couples , if they were not married . the strictest laws made sodomy illegal in every case , even between married couples . some sodomy laws also included bestiality in their definition of `` sodomy .
