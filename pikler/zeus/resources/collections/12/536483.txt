Federal judiciary of the United States     the federal judiciary of the united states is one of the three co-equal branches of the federal government of the united states organized under the united states constitution . article iii of the constitution requires the establishment of a supreme court and permits the congress to create other federal courts , and place limitations on their jurisdiction . article iii united states federal judges are appointed by the president with the consent of the senate to serve until they resign , are impeached and convicted , retire , or die . the federal courts are composed of three levels of courts . the supreme court of the united states is the highest court . it is generally an appellate court that operates under discretionary review , which means that the court can choose which cases to hear , by granting of writs of certiorari . there is generally no right of appeal to the supreme court . in a few situations ( like lawsuits between state governments or some cases between the federal government and a state ) it sits as a court of original jurisdiction .
