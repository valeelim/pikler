Oskar Heinroth     oskar heinroth ( mainz-kastel , 1 march 1871 – berlin , 31 may 1945 ) was a german biologist who was one of the founders of ethology . heinroth was director of the berlin zoo & aquarium for over 30 years . he married twice , both times to colleagues : he was married to magdalena ( née wiebe , 1904-1932 ) and , after her death , to katharina ( née berger , 1933-1945 ) . his studies of behaviour in the anatidae ( ducks and geese ) showed their instinctive behaviour patterns . he also rediscovered the phenomenon of imprinting , reported in the 19th century by douglas spalding but not followed up at the time . heinroth showed that the behaviour of birds was characteristic of a species , as much as its anatomy . his results were popularised by konrad lorenz , whose mentor he was . lorenz regarded heinroth as the true founder of the study of animal behaviour as a branch of zoology . sources . - oskar heinroth & magdalena heinroth 1924–1934 . `` die vögel mitteleuropas '' . illustrated , 4 vols , bermühler , berlin .
