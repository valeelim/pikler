Abduction     abduction is the criminal act of taking a person away by means of fraud , persuasion , by threat of violence or by force . kidnapping , by comparison , is limited to a threat of violence or forcefully taking adults or the by the taking of children . it often involves some form of false imprisonment . in some states , kidnapping and abduction are treated as the same thing . but in most jurisdictions they are separate crimes . unlawful interference with family members such as removing a child from its parents is abduction . it does not matter if the minor consents or not . kidnapping is the unlawful taking of a person against their will . abduction can also mean the taking of any female person for the purposes of prostitution , concubinage or ( forced ) marriage . during wars , children are often abducted . they may be treated brutally or killed . they may be victims of sexual violence or may be used as soldiers . children are abducted both by armed parties or governments . they may be taken as an act of retaliation against a population .
