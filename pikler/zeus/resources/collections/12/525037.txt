Shipwreck     a shipwreck is the remains of a ship that has been wrecked . shipwrecks are found either beached on land or sunken to the seabed of a body of water . they may be deliberate or accidental . unesco estimates that worldwide over 3 million shipwrecks . some are thousands of years old . historic wrecks are attractive to marine archaeologists because they preserve historical information . for example , studying the wreck of `` mary rose '' revealed information about seafaring , warfare , and life in the 16th century . military wrecks , caused by a battle at sea , are studied to find details about the historic events . they can reveal much about the battle that occurred . once in a while there is a discovery of a treasure ship , often from the period of european colonisation . some contemporary wrecks , such as the oil tankers `` prestige '' or `` erika '' , are of interest because of their potential harm to the environment . other contemporary wrecks are scuttled ( sank deliberately ) in order to create reef growth .
