Super Elastic Bubble Plastic     super elastic bubble plastic was the brand name for a children 's toy . it was made from the 1970s through the 80s by wham-o . the bubbles contained polyvinyl acetate dissolved in acetone . they also had ethyl acetate added . the acetone evaporated when the bubbles were inflated through a drinking straw . this left behind a solid plastic film . they were also highly flammable . the product was dangerous to children if spilled . it also gave off noxious fumes . the fumes could become concentrated inside the straw , making it dangerous to inhale through the straw while inflating a bubble . the toy was not recommended for children under 5. because of these problems , super elastic bubble plastic was eventually discontinued .
