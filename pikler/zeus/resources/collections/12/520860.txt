Warren Commission     the president 's commission on the assassination of president kennedy , known unofficially as the warren commission , was established by president lyndon b. johnson on november 29 , 1963. its purpose was to the assassination of united states president john f. kennedy that had taken place on november 22 , 1963. the commission took its unofficial name—the warren commission—from its chairman , chief justice earl warren . according to published transcripts of johnson 's presidential telephone conversations , some major officials did not want to form this commission . also that several commission members took part only reluctantly . one of their chief reservations was that a commission would create more controversy and people 's fears would be proved valid . the 88th u.s. congressional session passed senate joint resolution 137 authorizing the presidential appointed commission to report on the assassination of president john f. kennedy . its 889-page final report was presented to president johnson on september 24 , 1964. it was made public three days later . it concluded that president kennedy was assassinated by lee harvey oswald . they also stated that oswald acted entirely alone . the commission said that jack ruby also acted alone when he killed oswald two days later . the commission 's findings have proven controversial . they have been both challenged and supported by later studies .
