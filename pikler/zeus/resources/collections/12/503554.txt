Gallows letter     in postal history , a gallows letter was a letter with a `` gallows '' mark meaning `` very quickly '' . gallows letters were official letters or dispatches used by the privy council of elizabeth i of england . to express an extreme degree of urgency of delivery , the letter had on the address panel a rough drawing of a gallows . sometimes the picture included a hanging body . these drawings could also have repeated written directions as `` haste haste '' , `` post haste '' , `` haste for life '' , `` for life haste '' , etc . there are also modern forgeries of such letters . this is when counterfeit gallows marks have been applied onto original letters . related pages . - envelope - letter ( paper )
