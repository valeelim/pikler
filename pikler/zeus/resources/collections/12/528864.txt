Stock (firearm)    stock ( firearm ) a stock , also known as a shoulder stock , a buttstock , or simply a butt is a part of a rifle or other firearm , to which the barrel and firing mechanism are attached . it is held firmly against the shooter 's shoulder when firing the gun . stocks are also found on crossbows , though a crossbow stock is properly called a `` tiller '' . the stock provides a means for the shooter to firmly support the device and easily aim it . the stock also transmits recoil ( the shock of firing the weapon ) into the shooter 's shoulder . the term stock dates to 1571 and comes from the germanic word `` stoc '' , meaning tree trunk . this refers to the wooden nature of the gunstock .
