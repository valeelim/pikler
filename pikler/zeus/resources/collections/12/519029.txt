Ore-bulk-oil carrier     an ore-bulk-oil carrier , also called a combination carrier or obo , is a ship that carries wet or dry cargo . the idea is to reduce the number of empty voyages in which large ships carry cargo one way and are empty for another . obo carriers are today not as common as in the 1970s and 1980s . one of the most famous obo carriers is the `` derbyshire '' . it sank during a typhoon in 1980 in the pacific .
