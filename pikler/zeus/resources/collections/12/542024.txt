Bourgogne-Franche-Comté     bourgogne-franche-comté ( meaning `` burgundy–free county '' ) is one of the administrative regions of france . it is in eastern france and was formed in 2014 from two old regions : bourgogne and franche-comté . `` aquitaine-limousin-poitou-charentes '' was the temporary name for the region . its capital ( préfecture ) is dijon but the council of the region meets in besançon . the bourgogne-franche-comté region is the sixth largest region of france with an area of . it is in northeastern france and borders to the north with the grand est region , to the west with île-de-france and centre-val de loire regions , to the south with the auvergne-rhône-alpes region , and to the east with switzerland ( the cantons of vaud , neuchâtel and jura ) . the distances from dijon , the capital of the region , to other cities are : - paris , the national capital , ; - bordeaux , ; - toulouse , ; - marseille , ; - lyon , ; - montpellier , ; - nice , . the main rivers in the region are the saône and its tributary the doubs . the loire river flows through the southwest of the region , at nevers . the seine river starts in the côte-d'or department .
