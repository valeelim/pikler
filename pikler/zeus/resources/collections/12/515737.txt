Fifth Amendment to the United States Constitution     created on december 15 , 1791 , the fifth amendment to the united states constitution is a part of the united states bill of rights . this amendment establishes a number of legal rights that apply to both civil and criminal proceedings . it contains several clauses : it guarantees the right to a grand jury . it forbids double jeopardy ( being tried again for the same crime after an acquittal ) . it protects a person against self-incrimination ( being a witness against himself ) . this is often called `` pleading the fifth '' . the fifth amendment requires due process in any case where a citizen may be deprived of `` life , liberty , or property '' . any time the government takes private property for public use , the owner must be compensated . the language of the fifth amendment is : the fifth amendment requires the use of grand juries by the federal legal system for all capital and `` infamous crimes '' ( cases involving treason , certain felonies or gross moral turpitude ) . grand juries trace their roots back to the assize of clarendon , an enactment by henry ii of england in 1166. the united states is one of the few remaining countries that uses the grand jury .
