Little owl     the little owl ( `` athene noctua '' ) is a bird that lives in the temperate and warmer parts of europe , asia ( east to korea ) , and north africa . it was introduced into britain at the end of the nineteenth century , and into the south island of new zealand in the early twentieth century . this owl is a member of the strigidae , which contains most species of owl . the other group is the barn owls , tytonidae . the little owl is a small , cryptically coloured , mainly nocturnal species . it is found in a range of habitats including farmland , woodland fringes , steppes and semi-deserts . it feeds on insects , earthworms , other invertebrates and small vertebrates . males have territories which they defend against intruders . this owl is a cavity nester and a clutch of about four eggs is laid in spring . the female does the incubation and the male brings food to the nest , first for the female and later for the newly hatched young . as the chicks grow , both parents hunt and bring them food , and the chicks leave the nest at about seven weeks of age .
