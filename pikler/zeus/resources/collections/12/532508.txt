Pareidolia     pareidolia ) is a word form ancient greek to describe the phenomenon , that the human mind often perceives familiar patterns in a stimulus , even when they are not there . common examples are perceived images of animals , faces , or objects in cloud formations , the `` man in the moon '' , the `` moon rabbit '' , and hidden messages within recorded music played in reverse or at higher- or lower-than-normal speeds . pareidolia is a special case of clustering illusion .
