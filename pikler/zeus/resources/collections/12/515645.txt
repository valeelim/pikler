Biological rhythms     biological rhythms are the repeating cycles of activity which occur in living organisms . the best-known example is the daily of circadian rhythm , which fits the cycle of day and night . most forms of life have natural rhythms which fit them to natural changes in their environment . animals are most active in part of the day , and rest or sleep at other times . they may be : - diurnal , which describes organisms active during daytime - nocturnal , active in the night - crepuscular , active during the dawn and dusk hours plants undergo daily cycles as well . photosynthesis has parts which need light , called light-dependent reactions , and parts which can continue at night ( light-independent reactions ) . many other important cycles are also studied , including : - cycles longer than a day , such as the annual migration or reproduction cycles found in certain animals or the human menstrual cycle . - cycles shorter than 24 hours , such as the 90-minute rem cycle , the 4-hour nasal cycle , or the 3-hour cycle of growth hormone production .
