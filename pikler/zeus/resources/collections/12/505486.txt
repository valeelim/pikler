Black-headed parrot     the black-headed parrot is `` pionites melanocephalus '' . it lives in forests , most often in humid places , and nearby wooded habitats . they are known as 'caiques ' . the bird is widespread in south america , and has two subspecies which are very similar . it lives north of the amazon river and west of the ucayali river in brazil , northern bolivia , colombia , ecuador , french guiana , guyana , peru , suriname , and venezuela . it is quite common and lives in many protected areas . they are usually in pairs or small noisy groups of 10 to 30. they eat flowers , fruit , and seeds and possibly insects . males and females look the same : they have identical plumage .
