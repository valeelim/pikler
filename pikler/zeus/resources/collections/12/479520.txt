Full-size vehicle     a full-size car is a marketing term used in north america for an automobile larger than a mid-size car . traditional u.s. full-size passenger cars were designed to be comfortable for six occupants and their luggage for long-distance driving . the united states environmental protection agency ( epa ) currently uses the term `` large car '' to mean full-size cars based on their combined interior passenger and luggage volume . a full-size suv ( or cuv ) , is a utility vehicle that has over of cargo volume . this is measured when the rear seats are folded or removed for more cargo capacity . both full-size and mid-size suvs and cuvs have three rows of seating ( 8 or 9 passengers ) . a full size van is a marketing term used for a van larger than a minivan . seating is one difference . a full-size van may be equipped to seat up to 15 passengers . a minivan often seats up to 8 .
