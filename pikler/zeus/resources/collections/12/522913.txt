Douglas Hurd     douglas richard hurd , baron hurd of westwell ( born 8 march 1930 ) is a british conservative politician . he served in the governments of margaret thatcher and john major from 1979 to 1995. he was born in the market town of marlborough in wiltshire . hurd first entered parliament in february 1974 as mp for the mid oxfordshire constituency ( witney from 1983 ) . his first government post was as minister for europe from 1979 to 1983 ( being that office 's inaugural holder ) and he served in several cabinet roles since 1984. his jobs include secretary of state for northern ireland ( 1984–85 ) , home secretary ( 1985–89 ) and foreign secretary ( 1989–95 ) . he stood unsuccessfully for the conservative party leadership in 1990 , but retired from frontline politics during a cabinet reshuffle in 1995. in 1997 , hurd was elevated to the house of lords and is one of the conservative party 's most senior elder statesmen . he is a patron of the tory reform group and remains an active figure in public life .
