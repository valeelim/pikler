Wadj-wer     wadj-wer ( meaning `` great blue-green '' ) is an egyptian god of fertility . `` wadj-wer '' is sometimes shown as being part male and part female . he is seen as the personification of the mediterranean sea or some of the big lakes in the nile delta . he is often shown holding a ankh and a loaf . he is also shown to be pregnant , and he symbolizes the richness of the waters of the nile delta . references . - encyclopedia of gods , kyle cathie limited , 2002 - james p. allen ( 2010 ) . `` middle egyptian : an introduction to the language and culture of hieroglyphics '' ( 2nd ed. , revised ) . new york : cambridge .
