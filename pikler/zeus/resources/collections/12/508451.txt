Lake Marion (South Carolina)    lake marion ( south carolina ) lake marion is the largest lake in south carolina . it is centrally located . parts of it are in five different counties . the lake is referred to as south carolina 's inland sea . it has a shoreline . it covers almost 110,000 acres ( 450 square kilometers or 173.7 square miles ) of rolling farmlands , former marshes , and river valley landscape . the santee river was dammed in the 1940s to supply hydroelectric power . this was part of the rural electrification efforts initiated under president franklin d. roosevelt 's new deal during the great depression . it is one of the fifty largest lakes in the united states , whether natural or man-made reservoirs . it covers only about a third of the area of the fifteenth largest in size .
