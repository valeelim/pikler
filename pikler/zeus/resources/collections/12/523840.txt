LGBT rights in Mali     lesbian , gay , bisexual , and transgender ( lgbt ) persons in mali may face legal challenges not experienced by non-lgbt residents . according to the 2007 pew global attitudes project , 98 % of malian adults do not believe that homosexuality is acceptable by society . this was one of the highest rate of non-acceptance in the 45 countries surveyed . same-sex sexual activity is not a crime in mali . but according to a 2013 report on human rights in mali `` the law prohibits association 'for an immoral purpose ' '' . about 90 % of mali 's population is muslim . mali has long been attacked by islamic rebels and militants . this has put gays at risk . in 2013 islamist extremists took over the northern part of mali . france sent 3,500 troops to help mali . the extremists took over the town of gao and enforced strict sharia law . this religious law of islam punishes thieves by amputation and gay men by execution . french troops were able to rescue two malian men who were about to be executed for homosexuality .
