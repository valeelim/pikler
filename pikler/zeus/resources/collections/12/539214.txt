Rowley Habib     rowley habib ( 24 april 1933 – 3 april 2016 ) , also known as rore hapipi , was a new zealand poet , playwright , and writer of short stories and television scripts . in short story writing , from 1956 to 1971 habib was a regular contributor to `` te ao hou the new world '' , a magazine for māori . in 1984 , habib was awarded the katherine mansfield menton fellowship . in 2013 , creative new zealand awarded him a ngā tohu a tā kingi ihaka te waka toi award in recognition of his lifetime of service to māori arts , describing his play `` death of the land '' as a `` landmark in the development of māori theatre . '' habib died on 3 april 2016 , aged 82 .
