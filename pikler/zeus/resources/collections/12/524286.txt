Farina, South Australia    farina , south australia farina is a ghost town in south australia . it is on the edge of the desert , with an unpredictable annual rainfall of 163.6 mm . it was a farming and railway town , part of the ghan railway , 26 km north of lyndhurst and 55 km south of marree . in 2006 there were 55 people living near farina . before 1878 farina was called the gums or government gums , it was an important waterhole in a dry desert . farina was settled in 1878 by farmers who thought that if they ploughed the land and planted crops it would rain . it was named farina after the latin word for flour . the town was the railhead until 1884 when the railway reached marree . during the wet years of the 1880s , plans were laid out for a town with 432 Â¼-acre blocks . it was believed that it would be good for growing wheat and barley , however normal rainfall is not enough to grow these crops . several silver and copper mines were dug in the surrounding area . farina grew to 600 people in the late 1800s . the town had two hotels ( the transcontinental and the exchange ) and an underground bakery , a bank , two breweries , a general store , an anglican church , five blacksmiths , a school and a brothel . in 1909 , a 1143 kg iron meteorite was discovered north-east of the town .
