Fundamental rights     fundamental rights are a set of rights that are recognized under the laws of a country to protect their citizens . these laws protect these rights even from the governments themselves . these protected rights include the right to life , right to freedom , and the right to free will . other generally agreed upon examples of fundamental rights include freedom of religion , the right to free speech , the right to travel and the pursuit of happiness . fundamental rights are different from human rights . fundamental rights are those protected by laws of a jurisdiction . human rights are usually not enforceable by courts . related pages . - rights - human rights - natural rights ( also called unalienable rights ) - constitution
