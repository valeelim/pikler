Sidereal time     sidereal time is a time-keeping system . it is used by astronomers to find celestial objects . using sidereal time it is possible to point a telescope to the proper coordinates in the night sky . sidereal time is a `` time scale based on earth 's rate of rotation measured relative to the fixed stars '' . because the earth moves in its orbit about the sun , a mean solar day is about four minutes longer than a sidereal day . thus , a star appears to rise four minutes earlier each night , compared to solar time . different stars are visible at different times of the year . by contrast , solar time is reckoned by the movement of the earth from the perspective of the sun . an average solar day ( 24 hours ) is longer than a sidereal day ( 23 hours , 56 minutes , 4 seconds ) because of the amount the earth moves each day in its orbit around the sun . other pages . - rotation period
