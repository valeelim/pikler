Danegeld     the danegeld ( `` danish tax '' ) was a tax that the english kingdoms paid to the vikings between the 9th and 11th centuries . the vikings called it “ geld ” or “ gafol ” in the 11th century , but the term danegeld did not appear until the early 12th century . besides the taxes in england , the term also applies to eastern europe in the 9th century , where sami , finnish , and slavic tribes paid taxes to the swedes . in francia ( france ) , the franks paid off the vikings in normandy ( normans ) , and in return , the normans would convert to christianity and cease to raid .
