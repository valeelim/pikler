Minicoy Island     minicoy island or maliku ( mahal language : މަލިކު , məliku ) is the only inhabited island of the maliku atoll . the locals , the `` malikun '' , call their island `` maliku , '' as do the maldivians , the closest maldive island to minicoy is thuraakunu . since 1956 , the indian government has forbidden contact between people of the same nationals of the two islands despite their geographic proximity and ethnographic similarities . the cultural traits of minicoy differ from those of any other indian islands of lakshadweep . manners , customs and food are similar to those of the maldives to the south of minicoy and mahl , a dialect of dhivehi , is the language spoken on the island . this language employs the tāna script , written from right to left . minicoy islanders , like the close islands of the maldives follow sunni islam . the islands were probably buddhist before like they were in the maldives . in august 1947 the possessions of the emperor of british india passed to either the indian union or pakistan according to an agreed demarcation line . the rulers of the independent countries that were vassals of the emperor had the choice of acceding to either the dominion of india or pakistan .
