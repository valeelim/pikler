Xiphactinus     xiphactinus ( from latin and greek for `` sword-ray '' ) was a large , 4.5 to 5 m ( 15 to 20 feet ) long predatory bony fish that lived in the western interior sea , over what is now the middle of north america , during the late cretaceous . skeletal remains of xiphactinus have come from kansas , alabama , and georgia in the united states , as well as europe and australia . above all else , `` xiphactinus '' was a great swimmer , able to speed towards or away from virtually anything else in the seas of the time . it may have been able to leap above the waves at times to help dislodge parasites from its skin . it was not however immune from attack . if injured , its large size meant it was easy to spot and could become prey for sharks , mosasaurs , and other xiphactinus .
