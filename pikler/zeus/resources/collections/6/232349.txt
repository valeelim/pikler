Baking powder     baking powder is a dry chemical leavening agent used to make baked foods lighter and less dense . baking powder works by making a reaction between an acid and a base , which produces carbon dioxide bubbles to make the mixture light . it is used when the food should not taste of yeast or when the mixture is not elastic enough to hold bubbles for very long . because carbon dioxide is made faster by baking powder than yeast , breads made with it are called quick breads . most baking powders are made up of an alkaline ( usually baking soda ) , one or two acid salts , and a starch ( cornstarch or sometimes potato starch ) . baking soda is where the carbon dioxide comes from , and the acid releases it , like this :
