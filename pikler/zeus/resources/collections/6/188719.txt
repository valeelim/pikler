Sympathetic nervous system     the sympathetic nervous system is part of the autonomic nervous system . it becomes more active when you are stressed . it is a part of the `` fight or flight '' response . the sympathetic nervous system can increase heart rate ; make bronchial passages wider ; decrease motility ( movement ) of the large intestine ; make blood vessels narrower ; increase peristalsis in the esophagus ; cause pupil dilation , piloerection ( goose bumps ) and perspiration ( sweating ) ; and raise blood pressure . other pages . - parasympathetic nervous system - enteric nervous system
