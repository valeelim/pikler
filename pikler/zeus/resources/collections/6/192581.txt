Repertoire     repertoire ( pronounce : `` re-per-twahr '' ) is a french word used in music and in the theatre . it means a list of pieces of music or plays . sometimes the english word repertory is used . when a pianist speaks about his or her `` repertoire '' , it means all the pieces that he can play . most famous musicians have a wide repertoire ( they can play a lot of pieces ) . the `` piano repertoire '' means all music written for the piano . the violin has a much larger solo repertoire than the viola . that means that much more solo music has been written for the violin than for the viola . a theatre company may have a `` repertoire '' . that means all the plays that they regularly perform . sometimes theatre companies are called `` repertory theatres '' because they have a particular list of plays that they perform .
