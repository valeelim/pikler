Nimzo-Indian defence     the nimzo-indian defence is a popular chess opening with these initial moves : 1.d4 nf6 2.c4 e6 3.nc3 bb4 . the basic idea is for black to challenge for control of the central square e4 . this comes at the cost of exchanging his bb4 for the white nc3 when white plays a3 . the opening is named after aaron nimzovich , who developed and promoted it . initially a hypermodern opening , it usually transposes ( changes ) into classical form , as black occupies central squares by ... c5 and ... d5 . the white central pawns are under restraint . much preparation is needed before they can move forward .
