Seal of Maine     the seal of the state of maine was adopted in june 1820. there have been different kinds in the details of the seal , but the overall design and images remain true to the first . the center of the seal is a shield with a scene of a moose resting in a field bordered by water and woods , pine tree stand tall directly behind the moose . on either side of the shield , a farmer rests on his scythe , and a sailor leans on an anchor . above the shield is the motto `` dirigo '' ( i lead ) , and a north star . below the shield is a banner that reads `` maine '' . the legislature of 1919 thought that the design of the seal should no longer vary , and the design is still used today .
