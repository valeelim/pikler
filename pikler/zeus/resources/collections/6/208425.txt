Straight pool     straight pool , also called 14.1 continuous or simply 14.1 , is a pocket billiards ( pool ) game . it was the common sport of championship competition until faster-playing games like nine-ball and to a lesser extent eight-ball became more popular . this is the classic game from the history of pool . the game was especially popular in the united states , and was the subject of the classic 1961 film `` the hustler '' . in straight pool , the person shooting may attempt to pocket any ball on the table . the aim of the game is to reach a set number of points . the amount of points needed to win is agreed to before the game . one point is scored for each ball pocketed legally ( that is , without a foul ) . a typical game might require a player to score 100 points . this means that at least 100 balls must be pocketed to win . in professional competition , straight pool is usually played to 150 points . straight pool is a call-pocket game . this means that the player must call what pocket they mean to sink the ball into on every shot . it does not matter how balls reach the pocket . as long as no foul is involved , and the balls goes into the pocket that is called , a point is scored .
