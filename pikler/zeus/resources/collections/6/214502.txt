Topiary     topiary is the art of creating sculptures by clipping trees and bushes ( shrubs ) . the shrubs used in topiary are evergreen , have small leaves , and produce dense foliage . common plants used in topiary include bay laurel , holly bushes , myrtle and privet hedges . shaped wire cages are sometimes used in modern topiary but traditional topiary depends on using special scissors with patience and a steady hand . the word derives from the latin word for an ornamental landscape gardener . a friend of julius caesar introduced rome to the topiary . in japan and china they have “ cloud pruning ” where they cut bushes into the shapes of clouds and “ karikomi ” , where they cut large shrubs into curved shapes and objects . bonsai is a form of topiary . during the 16th century in europe typical traditional topiary included geometric shapes : balls , pyramids , cones , and spirals . in the 20th century walt disney used topiaries in the form of his cartoon characters to decorate his theme parks .
