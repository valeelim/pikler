Rhizobia     rhizobia are soil bacteria which become established inside root nodules of legumes ( the pea family : fabaceae ) . they fix nitrogen in a form the plant can use . rhizobia require a plant host ; they can not independently fix nitrogen . the relationship is a classic example of symbiosis and mutualism . the word rhizobia comes from the english root for root ( rhizo ) and the ancient greek for life ( bios ) .
