51 Pegasi     51 pegasi is a star that is like our sun and can be found 15.4 parsecs ( 50.1 light-years ) away from the earth in the constellation pegasus . it was the first sun-like star , other than the sun , found to have a planet orbiting it , this discovery was first made public in 1995. the exoplanet 's discovery was made public on october 6 , 1995 by michel mayor and didier queloz . the discovery was made using the radial velocity method at the observatoire de haute-provence , using a tool called the elodie spectrograph that is used to find planets that are outside our solar system . the star itself has an apparent brightness of 5.49 , and because of this it is able to be seen from the earth with binoculars , or with the naked eye if it is dark outside . 51 pegasi is a yellow dwarf star that is thought to be around 7.5 billion years old , which is somewhat older than our sun . 51 pegasi is also 4-6 % more massive than our sun with more metal content . however , it is running low on hydrogen . in 1996 astronomers baliunas , sokoloff , and soon calculated that it takes 51 pegasi b 37 days to orbit 51 pegasi .
