Bicameral     a bicameral system of government is one where there are two legislative or parliamentary chambers . the word comes from the latin `` bi '' ( meaning two ) and `` camera '' ( meaning chamber ) . in most cases they have different numbers of members . the smaller legislature is often called the `` upper house '' or `` senate '' and the larger is called the `` lower house '' , and sometimes also called by another name such as `` assembly '' or `` commons '' . for legislation to be passed , bicameral legislatures normally need a majority of members of both chambers to vote for the legislation . experts sometimes point out that often deadlocks occur in this kind of system . this can make it harder to pass laws . but others point out the checks and balances this system maintains . this prevents laws from being passed that favor a certain political faction , the government or group of people . bicameral systems in the world . - in the united kingdom , the two chambers are called the house of commons and the house of lords . - the united states congress is bicameral .
