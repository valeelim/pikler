Rafflesia     rafflesia is a genus of flowering plants . they are found in southeastern asia , on the malay peninsula , borneo , sumatra , thailand and the philippines . they are the biggest single flowers in the world . in one species the flowers can be over a meter ( three feet ) across . the flowers smell like a dead animal , which attracts flies . the flies , which normally lay their eggs on rotten flesh , pollinate the `` rafflesia '' when they get inside . `` rafflesia '' is a parasite , and does not use chlorophyll . the plant has no stem , leaves or true roots . it lives inside vines of the genus `` tetrastigma '' ( vitaceae ) . it spreads its absorptive organ , the haustorium , inside the tissue of the vine . the only part of the plant that can be seen outside the host vine is the flower . references . - jamili nais 2001 . `` rafflesia of the world '' . kota kinabalu : sabah parks . isbn 983-812-042-1 other websites . - parasitic plant connection : `` rafflesia '' gallery - video : 'david attenborough finds the rafflesia '
