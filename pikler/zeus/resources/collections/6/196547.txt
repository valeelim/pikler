New Zealand Dollar     the new zealand dollar ( nzd ) is the money used in new zealand , cook islands , niue , tokelau and the pitcairn islands . it has been in use since 1967 , before that the new zealand pound was used . one dollar is equal to 100 cents . it is normally used with the $ or nz $ to show it is the new zealand dollar . from 1967 the coins being used were 1 cent , 2 cents , 5 cents , 10 cents , 20 cents and 50 cents . the 1 cent and 2 cent coins being made of copper and the rest being made in cupro-nickel . in 1987 the 1 cent and 2 cent coins stopped being made and were n't used anymore by 1990. in 1991 the 1 dollar and 2 dollar banknotes got changed into coins . they were made of aluminium-bronze . in 2006 the new zealand government decided to stop using the 5 cent coin and make all the other coins expect for the 1 and 2 dollar coins smaller . they also made the new smaller 10 cent coin copper . the first paper banknotes were printed in 1967. the one , two , five , ten , twenty and one hundred dollar notes . the one and two dollar notes were replaced by coins in 1991. in 1983 a 50 dollar banknote was introduced . in 1999 , new zealand changed from paper banknotes to polymer banknotes .
