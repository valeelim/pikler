William Jennings Bryan     william jennings bryan ( march 19 , 1860-july 26 , 1925 ) was an american politician in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries . he was from the midwest . he is known for supporting causes that were not very popular or were old-fashioned , such as the silver standard or creationism . in the 1890s , he was a congressman from nebraska . he gained fame in 1896 for the `` cross of gold speech '' , a speech about the american economy he gave to the populist party . he ran for president as a democrat three times , in 1896 , 1900 , and 1908 , but lost each time . during this time , he was the main leader of the democratic party . he was one of the first people who gave lots of speeches around the country while running for president , and continued to give speeches even when not running on what was called the `` chatauqua circuit '' . he was united states secretary of state from 1913 to 1915. in his last years , he was one of the lawyers in the scopes monkey trial . he is portrayed in the book `` inherit the wind '' by the character matthew harrison brady .
