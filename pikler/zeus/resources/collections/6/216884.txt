Tteok     tteok is a korean traditional rice cake which is made of rice flour by kneading it with water . it tastes chewy and sticky . tteok is usually only eaten on holidays or birthdays . koreans eat tteok depending on the season . on the first day of the year , they make `` garaetteok '' which is shaped like a long white cylinder because it looks like a white thin thread that represents a long life generally in asian culture . sliced garaetteok is used for making a soup named tteokguk . in march , women make small sweet korean pancakes called `` hwajeon '' with flowers . they use flowers such as azaleas and chrysanthemums . on chuseok holiday , korean thanksgiving day , koreans make `` songpyeon '' . they dough rice flour and stuff sesame , bean or chestnut into each . after they put it in , they shape it into a half moon .
