Permanent way     the permanent way is the elements of railway lines : generally the pairs of rails typically laid on ties ( `` sleepers '' in british parlance ) embedded in ballast , intended to carry the ordinary trains of a railway . it is described as permanent way because in the earlier days of railway construction , contractors often laid a temporary track to transport spoil and materials about the site ; when this work was substantially completed , the temporary track was taken up and the permanent way installed .
