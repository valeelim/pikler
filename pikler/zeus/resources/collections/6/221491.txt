Child custody     child custody and guardianship are legal terms which are sometimes used to describe the legal relationship between a parent and his or her child . it includes such things as the right of the parent to make decisions for the child , and the parent 's duty to care for the child . child custody decisions have to be made in cases such as divorce , or when a child can not be looked after by either parent and has to be put into foster care or put up for adoption . in general : decisions about who will have custody of a child will be taken in such a way as is best for the child . a child may be given state custody . this may be because the child is in danger when living at home . there may , for example , be violence in the home . when there is a divorce case , a decision will be made about whether the mother or the father has custody of the child . there may be `` joint custody '' , which means that both parents have custody over the child . physical custody means : who looks after the child from day to day . it is about where a child will live .
