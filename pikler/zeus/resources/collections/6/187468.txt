Council areas of Scotland     a council area in scotland is an area made for local government . there is a total of 32 council areas in scotland . they are all governed by unitary authorities called `` councils '' . the council areas were created on 1 april 1996 , under the provisions of the local government etc . ( scotland ) act 1994. the act calls them `` local authority areas '' but most people call them `` council areas '' . the council areas have the option of being known as a `` '' when choosing to go by a gaelic name . however , only comhairle nan eilean siar ( council of the western isles ) has chosen this option , while the highland council ( comhairle na gaidhealtachd ) uses both an english name and a gaelic name . source : 2011 census for scotland
