Chronic stress     chronic stress is stress that happens over a long period of time . it is different than acute stress , which happens over a short period of time and then goes away . stress is a normal part of every living thing 's daily life . however , when stress becomes chronic , it can damage the body and the mind . when a person ( or animal ) is stressed , the body reacts in certain ways - for example , by releasing chemicals called corticosteroids . if this happens again and again , over a long period of time , it can change the way the brain works . these changes can damage working memory spatial memory , and decision-making skills . they can also increase aggression . chronic stress can cause ( or worsen ) many different medical problems . for example , people who have had chronic stress are more likely to get hypertension ( high blood pressure ) and heart disease . related pages . - stress
