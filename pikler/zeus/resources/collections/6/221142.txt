Sessility (zoology)    sessility ( zoology ) sessile is a common term in biology ; it is the opposite of motile . it means to stay in one place on the ground . it is used mainly for marine and freshwater species . they often have larvae which are motile , float or swim , and adult forms which are stationary . many sessile forms can move from place if they need to . the key idea is that they choose a suitable place , and stay fixed as long as they can . they usually secrete some substance which holds them on .
