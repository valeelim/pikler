Ian Wallace     ian wallace ( born 10 july 1919 ; died 12 october 2009 in london ) was a british bass-baritone opera and concert singer , actor and entertainer . wallace was born in london and went to school at charterhouse . he soon became known at school as someone who was good at making people laugh . he learned to sing lots of songs and accompanied himself on the banjolele . when he wanted to study at university he applied for a choral scholarship at king ’ s college , cambridge , but he was not offered a place there , so he went to trinity hall . there he studied law and was known for the jokes that he played on people . he also had singing lessons . wallace wanted to go into music theatre , but the war broke out and he joined the army . then he was ill with tuberculosis , but it did not stop him from singing to the people in the hospital . he took a long time to get better . gradually he started working in music theatre and then he got a chance to sing in opera . his voice was a bass baritone . he sang with the new london opera company in operas such as `` la bohème '' and `` the barber of seville '' . from 1948 to 1961 , he regularly sang at glyndebourne . he usually sang comic parts ( known as “ buffo roles ) .
