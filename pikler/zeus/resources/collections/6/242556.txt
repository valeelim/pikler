Hepatocellular carcinoma     hepatocellular carcinoma is the most frequently encountered type of liver cancer . it is more common in those with cirrhosis of the liver . if localized to the liver and small at the time of diagnosis , it can be surgically treated . if metastatic , it may be treated with nexavar , an oral medication approved in the treatment of advanced hepatocellular carcinoma . liver transplantation may be used in some cases . - more information about : hepatocellular carcinoma risk factors . - aflatoxin exposure - hepatitis b or c. - cirrhosis - diabetes
