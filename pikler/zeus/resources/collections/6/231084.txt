Wrongful life     wrongful life is a legal action in which someone is sued for not stopping the birth of a severely disabled child . in this type of lawsuit a child and the parents will sue a doctor or a hospital . the doctor or hospital is sued for not giving information about the disability during the pregnancy . the doctor or hospital may have not provided all the information on a genetic case before the pregnancy . the parents argue that if the mother had known this information , she would have had an abortion , or chosen not to become pregnant at all . in the past , only parents could sue for their `` own '' damages the occurred as a result of the birth of a disabled child ( e.g. , the mother 's own pregnancy medical bills and cost of psychiatric treatment for both parents ' emotional resulting from becoming aware that their child was disabled ) . this cause of action is known as wrongful birth . but the child could not sue for his or her own damages , which were often much more in terms of money and harm .
