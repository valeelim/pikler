Sea slug     sea slug is an informal name for a group of gastropods which look similar , but are not monophyletic . their similar shape and life styles is an example of convergent evolution . the group includes : - heterobranchia - nudibranchs : a clade with bilateral symmetry - sacoglossa , a clade of heterobranch sea slugs and snails which go in for `` kleptoplasty '' ( 'stealing ' plastids ) . they eat algae and keep the chloroplasts for their own use . - opisthobranchs : a long-used term now just an informal label . polyphyletic . - sea angels , or cliones , are opisthobranch gastropod molluscs of the clade gymnosomata . - sea hares are medium to large size marine gastropod molluscs . they belong to two families of the opisthobranchia . - cephalaspidea : a sub-order of opisthobranch gastropod molluscs . they usually possess a reduced internal shell . - onchidiidae : a family of air-breathing sea slugs . - sea butterflies : a group of pelagic sea snails sometimes called pteropods . they belong to the clade thecosomata .
