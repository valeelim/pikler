Rail gauge     rail gauge is the distance from the inside of one rail on a railroad track to the inside of the other . most countries use a standard gauge of 1,435 mm ( 4 ft , 8 1/2 in ) . wider gauges are called broad gauge , smaller gauges are called narrow gauge . broad gauge may be used where the track needs to have heavy things moving on it . narrow gauge is used to build railroads for less money , because the smaller track costs less . places where different gauges meet are called break of gauge .
