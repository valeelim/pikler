Mansfield, Victoria    mansfield , victoria mansfield is a small town at the base of the victorian part of the australian alps . it is about north-east of melbourne . in 2006 there were 4052 people living in mansfield . mansfield is the centre of a large shire ; it was made to join with the nearby town of benalla away , to form delatite shire . many people did not like this change and mansfield became the first de-amalgamation in victoria . mansfield was once mainly a farming and logging town , but is now a tourist-centre . it is the support town for the large australia ski resort mt buller . it is well known for the high country tradition of alpine grazing , made famous in the movie `` the man from snowy river '' . this was made around mansfield , based on the poem by banjo paterson . mansfield , first known as mount battery , was at the boundary of a number of pastoral runs ( very large farms ) . a township was surveyed in 1851 and named after mansfield in nottinghamshire , england . people came to live in the town after the discovery of gold nearby and the post office opened on january 1 , 1858 .
