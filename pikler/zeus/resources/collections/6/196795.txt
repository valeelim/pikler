Heliantheae     heliantheae is the most well known tribe of the sunflower family ( asteraceae ) . the name comes from the greek word for `` sun flower '' . the heliantheae is the third largest tribe in the sunflower family , which has 190 genera and about 2500 species . only the tribes senecioneae and astereae are larger . most heliantheae come from north america and south america , and mexico . a few genera come from tropical areas . most heliantheae are herbs or shrubs , but there are some that grow to the size of small trees . leaves are usually hairy and arranged in opposite pairs . the anthers are usually black . heliantheae can be grown on farms . these include sunflowers and jerusalem artichokes . many garden flowers are also in this group , such as `` coreopsis '' , `` cosmos '' , `` echinacea '' , `` rudbeckia '' , and `` zinnia '' . there are also some weeds in the family . species of `` ambrosia '' ( ragweed ) produce lots of pollen . each plant can produce about a billion grains of pollen over a season , and the plant is wind-pollinated . it is one of the main causes of hay fever .
