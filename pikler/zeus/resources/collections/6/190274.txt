Prussian House of Lords     the prussian house of lords ( ) was the first chamber of the parliament of the kingdom of prussia between 1850 and 1918. the second chamber was the prussian house of representatives ( `` preußisches haus der abgeordneten '' , also called the `` abgeordnetenhaus '' ) . the house of lords was created on 31 january 1850. its seat was on leipziger straße in berlin at a building rebuilt by the architect friedrich schulze in 1904. a member of the house of lords was known as a `` pair '' , or officially as a `` member of the prussian house of lords '' ( `` mitglieder des preußischen herrenhauses '' , or mdh ) . the house consisted of hereditary peers , life peers appointed by the king of prussia , peers by virtue of position , representatives of cities and universities , etc . the majority of members were nobles , although the house also had commoners as members , especially among the representatives of cities and universities .
