Japanese dormouse     the japanese dormouse ( glirulus japonicus ) is a species of rodent in the gliridae family . it is the only species in the genus glirulus . it is endemic to japan . its natural habitat is temperate forests . in japanese it is called `` yamane '' ( 山鼠 ) . it has the special ability of running at great speed upside down , underneath branches . its main food is nectar or pollen , although nursing females will eat small insects . references . - holden m.e . 2005. family gliridae . pp . 819-841 in `` mammal species of the world a taxonomic and geographic reference '' . d.e . wilson and d.m . reeder eds . johns hopkins university press , baltimore .
