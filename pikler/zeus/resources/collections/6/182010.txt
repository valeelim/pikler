Frederick Douglass     frederick douglass ( 1818-1895 ) was an african-american in the 19th century . he was born as a slave in maryland , but learned to read and escaped to the north in the 1830s . he soon became an abolitionist ( someone who wants to end slavery ) , and worked with other abolitionists such as william lloyd garrison . he was the most powerful speaker for abolitionism . frederick also published his own newspaper `` north star '' . he wrote two books , `` narrative of the life of frederick douglass '' and `` my bondage and my freedom '' . douglass spent several years in england and ireland . during the civil war , douglass was the most famous black man in the country , and met abraham lincoln . after the war , he served as ambassador to haiti and an advocate for equal rights for african-americans .
