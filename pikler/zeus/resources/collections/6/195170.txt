Warm front     a warm front is a leading edge of an advancing mass of warm air . it separates warm air from the colder air above . warm fronts usually have stratus and cirrus clouds , but sometimes they also have cumulus and cumulonimbus clouds . before the warm front passes , there can be rain or snow . while it is passing , there is often light rain or drizzle . warm fronts move more slowly than cold fronts . on a weather map warm fronts are shown as red lines with red semicircles pointing in the direction that the front is moving .
