Celluloid     celluloid is the name of a group of compounds made from nitrocellulose and camphor , plus dyes and other agents . it was the first thermoplastic . it was first called parkesine in 1856 , then xylonite in 1869 and then `` celluloid '' in 1870. celluloid is easily molded and shaped . it was first widely used as an ivory replacement , in billiard balls for example . celluloid catches fire very easily and also easily breaks down , and is no longer widely used . its most common uses today are for making table tennis balls and guitar picks .
