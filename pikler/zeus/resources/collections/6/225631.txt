Cumulus cloud     cumulus clouds are white clouds that are dense and large . they are known to look like cauliflower . mostly , you can see easily that cumulus clouds have flat bottoms but the tops are always changing shape . seeing a cumulus cloud means that the air is well mixed by up and down vertical drafts . when a cumulus cloud grows into a thunderstorm , the cloud is called a cumulonimbus cloud . this is a sign that the air is rising to the stratosphere . other sites . - ams glossary of meteorology - cumulus cloud at bbc weather - cumulus cloud page at university of richmond internal site - time-lapse of the formation of cumulus clouds
