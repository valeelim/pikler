Skagit River     the skagit river is a river in canada and the united states . it flows about from british columbia to the state of washington , where it flows into the pacific ocean north of seattle . the river begins by flowing south . it then enters north cascades national park , where it is dammed by ross dam , which forms ross lake , a reservoir . the river then leaves the lake and flows into diablo lake , also a reservoir . it then keeps flowing southwest through a deep river canyon , known as the skagit gorge . it then meets its largest tributary , the sauk river , and then turns west . ( the sauk is about 45 miles long , flowing northwest . ) after turing west , the skagit parallels the north cascades highway , leaving the national park . it meets another tributary , the baker river , from the north . ( the baker flows south about 30 miles and is dammed twice , in baker lake and lake shannon . ) the river then flows out to the sea near mount vernon , washington . the total extent of the basin is 1,505 square miles ( 3,900 square kilometers ) .
