Petting, Germany    petting , germany petting is a village near traunstein , in bavaria . it lies on the shore of waginger see , lake waging . the village was built on what was once an island , during the last ice age . the island was known as safe , and secure , and was inhabited even at that time . according to the records , the place name means `` place where there is water , at times '' . there used to be a church in the village , around 800 ad . unfortunately , this church was destroyed by the hungarians around the year 900. there had been a castle in what was then the churchyard ; the owners of the castle can be traced back to the 6th century . it is believed that petting had already existed during the time of the romans , with the same name . the part of the village called `` ringham '' can be traced to 788 ad , when it 's mentioned by a chronicle of the bishop of salzburg . in 1970 , about 1,700 people lived in petting ; in 2000 they were about 2,200 .
