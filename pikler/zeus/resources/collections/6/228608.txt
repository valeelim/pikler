Gypsophila paniculata     gypsophila paniculata is a species of perennial plant , a plant that lives for more than two years . the common name , a name that is in general use , is baby 's breath . the height of the species 2 to 3 feet long and the width of the species is 2 to 3 feet long . the species blooms anytime from april to august . the color of the flowers are white . the plant needs sunlight from the full sun meaning direct sunlight . the species is a herbaceous plant , has leaves and stems that die down at the end of the growing season to the soil level and have no woody stem above the ground . the species is native to central europe and siberia . the species is poisonous . the poisonous parts of the plant are the flowers and the rest of the plant are poisonous when they are dry . the species can cause eye irritation , sinus irritation meaning in the nasal cavity , or asthma after the plant is repeatedly handled . the plant can also cause minor , a lesser effect , skin irritation that only lasts for a few minutes .
