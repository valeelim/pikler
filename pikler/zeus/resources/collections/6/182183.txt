Fanfare     a fanfare is a short , showy , piece of music usually played for a special event . it is often played to announce the arrival of an important person , such as a king or queen . fanfares are usually played by trumpets and other brass instruments , often with drums . the word `` fanfare '' can also mean an event with a lot of publicity , even if there is no actual music . fanfares began in the middle ages . modern movies often show trumpets playing fanfares in ancient rome but there is little evidence that this ever happened . in 18th century france , the fanfare was a piece of music with energy and repeated notes . modern fanfares come from the 19th century in britain where they were written for important state events such as the crowning of a king or queen . hubert parry wrote a fanfare , `` i was glad '' , for the coronation of edward vii . famous fanfares . - `` fanfare for the common man '' , aaron copland - `` fanfare for a new theatre '' , igor stravinsky - `` fanfare for st edmundsbury '' , benjamin britten - `` 20th century fox fanfare '' , alfred newman 1954
