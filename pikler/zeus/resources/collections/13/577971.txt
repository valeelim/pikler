Rosalyn Yalow     rosalyn yalow was an american medical physicist . yalow was born on july 19 , 1921 in new york city . yalow graduated from hunter college in 1941. she also went to a all-female college in new york called hunter college . yalow was jewish . this made it difficult to become a scientist , due to prejudice . she continued trying . yalow got a phd from the university of illinois . she became a consultant in nuclear physics at the bronx veteran ’ s administration hospital in 1947. yalow served in the position of assistant chief of radioisotope service in 1950. she was made a research professor at the sinai school of medicine in new york city in 1968. in 1973 , she was made head of solomon a. berson research laboratory at the bronx veteran ’ s administration hospital . she won the nobel prize for physiology or medicine in 1977. rosalyn yalow was the second woman ever to win the nobel prize for physiology or medicine . she shared the award with roger guillemin and andrew schally . she was famous for the creation of the radioimmunoassay ( ria ) . the ria labeled things in the blood with radioactive marks . yalow and solomon berson first used the ria in 1959. it was first used to study the insulin in blood of diabetics . this was a big advancement that helped many people with diabetes . yalow died on may 30 , 2011 .
