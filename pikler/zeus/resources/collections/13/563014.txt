Yellowstone fires of 1988     the yellowstone fires of 1988 were the largest wildfires in the history of the yellowstone national park . they began as smaller individual fires . they spread quickly out of control . the fires were made worse by increasing wind and severe drought in the western united states . the fires burned for several months . on september 8 , 1988 , the entire park was closed to all non-emergency people for the first time in its history . the arrival of cool and moist weather in the late autumn brought the fires to an end .
