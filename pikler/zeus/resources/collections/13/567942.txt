Dermot Gallagher     dermot anthony gallagher ( 1944 – 15 january 2017 ) was an irish civil servant . he served as the secretary general of the department of foreign affairs . he left office as secretary-general on 24 january 2009 and was replaced by david cooney , former ambassador to the united kingdom . he was known for serving as ireland 's ambassador to the united states and for helping establishing peace with northern ireland . gallagher was born in carrick-on-shannon , leitrim . he studied and taught at the university college dublin . his diplomatic career began in 1969. during his diplomat career , gallagher worked in london , new york city at the united nations headquarters , in brussels and in san francisco , california . he was made `` deputy chief of the cabinet '' at the european commission . in 1971 , he married his wife , maeve farrell . he was appointed ambassador to the united states in 1991 where he developed a wide range of friendships and contacts . one of these was bill clinton , then governor of arkansas . during his time as ambassador , he helped secure a u.s. visa for sinn féin leader gerry adams . after his posting in washington d.c. , he returned home in 1997 with the title of second secretary general . along with bertie ahern , gallagher wanted to establish peace with northern ireland .
