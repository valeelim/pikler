Niuean language     niuean ( niuean : `` ko e vagahau niuē '' ) is a polynesian language spoken by about 8,000 people , the majority of whom live in new zealand . there are also niuean speakers in niue , where almost the entire population of about 2,000 speak the language , the cook islands and tonga . the closest relative of niuean is tongan , while māori , sāmoan , and hawaiian are also quite close . the alphabet was written in the 19th century by christian missionaries , being influenced by the samoan writing system .
