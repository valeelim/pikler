Xeroderma pigmentosum     xeroderma pigmentosum is a rare genetic disorder . ultraviolet ( uv ) light damages the dna in skin cells . normally the dna damage is repaired . but if a person has a defective repair gene , the dna is not repaired . this disease causes the skin to be sensitive to sunlight . a person with this disorder must always stay out of the sun , or they will get a sunburn . people with this disease are often called a `` moon child '' because they can only go outside during the night . this disease is present in both genders and in all races , with one in 250,000 people living in the us having this disease . a japanese person is about six times more likely to have this disease . this disorder is found in one in 250,000 people . people with xeroderma pigmentosum are about 1,000 times more likely to get skin cancer . there is no cure for xeroderma pigmentosum . the symptoms can be minimized if you do not go outside into sunlight . fewer than 40 % of individuals with the disease survive beyond the age of 20. some xp victims with less severe cases do manage to live well into their 40s .
