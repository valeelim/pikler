Egyptian goose     the egyptian goose ( `` alopochen aegyptiacus '' ) is a member of the duck , goose , and swan family , anatidae . it lives in africa , south of the sahara and the nile river valley . egyptian geese were thought to be sacred by the people in ancient egypt . they appeared in much of their artwork . they have been raised for food and bred in parts of africa since they were domesticated by the ancient egyptians . because of their popularity as an ornamental bird , they often escape . small feral populations have become established in western europe .
