Cymmer, Rhondda Cynon Taf    cymmer , rhondda cynon taf cymmer is a village in the rhondda valley wales . cymmer is located in the lower rhondda valley about half way between treorchy and pontypridd . cymmer , which is now seen as a part of porth takes its name from the old welsh word that describes a spot where two rivers of the same name join . before coal mining , not many people lived in cymmer . cymmer was one of the first villages within the rhondda valley . the lewis colliery had many examples of the pithead gear in south wales rhondda heritage park now has the only pithead gear in the rhondda . in 1847 george insole opened his first mine in cymmer . in 1855 he opened a second pit in cymmer near the old pit . tuesday 15th july 1856 at the old pit in cymmer , there was an explosion which was britain â€™ s largest mining accident at that time . one hundred and sixty men and boys lowered down the shaft to begin their shift when the explosion took place . 114 people were killed . the explosion was caused by miners carrying open flames which lit pockets of gas .
