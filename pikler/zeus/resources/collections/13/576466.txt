Prunus virginiana     chokecherries ( `` prunus virginiana '' ) are shrubs or small trees , which grow to around 4.9 m ( 16 ft 1 in ) . their leaves are oval with coarse , sharp edges . the flowers grow about 38.1–76.2 cm ( 15–30 in ) long in late spring ( well after chokecherry leaves appear ) . the fruits are about 1 cm ( 3⁄8 in ) across . they range in color from bright red to black . they have a very astringent taste , both somewhat sour and somewhat bitter . the fruits are sweetest when they are very ripe `` berries '' ( actually drupes ) . these are dark in color .
