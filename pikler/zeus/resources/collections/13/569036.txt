Abenaki language     abenaki , or abnaki , is an endangered algonquian language of quebec and the northern states of new england . the language has eastern and western forms . they differ in vocabulary and phonology . they are sometimes considered distinct languages . eastern abenaki languages were spoken by several peoples , including the micmac , maliseet , passamaquoddy , and penobscot of coastal maine . the last known fluent speaker of eastern abenaki ( penobscot ) died during the 1990s . however , several penobscot elders still speak penobscot . there is an ongoing effort to preserve it and teach it in the local schools . only a handful of western abenaki speakers remain today .
