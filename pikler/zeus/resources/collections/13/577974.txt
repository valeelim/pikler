Emilie du Chatelet     émilie du châtelet was a french physicist . she was born on december 17 , 1706 , in paris , france . du châtelet was born into an aristocratic family . she received an excellent education . du châtelet learned science , literature , and music . she married marquis du châtelet . he lived away from paris because he had a military and political career . they had three children . du châtelet took private lessons in math from pierre-louis moreau . du châtelet had an affair with francois-marie arouet de voltaire . voltaire recognized her talents . du châtelet helped voltaire write his book , `` elements of newton ’ s philosophy '' . she had an unwanted child with voltaire when she was 42. she translated `` principia mathematica '' by isaac newton into french while she was pregnant . she died six days after giving birth . she died because of a complication in her daughter ’ s birth . it was 1749. du châtelet ’ s translation of `` principia mathematica '' is still used today . she knew that her work was not appreciated during her time . years after she died , she was recognized as a role model for girls and women . she was known for juggling a career and a family . du châtelet is still celebrated around the world for her work in newtonian studies and mathematics .
