Cockpit (sailing)    cockpit ( sailing ) a cockpit is a name for the location of the controls of a vessel . it is usually an open well in the deck of a boat outside the cabin . in modern boats a cockpit may refer to an enclosed area . smaller boats typically have an `` aft cockpit , '' towards the stern of the boat . larger vessels may provide a `` center cockpit '' with greater protection from the weather . on a recreational sailboat , the cockpit is often the safest place for the crew . in the royal navy , the term `` cockpit '' originally meant the place where the coxswain was stationed . this led to the word being used for the area near the stern of a small boat that houses the rudder controls . the midshipmen and master 's mates were later assigned to sleep in the cockpit . it was also the place where the ship 's surgeon and his mates were stationed during battle .
