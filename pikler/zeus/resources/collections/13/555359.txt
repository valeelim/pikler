Arrondissements of the Hautes-Alpes department     there are 2 arrondissements in the hautes-alpes department . the french departments , and in other countries , are divided into `` arrondissements '' , which may be translated into english as districts ( in some cases , as boroughs ) . the capital of an arrondissement is called a subprefecture . if the prefecture ( capital ) of the department is in an arrondissement , that prefecture is the capital of the arrondissement , acting both as a prefecture and as a subprefecture . arrondissements are further divided into communes . the arrondissements of the hautes-alpes are : since its creation , the hautes-alpes department has had few changes : - 1790 : creation of the department with four districts : briançon , embrun , gap and serres ; the capital was chorges but in the same year it was moved to gap . - 1800 : creation of three arrondissements : gap , briançon and embrun . - 1926 : the arrondissement of embrun was eliminated . related pages . - arrondissement of briançon - arrondissement of gap
