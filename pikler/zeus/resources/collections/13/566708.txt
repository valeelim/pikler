Tithe     a tithe , meaning a tenth-part , is money or agricultural products paid to a religious organization . a tithe can be seen as a a tax , a fee for a service or a voluntary contribution . traditionally , a title is one-tenth of a person 's income paid to the church . tithing came from the book of numbers . in ancient israel , the tribe of levi were the priests . the other tribes gave tithes to the tribe of levi because they had been given no land in canaan . under the law of moses a tenth part is given to the priests . then , the priests were required to give a tenth part of what they received as an offering to god . these tithes were paid once a year . in france , tithes were taxes levied by the roman catholic church before the french revolution . tithes were levied on the third estate ( common people ) , which made up about 98 % of the french population . the tithes were taxes for land owned by members of the third estate . the tithe was abolished after the creation of the new constitution of france in 1791 , when the constitution was completed .
