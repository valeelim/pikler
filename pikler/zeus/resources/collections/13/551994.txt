Association fallacy     an association fallacy is a logical fallacy that occurs when someone says that a quality of one thing must applies to another just because they share a similar quality or belief . it can be used in a positive or negative way . it follows the general outline , a is a b , a is also a c , therefore , all bs are cs . an example of an association fallacy being used in positive way ( called `` pro hominem '' , or honour by association ) is as follows : - citizens of country x won more nobel prizes , gold medals , and literary awards than citizens of country y. therefore , a citizen of country x is superior to a citizen of country y. an example of an association fallacy being used in a negative way ( called `` guilt by association '' ) is as follows : - john is a shoplifter . john has black hair . therefore , all people with black hair are shoplifters .
