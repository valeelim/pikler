Geoffrey Blainey     geoffrey norman blainey ( born 11 march 1930 ) is an australian historian , academic , philanthropist and commentator . blainey is known for having written texts on the economic and social history of australia , including `` '' ( 1966 ) . he has published over 35 books , including histories of the world and of christianity . other well known works include `` the peaks of lyell '' ( 1954 ) and `` a short history of the world '' ( 2000 ) . blainey was made a fellow of the royal historical society of victoria in 1967. in 1975 he was made an officer of the order of australia for his works to australian literature . he was awarded a companion of the order of australia in the australia day honours list of 2000 for his service to academia , research and scholarship . blainey was born in melbourne , victoria . he studied at the university of melbourne . blainey has often appeared in newspapers and on television . he held chairs in economic history and history at the university of melbourne for over 20 years . in the 1980s , he was visiting professor of australian studies at harvard university . he received the 1988 britannica award for dissemination of knowledge and was made a companion of the order of australia in 2000 .
