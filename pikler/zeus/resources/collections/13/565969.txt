Small-cell carcinoma     small-cell carcinoma ( also known as `` small-cell lung cancer '' , or `` oat-cell carcinoma '' ) is a type of highly malignant cancer that most commonly arises within the lung , although it can occasionally arise in other body sites , such as the cervix , prostate , and gastrointestinal tract . small cell carcinoma has a shorter doubling time , higher growth fraction , and earlier development of metastases compared to a non-small cell carcinoma .
