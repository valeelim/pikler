Hammerscale (archaeology)    hammerscale ( archaeology ) hammerscale is what is left behind after you forge iron like it was done in ancient times . it is the metal version of sawdust . hammerscale is very useful in archaeology . hammerscale looks like either flakes or spheres . the flakes can be very different in size and shape . the spheres are hollow , and often the same size . hammerscale is black , light gray , or blue . hammerscale was probably made from magnetite . however , later steps in the iron working process have given hammerscale that is more and more pure . hammerscale is used to tell what some iron was used for , how it was used , and who made it .
