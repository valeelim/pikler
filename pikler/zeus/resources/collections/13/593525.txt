Escolta Street     escolta street is historic west to east street in the downtown district of binondo in manila , the capital of the philippines . it is very near to ( and parallel to ) pasig river . it starts at plaza moraga to the west and ends at plaza santa cruz and quintin paredes street to the east . it contains some examples of early skyscraper design in the country . the name of the street was derived from the spanish word `` escoltar '' , meaning `` to escort '' or `` to accompany '' . it is known as calle de la escolta in the spanish language . it was built in 1594 and was the center of business and commerce in metro manila until the 1960s , when the center of business slowly moved to makati .
