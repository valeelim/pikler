Tigranes the Great     tigranes the great ( armenian : տիգրան մեծ , greek : τιγράνης ο μέγας ) ( ruled 95 bce–55 bce ) ( also called tigranes ii and sometimes tigranes i ) was the king of armenia his country became for a short time the strongest state in the roman east . tigranes was born around 140 bce and was the son or nephew of artavasdes i or tigranes i. tigranes the great represented the artaxiad royal house . he was involved in many battles during his reign . he had battles against the parthian , seleucid , and roman empires . related pages . - kingdom of armenia ( antiquity ) ( expanded lands massively )
