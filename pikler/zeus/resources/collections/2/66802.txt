Hepatitis A     hepatitis a is an infection of the liver caused by the hepatitis a virus . unlike the other common forms of hepatitis ( hepatitis b and c ) , it does not cause chronic ( long-term ) liver disease . when a person has hepatitis a , the virus stays in their feces . hepatitis a is usually spread by eating food or drinking water that has been contaminated with infected feces . for example , hepatitis a can be spread by : - using ice that was made from contaminated water - eating fruits , vegetables , or other foods that are not cooked , which may have gotten contaminated when a person with hepatitis a prepared them - foods that are not cooked are more likely to spread hepatitis a because cooking food will kill the virus - eating shellfish that lived in contaminated water , and were not cooked well enough to kill the virus hepatitis a can also be spread by having sex with someone who has hepatitis a. signs and symptoms . - jaundice ( yellow skin and eyes ) - abdominal pain - fever - nausea and vomiting
