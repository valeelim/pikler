Saxe-Meiningen     the duchy of saxe-meiningen was one of the ernestine duchies . the dukes were never very important in forming the history of europe , and unlike their relative in the duchy of saxe-coburg and gotha never set out to form a long lasting dynasty . like all of the german monarchies the duchy became part of the weimar republic after world war i. in the reshuffle of ernestine territories after the death of the saxe-gothaducal line in 1825 , saxe-meiningen got the territories of hildburghausen and saalfeld . the state of saxe-meiningen was merged into the new state of thuringia in 1920. the capital of saxe-meiningen was meiningen ; it had an area of 2,468 km² and a population of 269,000 ( 1905 ) . the summer residence was altenstein . the present head of head of the ducal house of saxe-meiningen is prince frederick konrad ( born 1952 ) . dukes of saxe-meiningen . - bernhard i ( 1675 – 1706 ) - ernst ludwig i ( 1706 – 24 ) , son of bernhard i - ernst ludwig ii ( 1724 – 29 ) , son of ernst ludwig i - karl frederick ( 1729 – 43 ) , son of ernst ludwig i
