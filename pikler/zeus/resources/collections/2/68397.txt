Ryūgū-jō     in japanese mythology , ryūgū-jō ( 竜宮城 ) is the palace of ryūjin , the dragon god of the sea . it is located under the sea . depending on the version of the legend , it is built from red and white coral , or from solid crystal . those that live in the palace were ryūjin 's servants , which were various creatures of the sea . on each of the four sides of the palace is a different season , and one day at the palace is like 100 years on earth . in legend , urashima tarō visits ryūgū-jō .
