Prince of Wales     prince of wales is a royal title . it is usually held by the eldest son of the king or queen of the united kingdom . the title is not automatic , but is given by the reigning monarch . when a person becomes king or queen , their eldest son immediately becomes duke of cornwall and duke of rothesay . the current prince of wales , prince charles , was born in 1948 , and was known as `` prince charles of edinburgh '' ( his father is the duke of edinburgh ) . in 1952 , his mother became queen , and he became `` the duke of cornwall '' ( in scotland he was called `` the prince charles , duke of rothesay '' ) . in 1957 , the queen made him `` prince of wales '' , and he is now called prince of wales , except in scotland , where he is still the prince charles , duke of rothesay . king george ii created his son , prince frederick , prince of wales . when frederick died , the king made frederick 's eldest son , george , prince of wales . related pages . - titles of european royal heirs
