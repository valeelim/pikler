Touch rugby     touch rugby , also named touch or touch football , is a sport which is derived from rugby league . it minimizes body contact so that rugby players practice over the preseason and improve their handling skills without any risk of injuries . it also eliminates body contact to allow inclusion of both genders and people from all ages . touch rugby was started in south sydney in the late 1960 's , pursued as a non-contact off season sport and as a fitness program for rugby league players . the game was introduced in europe in the 1980 â€™ s and the first touch league began in ireland in 2005. it is played on fields or pitches of 70 meters long by 50 meters , wide with sidelines , a half-way line and in goal areas . according to official rules , each team can have up to 14 players , with 6 players per team in the field at one time . therefore , total 12 players get to play on a field . it is possible to interchange players as many as they want . by tossing a coin , starting team is decided and an attacking team gets to have six chances to make a score . these chances are called touches .
