Saalburg     the saalburg is a roman fortification in the taunus mountains in germany and was a stronghold in the upper germanic limes . it was constructed about 90 ce and enlarged until about 210. then the romans left it after 260. it is estimated that it was home to a `` cohors equitata '' , about 500 cavalry and infantry , to protect the access to the rhine-main valley from the germanic tribes in the north . eventually it decayed , and its stones were used as a quarry . in 1897 wilhelm ii ordered that it was rebuilt . today the saalburg houses a museum and is a center for roman archeology . it is located between bad homburg and wehrheim , some 30 km north of frankfurt am main . as part of the upper germanic limes the saalburg belongs to the unesco world heritage sites since 15. july 2005. other websites . - saalburgmuseum
