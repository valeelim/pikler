Tambourin     a tambourin ( pronounced the french way ) is a piece of music which was popular in france in the 18th century . it is based on a folkdance from the provence . the music is made to sound like the kind of drum which is called `` tambourin '' in french . jean-philippe rameau wrote several tambourins in his operas . other composers who wrote tambourins include françois-joseph gossec and maurice duruflé . tambourin also means a special kind of drum from the provence . it has a long , cylindrical shape and is played with one drumstick . it is found in orchestral music such as georges bizet 's `` l'arlésienne suite no . 2 ''
