Alpine tundra     alpine tundra is a type of geological terrain . it is to be found at high enough altitude at any latitude on earth . alpine tundra lacks trees . the lower parts do not have permafrost . because of this they are generally better drained than permafrost soils . alpine tundra changes to subalpine forests below the tree line . because alpine tundra is in many different widely separated regions of the earth , there is no animal species found in all areas of alpine tundra . some animals of alpine tundra environments include the kea parrot , marmot , mountain goats , chinchilla , pika , golden eagle and snow leopard . plants include mosses , lichens and poor grasses . some dwarf trees like birch and alder grow in tundras . alpine tundra is very cold with summer temperatures on average below 10 Â°c . all types of tundra are classified as a desert . tundras are mainly found in northern areas such as siberia and the north of canada .
