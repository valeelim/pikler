Jack Dempsey     william harrison `` jack '' dempsey ( june 24 , 1895 â€“ may 31 , 1983 ) was an american boxer who held the world heavyweight title between 1919 and 1926 known as `` the manassa mauler '' , dempsey was a tremendous puncher famous for his exciting fights , many of which set financial and attendance records . he won the title by beating jess willard on july 4 , 1919. he defended the title six times before losing it to gene tunney in 1926. they had a rematch in 1927 and tunney again beat dempsey . it was during this fight where the `` long count '' took place . dempsey knocked tunney down in the seventh round . the referee would not start counting until dempsey went to a neutral corner , so it is estimated that tunney had as many as fourteen seconds to rise ( instead of the usual ten ) . dempsey retired in 1928. he fought exhibitions after his retirement , served as a commander in the coast guard during world war ii , and opened a restaurant in new york city . dempsey died in 1983. other websites . - boxing record
