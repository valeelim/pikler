Four-horned antelope     the four-horned antelope ( `` tetracerus quadricornis '' ) is a bovine even-toed ungulate . it is also called by its hindi name `` chousingha '' . it is different from other bovines because it has four horns instead of two . four-horned antelopes are about 0.60 meters high , and they weigh about 20 kilograms . they have a light yellowish-brown fur with a white underside . they have a black stripe down their forelegs . males have four horns . the back horns are about 10 centimeters long , and the front horns are up to 4 centimeters long . four-horned antelopes live in india and nepal . they live in forests . four-horned antelopes live alone . they eat grass . four-horned antelopes are hunted by trophy-hunters because of their four horns , but they are also hunted for their meat . because of the hunting and also because of habitat loss , there are now fewer than 100 four-horned antelopes .
