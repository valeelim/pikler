Maple syrup urine disease     maple syrup urine disease ( msud ) is a genetic medical disorder . it causes a problem with how the human body deals with amino acid . it is also called branched chain ketoaciduria . maple syrup urine disease affects about 1 in 185,000 infants worldwide . the most noticeable symptom of the disorder is an infant with sweet-smelling urine . infants with this disease seem healthy at birth . if the disease is not treated , the person will suffer severe brain damage and eventually die . msud is much more common in children of amish and mennonite descent . from a very early age , the condition can be recognised by poor feeding , vomiting , lack of energy , seizures , and mental health issues . the urine of infants with this disease has a very sweet odor , much like burned caramel or maple syrup . this is what gives the condition its name . other websites . - maple syrup urine disease ( overview on nlm ) - msud family support group
