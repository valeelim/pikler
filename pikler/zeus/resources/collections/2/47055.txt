Bretton Woods system     the bretton woods system was the first system used to control the value of money between different countries . it meant that each country had to have a monetary policy that kept the exchange rate of its currency within a fixed value—plus or minus one percent—in terms of gold . the international monetary fund ( imf ) was created to fight against temporary imbalances of payments . the bretton woods system was the first monetary order that organized monetary relations among independent nation-states . it set out the rules for commercial and financial relations among the world 's major industrial states . plans to rebuild the international economic system after the end of world war ii started before the war ended . 730 delegates from all 44 allies of world war ii came to bretton woods , new hampshire for the `` united nations monetary and financial conference '' . the delegates discussed and then signed the bretton woods agreements during the first three weeks of july 1944. the planners at bretton woods set up a system of rules , institutions , and procedures to regulate the international monetary system . they started the international bank for reconstruction and development ( ibrd ) ( now one of five institutions in the world bank group ) and the `` international monetary fund '' ( imf ) . these organizations became active in 1946 after enough countries had ratified the agreement .
