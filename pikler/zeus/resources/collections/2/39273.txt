Timbre     timbre is a quality of sound . it is what makes two different musical instruments sound different from each other , even when each instrument plays the same musical note . `` playing the same note '' means they have the same pitch and loudness . for instance , timbre is the difference between a guitar and a piano playing the same note at the same volume . in music , `` tone quality '' and `` color '' are synonyms for timbre . there are many attempts by music theorists to explain timbre more fully . unfortunately , their ideas depend on the reader having an advanced knowledge of musical theory . the `` acoustical society of america '' ( asa ) acoustical terminology definition 12.09 of timbre is `` that attribute of auditory sensation which enables a listener to judge that two nonidentical sounds , similarly presented and having the same loudness and pitch , are dissimilar '' . it adds `` timbre depends primarily upon the frequency spectrum , although it also depends upon the sound pressure and the temporal characteristics of the sound '' .
