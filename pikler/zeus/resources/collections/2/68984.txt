Speaker (politics)    speaker ( politics ) speaker is the title of the person in charge of some legislatures , usually the lower house . the speaker is elected by other members of the house of commons of the united kingdom , and is usually a member of the government party , but leaves his or her party because the speaker must be `` neutral '' ( not like one side more than another ) . the speaker does not vote . if the speaker wants to stand in for re-election the other parties do not oppose him to show he is neutral . the speaker ( ceann comhairle ) of the dáil éireann is neutral , but the constitution ( bunreacht ) of ireland says he does not need to stand for re-election , he is given the first seat in his constituency . the speaker is the leader of the largest party . he or she is not neutral , but votes for his party 's policies . the speaker also helps to get his party 's ideas made law . the term of the speaker of the house is two years in office .
