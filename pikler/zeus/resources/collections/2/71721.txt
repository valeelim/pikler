Digital Object Identifier     a digital object identifier ( or doi ) is a permanent way to identify an online document . this identification is not related to its current location . a typical use of a doi is to give a scientific paper or article a unique number that can be used by anyone find the location of the paper . it may also make it possible to find an electronic copy , for example , on the internet . the doi system has a doi resolution system which is used to locate where the document is . when the document is moved , the doi resolution system is updated with the new location of the document . for example , links to `` web 2.0 authorship : issues of referencing and citation for academic integrity '' by kathleen gray `` et al .
