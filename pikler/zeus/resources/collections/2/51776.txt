Mauve     `` this is an article about the colour mauve . for information on the flemish painter , see anton mauve . '' mauve ( pronounce to rhyme with `` jove '' and `` stove '' ) is a pale purple colour . it is similar to lavender and lilac . the name comes from french form of `` malva '' meaning the `` mallow '' flower . another name for this color is mallow . the first recorded use of `` mallow '' as a colour name in english was in 1611. in 1856 , an eighteen year old chemist william henry perkin was trying to make artificial quinine . an unexpected residue caught his eye . it turned out to be the first aniline dye , mauveine . meaning of mauve . - mauve became very popular in the 1890s . this decade was sometimes referred to as the `` mauve decade '' , because william henry perkin 's aniline dye allowed the widespread use of that colour in fashion . - the colour mauve became associated with homosexuality because well known figures in the art world during the 1890s -- a decade that was called the `` mauve decade '' -- were gay such as author oscar wilde and artist aubrey beardsley .
