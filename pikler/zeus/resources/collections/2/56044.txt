Naphthalene     naphthalene is a crystalline , white hydrocarbon , with a strong smell . it is best known as the main ingredient in mothballs , urinal deodorizer blocks , and can be used as an antiseptic . in mothballs , it is used as an insecticide or pesticide . naphthalene is toxic . in humans , being exposed to naphthalene can destroy red blood cells . naphthalene may also cause cancer . in the early 1820s , two different papers are published on something that matches the description of naphthalene . both groups made it by distilling coal tar . in 1821 john kidd cited both reports , and condensed their results to accurately describe the properties of naphthalene , and how to make it . kidd named it naphthalene because `` naphtha '' means any explosive hydrocarbon mixture . by 1826 , michael faraday discovered the formula for it . emil erlenmeyer proposes that it is two fused benzene rings in 1866 , and carl gr√§be confirms this three years later . other websites . - naphthalene ( pim 363 ) - mostly on toxicity of naphthalene
