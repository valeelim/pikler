Lugworm     the lugworm , or sandworm , is a large worm of the phylum annelida . lugworms make the coiled tubes of sand that are a familiar sight on a beach at low tide but the lugworm itself is not seen except by people who dig them up from curiosity or to use as fishing bait . the lugworm can grow to a size of about 9 inches ( 22 cm ) . its body is segmented , like that of an earthworm . it has bristles in the middle of its body . it is a polychaete . it also has well-developed blood-vessels , and gills to breathe in the water .
