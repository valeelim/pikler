Septum     a septum ( latin : `` something that encloses '' ; plural septa ) is a partition separating two cavities or spaces . examples include : - nasal septum : the cartilage wall separating the nostrils of the human nose.often deviated or perforated through physical injury or cocaine abuse . - cephalopod septa : walls between each chamber , or `` siphuncle '' , in shells of nautiloids , ammonites , and belemnites ; i.e . cephalopods that retain an external shell . - the wall dividing the right side of the heart from the left side . fungi produce septa to partition filamentous hyphae into discrete cells .
