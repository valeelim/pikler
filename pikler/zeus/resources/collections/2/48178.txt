Senate     in a modern democracy , a senate is a group of people who are part of a legislature . a legislature passes or changes laws for their country , state , or other area . members of a senate are called senators . some legislatures are bicameral , meaning they have two groups of people ( working in separate places ) called `` houses '' . a senate can be called a `` house of senators '' . the other house of such legislatures is called a `` house of representatives '' , house of commons , or assembly . in such legislatures , both houses must pass the same bill to make it a law . modern democratic systems having legislatures with senates include the united states of america ( u. s. ) , canada , australia and many states in the united states . the first ever senate was the roman senate . the name comes from their word for `` elder '' . senate is also the name of the ruling body of some universities .
