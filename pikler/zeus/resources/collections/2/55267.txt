Ministry (government department)    ministry ( government department ) a ministry is a department of a government , led by a political minister . ministries are usually subordinate to the cabinet , and prime minister , president or chancellor . a government will usually have several ministries , each with a specialised field of service . national ministries vary greatly between countries , but some common ones include ministry of defence , ministry of foreign affairs , ministry of finance , and ministry of health . some countries such as switzerland , the philippines and the united states do not use the term `` ministry '' for their government departments , and instead simply call them departments . in hong kong the term `` bureau '' is used . in canada , some provincial-level government departments are called `` ministries '' ( such as in ontario and british columbia ) but most are called `` departments . '' their heads are referred to as `` ministers '' in both levels of government , however . other pages . - cabinet
