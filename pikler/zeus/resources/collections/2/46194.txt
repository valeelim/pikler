Relative key     when a piece of music is in a major key , the relative minor means the minor key which has the same key signature . it can be found by taking the sixth note of the first scale and playing a minor scale starting on that note . for example : in c major the sixth note is an a. therefore a minor is the relative minor of c major ( c major and a minor share the same key signature : no sharps or flats ) . c major is called the relative major of a minor . a complete list of relative minor/major pairs in order of the circle of fifths is : related pages . - modulation ( music ) - scale ( music ) - circle of fifths
