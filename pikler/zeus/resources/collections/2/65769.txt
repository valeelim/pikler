Glasgow Coma Scale     glasgow coma scale or gcs is a scale that is used to measure the consciousness of a person . it was invented in 1974 by `` graham teasdale '' and `` bryan j. jennett '' , professors of neurosurgery at the university of glasgow . gcs is used in evaluation of patients , especially in icus . this scale consists of three tests , which are described below . a score is given for each test , and the gcs score is calculated by adding the scores given to each test . the maximum score is 15 , which means the patient is fully conscious . the minimum score is 3 , and is usually seen in patients with brain death or those in deep coma . the scale comprises three tests : eye , verbal ( talking ) and motor ( movement ) responses . there are 4 grades for this test : 1. no eye opening 1. eye opening in response to pain ( for example when his sternum is pressed firmly ) . 1. eye opening to speech ( that is , when he is called ) . 1. eyes opening by himself ( normally ) . there are 5 grades for this test : 1. no verbal response ( not talking at all ) .
