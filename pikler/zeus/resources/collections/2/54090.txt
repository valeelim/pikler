County council     a county council is the elected administrative body governing an area known as a county . this term has slightly different meanings in different countries . county councils were responsible for more strategic services in a region , with smaller urban district councils and rural district councils responsible for other activities . the new system was a major modernisation , which became necessary because of the increase of functions carried out by local government in late victorian britain . in england and wales , a county council is the local government that governs a county . county councils were introduced in 1889 in england and wales by the `` local government act 1888 '' . they took over the administrative functions of the unelected `` quarter sessions '' . the system was soon extended to scotland , by the `` local government ( scotland ) act 1889 '' , and the island of ireland by the `` local government ( ireland ) act 1898 '' . except in scotland , the areas they covered were called administrative counties and were not always the same as the traditional shire counties .
