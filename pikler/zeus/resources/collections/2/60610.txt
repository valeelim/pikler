Anomie     anomie means the absence of the usual moral or ethical values , where a person or group lacks ethical standards . in the context of society or government it may be a cause of social unrest . the word comes from greek , namely the prefix `` a- '' “ without ” , and `` nomos '' “ law ” . the original meaning of `` anomie '' was `` against or outside the law '' . in contemporary english the word `` anomie '' can mean not only normlessness but also anarchy . émile durkheim and later theorists , meant with `` anomie '' a reaction against or a retreat from the regulatory social controls of society , and is a completely separate concept from anarchy which is an absence of effective rulers or leaders . in albert camus ’ s existentialist novel `` the stranger '' , the protagonist meursault struggles to construct an individual system of values as he responds to the disappearance of the old . he exists largely in a state of `` anomie '' , as seen from the apathy evinced in the opening lines : `` “ aujourd ’ hui , maman est morte . ou peut-être hier , je ne sais pas ” '' ( “ today mother died . or maybe yesterday , i do n't know . ” )
