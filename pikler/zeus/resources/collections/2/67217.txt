Fibrous protein     fibrous proteins , also called scleroproteins , are long filamentous protein molecules . fibrous proteins are only found in animals . fibrous proteins form 'rod ' or 'wire ' -like shapes and are usually inert structural or storage proteins . they are generally water-insoluble . fibrous proteins are usually used to construct connective tissues , tendons , bone and muscle fiber . examples of fibrous proteins include keratins , collagens and elastins . fingernails and claws are made up of the common fibrous proteins , keratin .
