Islamic calendar     the islamic calendar ( ) is a calendar . it is used to determine islamic holidays . it is a lunar calendar . it has 12 months , and about 354 days . this means that each year , the calendar is about 11 days shorter than a solar calendar , like the gregorian . islamic years are often called `` hijra '' years , because the first year of the calendar when the `` hijra '' occurred , that is when muhammad went from mecca to medina . each year is therefore labelled with either `` h '' or `` ah '' . what the months are called . 1. muharram محرّم ( long form : muḥarram ul ḥaram ) 1. safar صفر ( long form : ṣafar ul muzaffar ) 1. rabi ' al-awwal ( rabī ' i ) ربيع الأول 1. rabi ' al-thani ( or rabī ' al thānī , or rabī ' al-akhir ) ( rabī ' ii ) ربيع الآخر أو ربيع الثاني 1. jumada al-awwal ( jumādā i ) جمادى الأول 1. jumada al-thani ( or jumādā al-akhir ) ( jumādā ii ) جمادى الآخر أو جمادى الثاني 1. rajab رجب ( long form : rajab al murajab ) 1. sha'aban شعبان ( long form : sha'abān ul moazam ) 1. ramadan رمضان ( or ramzān , long form : ramaḍān ul mubarak )
