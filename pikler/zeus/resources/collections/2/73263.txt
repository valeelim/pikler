Fecundity     fecundity , coming from the word `` '' , generally means the ability to reproduce . in biology and demography , fecundity is the ability to reproduce of an organism or population , measured by the number of gametes ( eggs ) , seed set or asexual propagules . fecundity is under both genetic and environmental control , and is the major measure of biological fitness . `` fecundation '' is another term for fertilisation . fecundity can increase or decrease in a population according to current conditions and certain regulating factors . for instance , in times of hardship for a population such as a lack of food , juvenile and eventually adult fecundity may decrease . in the philosophy of science , 'fecundity ' refers to the ability of a scientific theory to open new lines of theoretical inquiry . here , the original meaning is extended by analogy . other pages . - fertility
