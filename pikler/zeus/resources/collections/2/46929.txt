Corporation     a corporation is a union of natural persons that has its own legal status . this status is independent from the persons involved . because it is recognized by governments as a separate entity , the corporation must file tax returns and pay taxes and conform to state and federal law . this separation of persons and corporation gives it special powers . a corporation 's status and capacity is determined by the law of the place of incorporation . investors and entrepreneurs often form joint stock companies . therefore , the term `` corporation '' often means such `` business corporations '' . corporations may also be formed for local government ( municipal corporation ) , political , religious , and charitable purposes ( not-for-profit corporation ) , or government programs ( government-owned corporation ) . in common speech , the word `` corporation '' refers usually to limited responsibility corporation . that is a business firm where each of the partners invest a sum of money in as the capital of the corporation . they receive shares for the sum they have invested . if the company becomes bankrupt , the business partners are responsible for only the name value of their shares of the company 's debts . the partners do not use their personal money to pay for the debt .
