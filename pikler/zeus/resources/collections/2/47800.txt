LD50     the name ld is an abbreviation for `` lethal dose , 50 % '' or median lethal dose . it is the amount of the substance required ( usually per body weight ) to kill 50 % of the test population . the test was created by j.w . trevan in 1927 but has been phased out . the u.s. food and drug administration has begun to approve non-animal alternatives to ld50 , in response to research cruelty concerns and the lack of validity/sensitivity of animal tests as they relate to humans . examples . - oral ld of grain alcohol : 10.6 g/kg in young rats , 7.06 g/kg in aged rats . - oral ld of nicotine : 50 mg/kg in rats . - oral ld of table salt : 3000 mg/kg in rats - ld of delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol ( thc ) ( active ingredient found in cannabis ) : fischer rat , oral 1,270 mg/kg 1.270 - ld of smoked and inhaled cannabis or marijuana : unknown . at present it is estimated that marijuana 's therapeutic index is around 1:20,000 or 1:40,000 ( the `` 1 '' in the prior estimate is based on an `` effective dose '' of the .9 grams contained in nida-supplied marijuana cigarettes , although the potency of cannabis changes based on the genetics of the source plant ) placing its ld50 around at least 18,000g .
