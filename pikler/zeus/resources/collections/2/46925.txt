Ampere     the ampere or amp ( symbol : a ) is the standard unit of electric current . for instance , an electric current of one amp is one coulomb per second . the amp is named after andré-marie ampère who studied electromagnetism . the amp is defined using two parallel wires placed one metre apart in a vacuum . the wires are very long and very thin . one amp is defined as the current producing an attractive force of 2×10 newtons per metre of length between these wires . other websites . - the nist reference on constants , units , and uncertainty - a short history of the si units in electricity
