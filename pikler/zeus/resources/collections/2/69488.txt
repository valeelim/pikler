Saburo Sakai     saburo sakai ( , `` sakai saburō '' , august 25 , 1916 – september 22 , 2000 ) was a japanese naval pilot and fighter ace ( `` gekitsui-o '' ) of the imperial japanese navy during world war ii . he was the 4th best japanese pilot by number of enemy aircraft he shot down . he survived the war , and became a famous figure for his criticism of the war and the government of the japanese empire . he became a pacifist and a buddhist and promised that he would never again kill another living thing , even a mosquito . only months before his death , sakai told reporters that he still prayed for the souls of the chinese , american , australian and dutch pilots he had killed . he also helped microsoft design the popular computer game `` combat flight simulator 2 '' . sakai shot down 64 enemy airplanes , including a b-32 dominator on the last day of the war . other websites . - the last samurai - `` saburo sakai passed away september 22 , 2000 '' - memorial to saburo sakai - copy of sakai 's `` new york times '' obituary - excerpt from `` samurai ''
