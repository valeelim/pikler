Japanese mythology     japanese mythology is a system of beliefs that includes shinto and buddhist traditions . the shinto religion alone has a very big number of `` kami '' ( japanese for `` gods '' or `` spirits '' ) . most japanese myths , as generally known today , are based on the `` kojiki '' , `` nihonshoki '' and some other books . the `` kojiki '' or `` record of ancient things '' is the oldest known book of myths , legends , and history of japan . the `` shintoshu '' explains origins of japanese gods and goddesses from a buddhist point of view while the `` hotsuma tsutae '' has a very different version of mythology . one notable thing of japanese mythology is that it explains the origin of the imperial family , and assigned them divinity . the japanese word for the emperor of japan , `` tennō '' ( 天皇 ) , means `` heavenly emperor '' . related pages . - shinto - shinto shrine - kami - takamagahara
