Forestry Commission     the forestry commission is a uk government agency that was set up in 1918. the commission is responsible for many forests and woodlands in great britain . its job is to protect and expand britain 's forests and woodlands and increase their value to society and the environment . some of its forests are pine trees which the commission manages and carefully cuts down trees for wood and paper making . the money from this helps the commission to maintain it other forests which are not farmed but which are used for recreation , by people wanting to visit the countryside , and also to fund some scientific research into the ways to grow trees and to keep them healthy . the forestry commission looks after a lot of the forests in the list of forests in the united kingdom
