Dictator     the word dictator or despot in modern times is used to describe the absolute ruler ( other than a king ) of a country , who uses force and fear to keep himself and his friends in authority , and can effectively make laws all by himself . a country that is ruled by a dictator is called a dictatorship . the word `` dictator '' is from the roman republic where a man would be given absolute power for one year to get the country out of an emergency . some dictators gained political power by winning an election and cancelling new elections once they took power . it is not always clear whether a leader is a dictator or not . some leaders got into power by elections , but sometimes they gave false election results . kings and emperors often use force and fear too , but usually they are not called dictators . this is because those monarchs have some reason for being in power ( usually their father was king or emperor ) , but a dictator gained power himself . also , when someone is king or emperor of a country , usually there were several kings or emperors before them . a dictator often creates the job of dictator by gaining power .
