Moray eel     moray eels is a family of eel . sometimes they are also called by their latin name muraenidae . moray eels can be found all over the world . there are 200 different species in 15 genera . typically , moray eels grow to a length of about 1.5 metres . the largest known moray eel is the slender giant moray , which can reach 4 metres in length . moray eels live in coral reefs and rocky areas , at a depth of about 200m . these reefs are usually in tropical or subtropical waters . they spend most of their time inside deep cracks in rocks . they are very dangerous and have big teeth morays are carnivores . they prey on other fish , cephalopods , mollusks , and crustaceans . groupers , other moray eels , and barracudas are amongst their predators.they have razor sharp teeth which can pierce your skin .
