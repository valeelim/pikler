Albany, Western Australia    albany , western australia albany is a city on the south coast of western australia . in 2006 , there were 25,196 people living in albany . the city centre is in between two hills , mount melville and mount clarence . albany is built on king george sound , named by captain vancouver in 1791. major edmund lockyer started the first settlement here on 21 january 1827. the governor of western australia , james stirling , named the city albany in 1832 after prince frederick , duke of york and albany , the second son of king george iii .
