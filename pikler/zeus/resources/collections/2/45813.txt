Ilama (fruit)    ilama ( fruit ) ilama is a kind of fruit , and also the name of the tree that it grows on . the trees and fruits can be found in tropical central america . the name comes from the spanish tranlsation of the nahuatl `` ilamatzapotl '' . its rough translation is 'old woman 's sapote ' . the name is also applied to a similar fruit , soncoya or cabeza de negro ( `` a. pupurea '' ) which is cultivated as an alternative to the cherimoya . the soncoya is similar in size to the ilama but grey brown in color with hard bumps on the surface , and orange flesh that tastes like mango or papaw . the ilama fruit is delicious when eaten ripe . it is either eaten on the half-shell or scooped out with a tool . the ilama is usually chilled when served , it is sometimes served with a little cream and sugar to intensify the flavor , or with a drop of lime or lemon juice to bring in a tart and bitter tinge .
