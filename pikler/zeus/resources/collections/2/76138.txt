Canadian Hurricane Centre     the canadian hurricane centre ( chc ) is a canadian agency that is responsible for monitoring and tracking tropical cyclones that come near or affects canadian territory . the agency warns people about on-coming tropical cyclones and makes hurricane advisories when needed . its headquarters are in dartmouth , nova scotia . chc usually works together with its united states counterpart , the national hurricane center in miami , to coordinate the tracks and positions of all storms that show a possible threat to canada . while atlantic hurricanes are normally uncommon in canada , they do approach or strike from time to time . among recent events are hurricane bob , which struck new brunswick , in 1991 , hurricane luis which struck newfoundland in september 1995 , hurricane hortense which struck nova scotia in september 1996 , hurricane michael which struck newfoundland in october 2000 , and hurricane juan , which struck nova scotia in september 2003. other websites . - chc website - official canadian weather warnings
