Aigues-Mortes     aigues-mortes ( french for `` dead waters '' ) is a city in the south of france . it is in the languedoc-roussillon region . it was originally founded by the romans in the year 102 bc . the first records mentioning it under its current name date from the 10th century . louis ix of france rebuilt the port in the 13th century . it was the only french port of the mediterranean at that time . the city is laid out as a bastide . it was the starting point for the seventh crusade ( 1248 ) and eighth crusade ( 1270 ) . due to changes of the coastline , the city is several miles from the sea . it is linked to the sea through a canal , nowadays . it has well-preserved city walls . in 1999 , about 6.000 people lived there . aigues-mortes is about 35 km from n√Æmes .
