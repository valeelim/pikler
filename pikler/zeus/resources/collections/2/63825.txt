Fiefdom     in feudalism , a fiefdom ( also called a fief , feud , feoff , or fee ) was a property or right that an overlord gave a vassal in exchange for military service . this property , of whatever size , could be inherited by the man 's heirs . however , not only land but anything of value could be held in fee , including a political office , hunting or fishing rights or monopolies in trade . a fief included the right of possession of something but with conditions .
