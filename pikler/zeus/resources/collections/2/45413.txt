Trunk (botany)    trunk ( botany ) the trunk is the main stem of the tree . in terms of botany it means the main structural member of a tree that is directly connected to the roots and which supports the branches . the trunk is also often called the bole . the trunk is covered by the bark , which protects the tree against damage and often differs markedly from the bottom of the trunk to the top , depending on the species . the trunk , or bole , is the most important part of the tree for timber production . the tree trunk can be separated into five different layers : the heartwood , the sapwood , the vascular cambium , the inner bark , and the outer bark . other pages . - wood other websites . - inside a tree trunk from university of the western cape
