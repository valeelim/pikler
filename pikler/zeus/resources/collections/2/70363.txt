Crux     crux ( ipa : /ˈkrʊks/ , latin : cross ) , commonly known as the southern cross ( in contrast to the northern cross ) , is one of the modern constellations . it points to the directions of north , south , east and west . it is the smallest modern constellation . it was first described by the italian navigator andrea corsali in 1515. in the past , sailors used to use crux as a means of navigation . the southern cross was used on the flag flown by miners in ballarat , victoria , during the rebellion known as the eureka stockade . the southern cross flag has been used as a symbol of protest in australia since 1854 .
