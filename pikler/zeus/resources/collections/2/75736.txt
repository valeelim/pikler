Lüneburg     lüneburg is a city in lower saxony in north germany . it is about 50km southeast of hamburg . it is the capital of the district of lüneburg . about 102,660 people live there . the ilmenau river , a tributary of the elbe , flows through lüneburg . in medieval times the town was very rich due to the salt trade . salt used to be transported along the old salt route to the baltic sea from where it was sent by ship to other countries . there were salt mines near the town for a thousand years . salt mining stopped in 1980. the salt mining caused a lot of deforestation . the countryside changed into heathland which is called the lüneburger heide ( lüneburg heath ) . on may 23 , 1945 the nazi war criminal heinrich himmler committed suicide in lüneburg after he had been captured by the british army . he swallowed a potassium cyanide capsule before his interrogation could begin . lüneburg is twinned with scunthorpe , england .
