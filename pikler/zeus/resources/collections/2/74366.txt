Swordfish     swordfish ( `` xiphias gladius '' ) are large fish with a long , flat bill . they are a popular sport fish . swordfish are elongated , round-bodied , and lose all teeth and scales by adulthood . the international game fish association 's all-tackle angling record for a swordfish was a 1,182 lb ( 535.15 kg ) specimen taken off chile in 1953. they are the sole member of their family xiphiidae . the swordfish is named after its sharp bill , resembling a sword ( latin gladius ) , which together with its streamlined physique ( smooth body ) allows it to cut through the water with great ease and agility . swordfish are fast predators of other fish . the `` sword '' is not used to spear , but may be used to slash at its prey to injure the prey animal , to make for an easier catch . mainly , the swordfish relies on its slow speed and agility in the water to catch its enemy . it is undoubtedly among the slowest fish . swordfish is a particularly popular fish for cooking . since swordfish are large animals , meat is usually sold as steaks , which are often grilled . the color of the flesh varies by diet , with fish caught on the east coast of north america often being rosier .
