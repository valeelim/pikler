Governor of California     the governor of california is the highest office in the state government in the american state of california . the office of governor of california was created in 1849 , before california became a state . before it was a state , there had been six american military governors and numerous mexican governors when california was part of mexico . the current governor of california is edmund g. brown , jr. the governor is in charge of making yearly `` state of the state '' speeches to the california state government , submitting the budget , and making sure that state laws are being followed . some well known former governors include arnold schwarzenegger , george deukmejian , pat brown , and ronald reagan . powers of the governor . - the governor has the power to say no to laws he does not like ( veto ) . it can still become a law by getting a two-thirds majority in vote in both houses of the government . - law-enforcement powers include the ability to give pardons and get rid of prison sentences .
