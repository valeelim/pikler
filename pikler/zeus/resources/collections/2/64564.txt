Bezenye     bezenye is a village in the north-west of hungary . it is very close to the border with slovakia and only 9 km from the slovakian capital city bratislava . the population is about 1600. archaeologists have found things under the ground which show that the first people to lived there were old germanic tribes . later , hungarian ( magyar ) people lived there . then troops from turkey destroyed a lot of the village and the people fled . new people came to live in the area : croatians and germans . the croatians called the village `` bizonja '' and the germans called it `` palersdorf '' . `` bezenye '' is the hungarian name . it comes from a slav word `` baza '' meaning an elderberry tree . after world war ii many of the poorer germans had to go and live in germany . new hungarian families from slovakia came to live in bezenye . today bezenye is not a typical hungarian village . the people speak a mixture of hungarian , croatian , slovak and german . the roman catholic church of the blessed virgin mary has services in hungarian and croatian .
