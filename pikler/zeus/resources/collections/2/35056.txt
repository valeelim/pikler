Civil Aviation Authority     the civil aviation authority ( caa ) , is the publicly-owned organisation in charge of flying ( aviation ) in the united kingdom . it was created in 1972 , doing the tasks previously done by the department of transport . this means it is in charge of : - rules about aviation ( policy ) - licencing of both personnel and machinery - pricing of certain services ( air traffic control , landing fees , etc . ) the caa no longer provides air traffic control services ( atc ) , this responsibility was passed to a private company called national air traffic services ( nats ) in 2001 which the government owns 49 % of . some other duties have passed to the european aviation safety agency ( easa ) , such as type certification . caa-uk is part of the rulemaking group of the easa .
