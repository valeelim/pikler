Entablature     an entablature ( ipa [ ɛnˈtæblətʃə ] ; latin , `` and tabula '' , `` a tablet '' ) is a major piece of classical architecture . it is the big part of moldings and bands which sits flat above the columns , resting on their capitals ( tops ) . entablature is made of three parts , the architrave - the bottom part which runs right above the wall or columns below ; the frieze – an unmolded strip that may or may not be decorated ; and the cornice - the top part which sticks out below the pediment . the entabulature is an improvement on the older and less advanced lintel , which also sits between two posts and holds up roof rafters . related pages . - frieze
