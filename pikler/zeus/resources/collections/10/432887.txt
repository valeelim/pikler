Arrogance     arrogance ( also called hubris ) indicates strong pride or acting extremely self-important . in particular , a loss of contact with reality and overestimating one 's own competence or capability . arrogance is also associated with lack of humility and love toward oneself . examples include making oneself far more important than others or superiority . children raised around arrogance may be arrogant themselves . arrogance is usually detrimental and fosters a negative-minded culture . some sources , however , also indicate arrogance means taking and receiving pride with one 's work . arrogance is different from confidence . arrogance may be associated with insecurity . confidence has an association with improving and examining one 's own weaknesses .
