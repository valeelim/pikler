Hasty generalization     hasty generalization is an informal fallacy of generalisation by making decisions based on too little evidence or without recognizing all of the variables . in statistics , it may mean basing broad conclusions of a survey from a small sample group . a hasty generalization made from a single example is sometimes called the `` fallacy of the lonely fact '' or the `` proof by example fallacy '' . when evidence is intentionally excluded to bias the result , it is sometimes termed the `` fallacy of exclusion '' . hasty generalization may follow this pattern related pages . - blind men and an elephant - syllogism - conflation other websites . - `` list of common logical fallacies in propaganda and debate '' at gatecreepers.com - `` hasty generalization '' at fallacyfiles.org
