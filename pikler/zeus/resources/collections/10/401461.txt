Kiddush     kiddush ( ) is a blessing said by jews at the start of shabbat and holiday meals . the term is also used to refer to a light meal served in the synagogue after the long shabbat and holiday morning prayers . the word comes from a hebrew root meaning `` holy '' . there are two versions of the ten commandments in the bible . these versions use different words . the version in exodus says `` remember shabbat to keep it holy . '' the version in deuteronomy says `` guard shabbat to keep it holy .
