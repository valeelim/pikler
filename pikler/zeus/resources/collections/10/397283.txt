Hop (networking)    hop ( networking ) computer networks route data packets between a source and a destination . in most cases , the source of a packet is not directly attached to the destination , the packet needs to travel through other stations in between the two . each time a packet travels through a router , or a gateway it takes one hop . to prevent network congestion , the maximum number of hops a packet can take is limited . with ipv4 , this count is known as time to live , with ipv6 it is called hop count . each time the packet takes one hop , the count is . when it reaches zero , the packet is if it has not reached its destination .
