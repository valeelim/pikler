Aescwine of Wessex     aescwine was a king in wessex from about 674 to 676. he was one of several in wessex at the time . aescwine was the son of cenfus , who also ruled wessex as a subking . they both claimed descent from cynric through his son ceolwulf . after the death of cenwalh the underkings took over wessex and ruled it for about ten years . according to the anglo-saxon chronicle cenwalh was succeeded as ruler by his wife seaxburh . she ruled for about one year trying to hold central control over wessex . in 675 , aescwine defeated wulfhere over territory north of the thames . aescwine reigned from about 674 to 676. he was succeeded by centwine . other websites . - britannia : kings of wessex
