Medullary ray     in botany , medullary rays ( pith rays or wood rays ) are sheets or ribbons extending vertically through the tree perpendicular to the growth rings . the cells in each ray are elongate and horizontal . these are living cells , unlike the dead cells of the wood xylem . the rays allow the radial transmission of sap . if wood is cut into boards with the growth rings roughly perpendicular to the face of the board , the medullary rays often produce beautiful `` figure '' such as silver grain , medullary spots , pith flecks , etc .
