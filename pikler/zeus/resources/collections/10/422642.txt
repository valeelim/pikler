Tommy Flowers     tommy flowers mbe ( thomas harold flowers , 22 december 1905 â€“ 28 october 1998 ) was a british engineer . during world war ii , flowers designed colossus , the world 's first programmable electronic computer , to help solve encrypted german messages . the first mark 1 , with 1500 vacuum tube valves , ran at dollis hill in november 1943 , and then at bletchley park in january 1944. a mark 2 redesign with 2,400 valves had begun before the first computer was finished . the first mark 2 colossus went into service at bletchley park on 1 june 1944 , and immediately produced vital information for the imminent d-day landing . flowers had a crucial meeting with dwight d. eisenhower and his staff on 5 june . a courier handed eisenhower a note summarizing a colossus decrypt . this confirmed that hitler wanted no additional troops moved to normandy , as he was still convinced that the preparations for the normandy invasion were a diversionary feint . eisenhower announced to his staff , `` we go tomorrow '' .
