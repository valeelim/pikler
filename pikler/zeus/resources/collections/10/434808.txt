Aberdeen, Hong Kong    aberdeen , hong kong aberdeen is a town and area in hong kong . aberdeen is in the southern district on hong kong island . aberdeen usually refers to the town but it can also be used to refer to the areas wong chuk hang , ap lei chau and tin wan . the population of aberdeen was 163,459 in 2011. aberdeen harbour is the harbor between the town and ap lei chau . it is a popular tourist spot and is one of the nine harbors in hong kong . aberdeen is also known for its floating village which has around 600 junks and 6,000 people living there . the town was named after george hamilton-gordon , 4th earl of aberdeen ; the former prime minister of the united kingdom from 1852 to 1855 .
