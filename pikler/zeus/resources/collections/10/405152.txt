Over the River and Through the Wood     `` over the river and through the wood '' is a thanksgiving poem by lydia maria child . it appeared in the second volume of her `` flowers for children '' in 1844. the original title of the poem is , `` the new-england boy 's song about thanksgiving day '' . boston college writes : `` starting with the familiar line , `` over the river and through the wood '' , this poem is easily child ’ s most famous work . child never revised the poem herself , but the verses changed over time , especially when they were set to music.with its bouncing rhythms and high spirits , the poem draws on the writer ’ s childhood memories of visiting her grandfather ’ s house on thanksgiving . having gained notoriety for her ideas about race , child kept her antislavery ideals out of this volume in an effort to avoid controversy and boost sales . `` flowers for children '' contains only two stories involving race , and they both avoid addressing racial prejudice head on . in this nostalgic poem , child takes us back to the simple pleasures of a family holiday .
