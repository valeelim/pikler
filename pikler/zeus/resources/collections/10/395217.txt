Memorial for the Jewish Soldiers in the Soviet army in World War II     the memorial for the jewish soldiers in the soviet army in world war ii is a memorial dedicated to the jews in the red army who died in world war ii . the memorial is in the national military and police cemetery in mount herzl in jerusalem . it is next to the memorial to those who served in the polish army . the memorial was started by the organization of veterans of the red army in world war ii . it was built in 1989 with a design of the architect yizhak rachelin and the engineer `` ze'ev kariv '' . the memorial is a wall with pink stones and red stones . it has the number `` 20,000 '' for the fallen jews of the russian army . there is a hebrew bible verse inscribed from judges 3:2 : the lord `` did this to teach warfare to the descendants of the israelites . '' in front of the memorial there are stairs going to ceremonial plaza between two boards with the sign of the years `` 1941 '' and `` 1945 '' . other websites . - jews who died fighting in red army in the `` jewish daily forward ''
