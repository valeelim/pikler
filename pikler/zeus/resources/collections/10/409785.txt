B2 disability sport classification     b2 is a group to allow athletes with different vision to compete against each other in sports . b2 is a group to allow athletes with different vision to compete against each other in sports . the international blind sports association ( ibsa ) says b2 athletes are : `` from ability to recognise the shape of a hand to a visual acuity of 2/60 and/or visual field of less than 5 degrees . '' the canadian paralympic committee says b2 athletes havve `` up to approximately 3-5 % functional vision . '' b2 is used in other sports other than sports run by the ibsa . other sports using b2 are blind golf . blind golf rules say b2 golfers include `` from ability to recognise the shape of a hand up to visual acuity of 2/60 or visual field of less than 5 degrees '' . b2 is equal to other disability classifications for other sports . in adaptive rowing , the similar b2 class is lta-b2 . in horse riding , grade 4 is the similar group to b2 . for swimming , the similar disability group for b2 is s12 .
