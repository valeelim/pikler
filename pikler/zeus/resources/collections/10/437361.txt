Apomixis     apomixis is reproduction where only one parent passes genes to the offspring . apomictically produced offspring are genetically identical to the parent ( clones ) . the method is most seen in plants , especially flowering plants . brambles ( blackberry ) are apomictic . some authorities give `` reproduction by special tissues without fertilisation '' , or `` asexual formation of a seed from the maternal tissues of the ovule , avoiding meiosis and fertilization , leading to embryo development '' . apomixis includes some methods which are more common in animals , such as parthenogenesis . in botany , apomixis was defined by hans winkler as the replacement of the normal sexual reproduction by asexual reproduction , without fertilization . horticulture methods , such as propagation from cuttings or leaves , is not apomixis . natural replacement of the seed by an asexual method is apomixis .
