Dialectical behavior therapy     dialectical behavior therapy ( dbt ) is a form of psychotherapy developed by marsha m. linehan , a psychology researcher at the university of washington . linehan originally developed dbt to help people with borderline personality disorder ( bpd ) . dbt uses cognitive-behavioral techniques to get patients to control their emotions and test what reality is . it also tests the patient 's tolerance for distress , and tries to get them to accept their situation and be aware of their feelings . linehan 's ideas about how to be aware of your feelings come from buddhist meditation . dbt may be the first therapy that has been shown to be effective in treating bpd . it can also help patients with spectrum mood problems , including self-injury . dbt may help victims of sexual abuse and people addicted to drugs .
