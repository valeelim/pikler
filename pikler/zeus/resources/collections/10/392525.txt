Thomas Elder     sir thomas elder ( august 5 , 1817â€”march 6 , 1897 ) was a scottish businessman who moved to south australia and set up the largest wool selling company in the world . elder came to south australia in 1854. he worked with his brother george , and then became a business partners with edward stirling , robert barr smith and john taylor . they spent money setting up the copper mines at moonta and wallaroo which made them all very rich . when stirling and taylor retired , elder and smith became elder smith and company . this company became the biggest wool selling company in the world . elder spent money buying large properties for sheep in outback south australia , queensland and western australia . some of these included paratoo , umberatana , mount lyndhurst , blanchewater and beltana . he had ridden camels while on holiday in palestine , and thought they would be good for use in the australian deserts . he brought 124 camels and afghan people to breed camels at beltana . these were used for the building of the australian overland telegraph . elder provided money and camels for exploration by peter egerton warburton ( 1872-73 ) , ernest giles ( 1875 ) , john ross ( 1874 ) , lewis ( 1875 ) , and the royal geographical society of south australia ( 1891 ) .
