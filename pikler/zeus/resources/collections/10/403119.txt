Guillain–Barré syndrome     guillain–barré syndrome ( gbs ) , sometimes landry 's paralysis or guillain–barré–strohl syndrome is a syndrome that causes weakness of the feet and hands . guillain–barré syndrome is rare , at 1–2 cases per 100,000 people annually , but is the most common cause of acute non-trauma-related paralysis . the syndrome is named after the french physicians georges guillain and jean alexandre barré , who described it in 1916 .
