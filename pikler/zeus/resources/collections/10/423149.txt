Aircraft Apprentice Scheme     the aircraft apprentice scheme was a training programme in the royal air force . it lasted from 1920 to 1966. the training programme was for aircraft ground crew ( those who servicing the aircraft on the ground , as opposed to the pilots ) . the apprentice scheme was the result of the royal air force 's decision to open schools of technical training . raf halton was chosen as the home for the training of men and boys . the school of technical training ( for boys ) was not formed as a separate unit until 1919 and only became a permanent establishment on 1 january 1920. in march of that year , raf halton was named no . 1 school of technical training . no . 2 school of technical training at raf cranwell was created because there was not enough accommodation to house the boys at raf halton .
