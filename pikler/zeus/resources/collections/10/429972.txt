Korean Broadcasting System     korean broadcasting system ( kbs ; hangul : 한국방송공사 ; hanja : 韓國放送公社 ; hanguk bangsong gongsa ) is one of the public broadcasting , which was established in 1927. kbs headquarters are located in the yeouido district of seoul . it is one of the biggest south korean television networks . the purposes of kbs can be divided into 3 part . first of one is making good broadcasting culture . second one is creating national culture . third one is globalization of korean culture . kbs commenced the country 's first radio broadcasting service in 1927 as kyeongseong broadcasting corporation ( jodk ) . after liberation , it changed the name to seoul central broadcasting station in 1947 and launched korea 's first television broadcasting service in 1961. a system of public broadcasting was established in 1973. for reinforcement of public management , 1tv and radio1 have n't run a commercial since october 1994. first broadcasting hd programs in 2001 , kbs completely transferred to digital broadcasting in 2012. in addition to 18 regional stations and 11 overseas branches , there are eight subsidiary companies such as kbsn , kbs business and kbs media , which manage kbs content .
