Darwin–Wedgwood family     the darwin–wedgwood family is actually two inter-related english families , descended from the prominent 18th century doctor , erasmus darwin , and josiah wedgwood , founder of the pottery firm , josiah wedgwood and sons , the most notable member of which was charles darwin . there were a number of first and second cousin marriages between the two families : charles darwin married his cousin emma wedgwood . the family contained at least ten fellows of the royal society and several artists and poets ( including the composer ralph vaughan williams ) . the wedgwoods were extremely important in british industry in the west midlands . as quakers , they were all anti-slavery , and so were the darwins . the families were also closely related to the banking family of francis galton . galton was one of charles darwin 's cousins .
