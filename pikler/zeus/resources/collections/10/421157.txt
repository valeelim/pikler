Consanguinity     consanguinity ( `` blood relation '' , from the latin `` consanguinitas '' ) means being of the same kinship as another person . consanguinity also means being descended from the same ancestor as another person . the laws of many jurisdictions set up degrees of consanguinity when prohibiting sexual relations and marriage . rules of consanguinity are also used to determine heirs of an estate . these are according to laws that govern intestate succession . these vary from jurisdiction to jurisdiction . most cultures have prohibited marriages between individuals who are closely related by blood ( consanguinity ) . there were some exceptions . brothers and sisters , as well as fathers and daughters , often married in ancient egypt . these were primarily limited to the royal family from the earliest times . some theories hold that it strengthened a king 's claim to the throne . in biblical history , abraham married his ( half- ) sister sarah . most early societies had rules or laws that limited consanguineous marriages . so the dangers of inbreeding must have been known to them .
