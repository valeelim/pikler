Mantarur     mantarur is a hilly area in the southwest of the northern territory , australia . it forms the southeastern end of the olia chain of mountains . this outcrop includes butler 's dome ( ; 1102 metres above sea level ) . the granite escarpment called foster 's cliff is nearby to the east . mantarur is located about southwest of kata tjuá¹¯a . it is on pitjantjatjara land , part of the petermann aboriginal land trust . a family outstation was established here in the 1980s , at a rockhole in the western slopes . it has since been abandoned . frank rees george led a prospecting expedition through the area between 1905 and 1906. george gave names to several of the landforms , including butler 's dome , which is believed to have been named after richard butler , who was a minister of south australia . he named foster 's cliff after r. w. foster , the south australian commissioner of public works . the expedition found traces of gold at foster 's cliff , but not enough of it to warrant mining . it was the only place in the petermann ranges found to contain any gold .
