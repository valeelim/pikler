Channel drift     channel drift , also called network decay , is when a television channel moves away from its original shows to get a new audience , or to get higher ratings . it often involves a move from shows aimed at viewers that know a lot about the channel 's subject to shows aimed at a wider audience . most examples of channel drift have a channel add infotainment or reality shows to their schedule . a well-known example of channel drift is mtv . when it started in 1981 , mtv was a channel that showed music videos . in the 1990s , it started adding entertainment and reality shows aimed at a young adult audience . this was the start of a move towards the channel 's current focus on shows aimed at adolescents and young adults . other websites . - network decay - tv tropes wiki
