Monarchy of Pakistan     the monarchy of pakistan was the form of government of the dominion of pakistan from 1947 to 1956. under this system , pakistan was an independent commonwealth realm with a hereditary , constitutional monarch as the head of state . the monarch was the monarch of the united kingdom , and was also shared with a number of other countries . the role of the monarch was defined under the constitution . most of the monarch 's duties were carried out by the governor-general of pakistan . the monarchy of pakistan was created by the indian independence act of 1947. this act , made by the parliament of the united kingdom , divided its british india into two new countries : india and pakistan , each of which became an independent constitutional monarchy . succession to the throne of pakistan was , like that of the united kingdom , governed by the act of settlement 1701. pakistan adopted a new constitution on 23 march 1956. this constitution abolished the monarchy , making pakistan a republic in the commonwealth of nations . pakistan left the commonwealth in 1972 , but rejoined in 1989 .
