United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change     the united nations framework convention on climate change is an international treaty , that was signed in 1993. it wants to slow the speed at which the climate of the earth changes . it also wants to avert the risk of a climate change that is harmful to mankind . 154 countries signed the treaty in 1992 in new york . today , 194 countries are member of the treaty . these countries meet once a year to agree on politics against global warming . other page . - intergovernmental panel on climate change - kyoto protocol
