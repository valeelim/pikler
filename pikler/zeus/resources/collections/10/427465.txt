House of Orléans     the house of orléans was a french noble and royal family . it descends from louis xiv of france and his only brother philippe , duke of orléans . the families main traditional home was the palais royal in paris . the family later became very powerful during the reign of louis philippe i. he ruled france under the title `` king of the french '' . the king successfully married his children into other royal families of europe . the present head of the house is prince henri of orléans . he is known as the count of paris . he also claims the title king of the french , but is known simply as `` his royal highness the count of paris '' and sometimes the `` duke of france '' . however , the titles are not officially recognised because france is no longer a monarchy . henri has been a claimant to the throne since 1999. the count was married to duchess marie thérèse of württemberg . she is herself a daughter of a one-time claimant to the throne of württemberg .
