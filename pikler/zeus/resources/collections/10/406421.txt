Louis Antoine Godey     louis antoine godey ( june 6 , 1804 – november 29 , 1878 ) was founder and publisher of the 19th-century women 's magazine , `` godey 's lady 's book '' . he was born in poverty in new york city , new york . his parents were survivors of the french revolution . he was mostly self-educated . godey went to work as a paperboy at age 15 , moved to philadelphia , pennsylvania , and went to work for the `` daily chronicle '' . within a few years , he began publishing his own magazine , `` the lady 's book '' . contributors included harriet beecher stowe , edgar allan poe , and nathaniel hawthorne . godey also published `` the young people ’ s book '' in 1841 , and the `` lady ’ s musical library '' in 1842. on august 31 , 1833 , godey married maria duke . they had five children . he retired to st. augustine , florida . he died after a short illness in philadelphia in 1878. references . - godey , louis antoine
