Kowtow     kowtow , which is borrowed from `` koutou '' in mandarin chinese , is a tranditional prostration common in eastern asia . kowtow means kneeling and knocking one 's head onto the ground . kowtow is used in showing respect to parents or gods , or as a form of punishment by the parents when one has done something wrong . in the qing dynasty of china , the main usage of kowtow was to show respect and loyalty to the king . nowadays , people seldom kowtow and bow to others instead . however , in some chinese weddings , the couple kowtow to the parents . at some schools in taiwan , students still have to kowtow to thank their teachers .
