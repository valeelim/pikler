Theory of the Earth     theory of the earth was a publication by james hutton which laid the foundations for geology . in it he showed that the earth is the product of natural forces . what could be seen happening today , over long periods of time , could produce what we see in the rocks . this idea , uniformitarianism , was used by charles lyell in his work , and lyell 's textbook was an important influence on charles darwin . hutton recognized that rocks record the evidence of the past action of processes which still operate today . he also anticipated natural selection , as follows : hutton 's prose hindered his theories . john playfair in 1802 restated of huttton 's geological ideas in clearer english . however , he left out hutton 's thoughts on evolution . charles lyell in the 1830s popularised the idea of an infinitely repeating cycle ( of the erosion of rocks and the building up of sediment ) . lyell believed in gradual change , and thought even hutton gave too much credit to catastrophic changes . hutton 's work was published in different forms and stages :
