British Rail Class 10     the british rail class 10 diesel locomotive was a variation on the class 08 diesel-electric shunter . a blackstone diesel engine was fitted instead of one made by the english electric company . traction motors were by either the general electric company plc ( gec ) or british thomson-houston ( bth ) . the locomotives were built at the br works in darlington and doncaster over the period 1953-62. early batches were classified d3/4 ( those with gec motors ) and d3/5 ( those with bth motors ) .
