Black-necked crane     the black-necked crane ( `` grus nigricollis '' ) , also known as tibetan crane , is a large bird and medium-sized crane . it is 139 cm ( 55 in ) long , it has a 235 cm ( 7.8 ft ) wingspan and weights 5.5 kg ( 12 lbs ) . it is whitish-gray crane with a black head , red crown patch , black upper neck and legs , and white patch to the rear of the eye . it has black primaries and secondaries . both sexes are similar . in kashmir , it is the state/regional bird of the disputed territory .
