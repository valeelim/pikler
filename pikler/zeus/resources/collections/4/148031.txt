British Rail Class 315     british rail class 315 alternating current ( ac ) electric multiple units ( emu ) were built by brel at york works from 1980 to 1981. they were the fifth and final variety of british rail 's then-standard 1972 design for suburban emus , which eventually encompassed 755 vehicles and five classes ( class 313/314/315/507/508 ) . like the glasgow-based class 314 units , they are only able to operate from 25 kv overhead line current collection . the fleet works inner-suburban services on the great eastern main line run by the train operating company tfl rail out of london liverpool street . class 315s also work services from liverpool street to chingford , enfield town and cheshunt operated by london overground .
