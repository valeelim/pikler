Salmonella     salmonella is a genus of bacteria . it is a major cause of illness throughout the world . the bacteria are generally passed on to humans by eating or drinking food of animal origin which has the bacteria in it , mainly meat , poultry , eggs and milk . bacteria from the genus `` salmonella '' can cause diseases , such as diarrhea , cholera and typhus . these bacteria are zoonotic , meaning they can infect both animals and humans . `` salmonella '' is closely related to the `` escherichia '' genus and are found worldwide in cold- and warm-blooded animals ( including humans ) , and in the environment . they cause illnesses like typhoid fever , paratyphoid fever , and foodborne illness . `` salmonella '' is also extremely dangerous , and like most diseases , weaker humans like the old and young could easily die from it . `` salmonella '' can only be killed in food by cooking at high temperatures . this is called denatured .
