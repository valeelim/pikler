Copepod     copepods ( meaning `` oar feet '' ) are small , shrimp-like crustaceans that swim in seas , lakes , and ponds . copepods are very important in the food web , and many animals eat them . there are 10 orders of copepods and over 4500 species ; a few orders are free-swimming , but many are parasites ( of fish ) . the free-swimming copepods move through the water in jerky motions by moving their swimming legs .
