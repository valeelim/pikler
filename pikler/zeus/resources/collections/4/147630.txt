Symphonie Fantastique (Berlioz)    symphonie fantastique ( berlioz ) the symphonie fantastique is a symphony written by the french composer hector berlioz . it is one of the most famous romantic works for orchestra . the official title of the piece is episode de la vie d ’ un artiste ( an episode in the life of the artist ) , but it is always called by its subtitle `` symphonie fantastique '' which means `` fantasy symphony '' . the “ fantasy ” refers to the story that is described by them music . ( `` fantasy symphony '' is a better translation than `` fantastic symphony '' because `` fantastique '' is not like the modern meaning of the english word `` fantastic '' ) . buddyy the symphony lasts about 45 minutes and is divided into 5 movements . berlioz himself wrote down the story that the music describes , just as beethoven had done with his `` sixth symphony '' . berlioz ’ s work is about a young artist . in the music the young artist is represented by a tune . this tune is often heard during the symphony . that is why it is called an “ `` idée fixe '' ” , which means a “ fixed idea ” , i.e . an idea that keeps coming again and again . an `` idée fixe '' is what wagner would have called a leitmotif ( a tune which is always used to describe a particular person or thing in a piece of music ) . the first performance took place at the paris conservatoire in december 1830. berlioz made several changes to the music between 1831 and 1845 .
