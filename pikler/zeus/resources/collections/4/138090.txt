Permutation box     in cryptography , a permutation box ( or p-box ) is a method of bit-shuffling used to permute or transpose bits ( rearrange or swap the order of bits ) across s-boxes inputs , maintaining diffusion while transposing . in block ciphers , the s-boxes and p-boxes are used to make the relation between the plaintext and the ciphertext difficult to understand ( see shannon 's property of confusion ) . related pages . - s-box - substitution cipher
