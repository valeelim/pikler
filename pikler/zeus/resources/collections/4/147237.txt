British Rail Class 112     the class 112 dmus used the standard cravens body used for class 105s but had a single rolls-royce engine of 238 hp per car , and they were all formed into 'power twins'- two car sets with both vehicles powered . there were two batches built , the first 50 vehicles ( 25 sets ) had standard mechanical transmission via a gearbox and were allocated the class 112. the second batch of 50 cars ( 25 sets ) had hydraulic transmission , and became class 113s . the cars were built for services in the lmr central division and in the liverpool - st helens area , where the gradients in the lancashire & yorkshire area required more power . both types also spent some time working from cricklewood . the gross weight of a set with all seats occupied was approximately 70 tons , giving 6.8 hp per ton . empty , it was 8.1 hp/ton , which compared favourably with 5.7 hp/ton that the cravens power/trailer had .
