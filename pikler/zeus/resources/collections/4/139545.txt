Vaucluse     the vaucluse ( ) is a department in the southeast of france in the provence-alpes-côte d'azur region . its prefecture is avignon . it is named after a famous spring , in the `` commune '' fontaine-de-vaucluse . the name vaucluse comes from the latin `` vallis clausa '' ( `` closed valley '' ) as the valley here ends in a cliff from which rises a spring . vaucluse was not one of the 83 french departments made during the french revolution , on 4 march 1790. it was created on 12 august 1793 with four districts : apt , orange , ouvèze ( carpentras ) , vaucluse ( avignon ) ; its capital was avignon . in 1800 , with the creation of the `` arrondissements '' in france , the districts were changed into `` arrondissements '' : avignon , apt , carpentras and orange . after the battle of waterloo ( 18 june 1815 ) , the department was occupied by the austrian army from june 1815 to november 1818. on 10 september 1926 , the `` arrondissement '' of orange was eliminated and the subprefecture of apt was moved to cavaillon but went back to apt in 1933 .
