Ferdinand Lassalle     ferdinand lassalle ( 11 april 1825 — 31 august 1864 ) was a german jurist and socialist politician . lassalle was born in breslau . his father sent him to a business school in leipzig . lassalle changed to the university of breslau . he later studied in berlin . he studied philosophy , philology ( latin and ancient greek ) and law . lassalle took part in the revolutions of 1848-49. he was put into prison and later banned from berlin . he lived in düsseldorf that time . in 1859 , lassalle returned to berlin . lassalle was a liberal politician in the early 1860s . the liberals had some struggles with otto von bismarck . lassalle thought about the living conditions of the working class . he found that the idea of self-help would not help the working class people . this led him to become a member of the communist league . there he was in opposition to karl marx and friedrich engels . lassalle founded the allgemeiner deutscher arbeiterverein ( general german workers ' association , adav ) in 1863. this party later became the social democratic party of germany ( spd ) .
