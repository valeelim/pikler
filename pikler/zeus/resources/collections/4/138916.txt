Feathertail glider     the feathertail glider ( `` acrobates pygmaeus '' ) , also known as the pygmy gliding possum , pygmy glider or flying mouse , is the world 's smallest gliding mammal . it is named after its long feather-shaped tail . although only the size of a very small mouse ( 65 to 80 mm and 10 to 14 g ) , it can leap and glide long distances from tree to tree , up to 25 metres . like other gliding mammals , the feathertail glider has a skin membrane between the fore and hind legs ; thicker than that of the other marsupials like the sugar glider , but smaller in proportion , extending only between the elbows and knees .
