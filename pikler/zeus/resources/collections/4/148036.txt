Nehebkau     in egyptian mythology , nehebkau ( also spelled nehebu-kau , and neheb ka ) guarded the entrance to duat , the egyptian underworld . he was made up of ka and ba , the two parts of the soul . he is pictured as a snake with two heads . atum was said to have to keep his finger on nehebkau to keep him from being out of control . since he was a snake , he was believed to heal those bitten by snakes and/or scorpions .
