Piranha     the piranha ( also known as the caribe ) is a ferocious , schooling , fresh-water fish . it is native to warm lowland streams and lakes in south america , east of the andes mountains . piranhas have been introduced to other places , including northern brazil , hawaii , and parts of central and north america . there are many species of piranha ; they belong to the genera `` pygocentrus '' and `` serrasalmus '' . they reproduce by laying eggs . piranhas are opportunistic carnivores ( flesh-eaters ) . they eat aquatic and land animals that are in the water . some of the prey includes fish , mollusks , crustaceans , insects , birds , lizards , amphibians , rodents , and carrion ( dead meat that they find ) . these fish are diurnal ( most active during the day ) . many animals prey upon piranhas ( especially young piranhas ) , including other piranhas , caimans , water snakes , turtles , birds , otters , and people .
