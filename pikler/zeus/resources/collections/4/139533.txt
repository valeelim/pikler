Malemort-du-Comtat     malemort-du-comtat is a commune of 1,203 people ( 1999 ) . it is in the region provence-alpes-côte d'azur in the vaucluse department in the south of france . during the time of the roman empire , the village was called `` calvia . '' the name malemort means `` bad death '' and was probably used after the destruction of the village by the franks . comtat means `` county '' , the land belonging to a count . wine making has been carried out in region for at least 2,400 years . when wine making started again in medieval times , the archbishop of carpentras gave his monks a vineyard in malemort du comtat in 882. the village is in the mountains , surrounded by olive and cherry trees . the city was protected by high walls and eleven towers which were built in the 12th century . three of the gateways in the wall are still standing . the church , notre dame de l ’ assomption , was built in the 13th century .
