Neptunium     neptunium is a chemical element that has the symbol np on the periodic table . it has the atomic number 93 which means it has 93 protons and electrons in its atoms . it is named after the planet neptune in the same way as uranium is named after the planet uranus . neptunium was discovered in the year of 1940 by two men named edwin mcmillan and phillip h. abelson . neptunium is a silvery- metallic element and is radio-active . its melting point is 637 degrees celsius and it 's boiling point is 4000 degrees celsius .
