White-collar crime     white-collar crime refers to crimes committed by high-ranking officials , nobles and people of high status . noble warriors , especially young bachelor knights who were not assigned any tasks , were often involved in it , which is also known as `` fur-collar crime '' . it is the commission of theft , plunder , and pillage because they were bored and out of money . being members of the nobility , crimes committed against a helpless peasantry often went unpunished during the 15th century in western europe .
