Maharaja     the word mahārāja ( also spelled maharajah ) is sanskrit for `` great king '' or `` high king '' . many languages of india have borrowed the word 'maharaja ' , there languages include punjabi , bengali , hindi and gujarati . its use is mainly for hindu rulers . a female ruler is known as maharani ( or maharanee ) or great queen . she can be the wife of a maharaja or a ruler herself . in 1971 , the government of indira gandhi abolished the titles and money for all indian rulers . however some people still claim such titles . before independence in 1947 , india and pakistan contained more than 600 princely states each with its own ruler . some states called the ruler raja or thakur ( if the ruler were hindu ) or nawab ( if he was muslim ) ; there were many other titles as well .
