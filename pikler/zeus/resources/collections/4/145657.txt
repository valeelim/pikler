Gossypium sturtianum     `` gossypium sturtianum '' , commonly called `` sturt 's desert rose '' , is the floral emblem of the northern territory . it was named after explorer charles sturt who found the plant in creeks near the site of broken hill , new south wales in 1844. he collected the seeds and brought them back for robert brown ( 1773-1858 ) , a scottish botanist , to study . a stylized drawing of the flower is used on the northern territory 's flag .
