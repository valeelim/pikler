British Rail Class 86     the british rail class 86 was the standard electric locomotive built during the 1960s , developed as a result of testing with the earlier classes 81 , 82 , 83 , 84 and 85. one hundred of these locomotives were built from 1965-1966 by either english electric at vulcan foundry , newton-le-willows , or british rail ( br ) at their doncaster works . the class was built to haul trains on the then newly electrified west coast main line , from london euston , to birmingham , crewe , manchester piccadilly , liverpool and later preston and glasgow . they helped to replace steam locomotives , which were finally withdrawn by br in 1968 .
