Borodin Quartet     the borodin quartet is a string quartet that was founded in 1945 in what was then the soviet union . the four players have not always been the same people : there have been some changes to the group , but it is one of the world 's longest lasting string quartets . in 2005 they celebrated their 60th anniversary . until he retired in 2007 the cellist valentin berlinsky had almost always been the cellist of the quartet . when they started they were known as the moscow philharmonic quartet . in 1955 the quartet changed its name to borodin quartet , named after the composer alexander borodin who composed two famous string quartets . they often got invitations to play in the west , but the soviet communist government would not allow them to travel out of their country . the quartet had to accept the concerts that their government allowed them to do . they played at the funerals of the dictator stalin and the composer sergei prokofiev which were on the same day ( both men died on 5 march 1953. however , they were not paid anything for either event .
