Liquorice     liquorice ( uk ) or licorice ( us ) ( see spelling differences ) ( , or ) is the root of glycyrrhiza glabra , from which a sweet flavour can be extracted . the liquorice plant is a legume ( related to beans and peas ) and native to southern europe and parts of asia . it is an herbaceous perennial , growing to 1 m in height , with pinnate leaves about 7–15 centimetres ( 3–6 inches ) long , with 9–17 leaflets . the flowers are 0.8–1.2 cm ( 1/3 to 1/2 inch ) long , purple to pale whitish blue , produced in a loose inflorescence . the fruit is an oblong pod , 2–3 centimetres ( about 1 inch ) long , containing several seeds .
