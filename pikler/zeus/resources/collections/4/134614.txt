Blue-baby syndrome     blue- baby syndrome , or blue baby is usually caused by a heart defect which laymen often call `` a hole in the heart '' . normally , oxygenated blood from the lungs is separated from deoxygenated blood from other tissues . a defect in the heart ventricle walls can allow deoxygenated blood to mix with the blood from the lungs . the resulting blood going through the aorta has less oxygen than usual , and the baby looks blue . an operation is usually done to fix this there are several heart defects which produce this syndrome . there are also some causes which are not related to heart conditions . on november 29 , 1944 , the first successful operation for the heart condition was done at the johns hopkins hospital . in 1976 , the university awarded the surgeon an honorary doctorate . blue baby : a baby who is cyanotic ( blue ) , due usually to a heart malformation that prevents the baby 's blood from being fully oxygenated . the bluish color reflects the deoxygenated state of the blood . ( oxygenated blood is red . ) sometimes the term `` blue baby '' is also applied to a child who is cyanotic due to failure by the lungs to oxygenate the blood .
