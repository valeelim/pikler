Penetrometer     a penetrometer is a device to test the strength of a material . it is often used on soil . the penetrometer is dropped on soil or it is pressed against soil to measure how deep a hole it makes . this is a way to decide if soil is strong enough to build a road on . scientists can use a penetrometer to measure how much moisture is in soil . penetrometers are used on space probes such as the cassini-huygens probe , to measure the amount of moisture in soil on other planets . sources . - university of arizona document , `` an impact penetrometer for a landing spacecraft '' . - a picture of a penetrometer from the european space agency ( esa )
