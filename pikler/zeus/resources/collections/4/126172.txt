James Abbott     general sir james abbott ( 12 march 1807 â€“ 6 october 1896 ) was a british army officer in colonial india . he became famous in the northwest frontier region of india in the middle part of the 19th century ( today this is the khyber pakhtunkhwa province of pakistan ) . he was one of henry lawrence 's 'advisers ' to the sikhs , after the first anglo-sikh war ( 1846 ) , and then became the first deputy commissioner of hazara ( 1849 - 1853 ) . as part of the treaty of lahore signed after the defeat of the sikhs in the first anglo-sikh war , hazara and kashmir were going to be transferred to gulab singh , hazara however stayed under the lahore government and governed by james abbott . the pakistani city of abbottabad was founded by him in january 1853 and is named after him . james abbott was the brother of augustus abbott and frederick abbott , who also served as officers in the forces of the east india company . his picture was created by the artist b. baldwin , this picture is located in the national portrait gallery in london , though it is not currently on display .
