Mongolic languages     the mongolic languages are a group of languages that are spoken in central asia . some linguists propose to put mongolic languages in the same group as the turkic and tungusic languages . they call this group the altaic languages , but not all linguists agreed upon this . the best-known member of this language family is mongolian . in cyrillic orthography as it is used in mongolia it is written монгол хэл , and in the vertical uygur-derived script as used in it is inner mongolia in china it is written mongγol kele ) . it is the primary language of most of the residents of mongolia , and is spoken by around 5.7 million people in mongolia , russia , and china . references . - γarudi ( 2002 ) : `` dumdadu üy-e-yin mongγul kelen-ü bütüče-yin kelberi-yin sudulul '' [ the study of grammatical forms in middle mongolian ] . kökeqota : öbür mongγul-un arad-un keblel-ün qoriy-a . - janhunen , juha ( ed . ) ( 2003 ) : `` the mongolic languages '' . london : routledge . - janhunen , juha ( 2003a ) : `` written mongol '' . in : janhunen 2003 : 30-56 .
