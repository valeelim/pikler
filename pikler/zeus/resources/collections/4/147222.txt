British Rail Class 101     the british rail class 101 diesel multiple units were built by metro-cammell at washwood heath in birmingham from 1956 to 1959 , following construction of a series of prototype units . this class proved to be the most successful and longest-lived of all br 's `` first generation '' dmus , with the final five units being withdrawn on 24 december 2003. the oldest set was , by then , just over 47 years old .
