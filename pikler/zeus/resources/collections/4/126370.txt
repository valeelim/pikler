Almaty     almaty ( ; formerly known as `` alma-ata '' ( алма-ата ) , also `` verniy '' , ( верный ) ) is the largest city in kazakhstan . it has a population of over 1,700,000 people ( as of 2015 ) . eight percent of the population of the country live in almaty . it was the capital of kazakhstan from 1927 to 1998. although it is not the capital today , almaty is still the major commercial center of kazakhstan . the city is close to china , and was once on the northern silk route between china and the middle east . its name derives from the turkic languages . the city is in an earthquake zone . there have been three quite serious earthquakes in the last 150 years . on 4 february 1854 the russian empire decided to build a fort verniy on the west bank of the malaya almatinka river . later russian settlers arrived . in 1922 the soviet union renamed the city to alma-ata . in 1927 alma-ata became the capital of the kazakh ssr . almaty has a hot-summer humid continental climate ( dfa in the koeppen climate classification ) . almaty tv tower is the highest tv tower above sea level ( 1130 m. ) . the tower is the highest building in the city ( 372 m. ) . this building able to withstand 10.0 magnitude .
