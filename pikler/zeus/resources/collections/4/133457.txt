Hazratbal Shrine     the hazratbal shrine ( kashmiri : حضرت بل , literally : majestic place ) , is a muslim shrine in srinagar , jammu and kashmir , kashmir . it contains a relic believed by many muslims of india to be a hair of the islamic prophet muhammad . the name of the shrine comes from the arabic word hazrat , meaning holy or majestic , and the kashmiri word bal , meaning place . the shrine is on the left bank of the dal lake , srinagar and is considered to be kashmir 's holiest muslim shrine . the moi-e-muqqadas ( the sacred hair ) of mohammed is believed to be preserved here . the shrine is known by many names including hazratbal , assar-e-sharief , madinat-us-sani , or simply dargah sharif .
