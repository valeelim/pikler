British Rail Class 126     the introduction of what became the class 126 diesel multiple units originated in a british transport commission report of 1952 that suggested the trial use of diesel railcars . br 's swindon works were chosen to design and build express units for the ex-north british railway edinburgh waverley to glasgow queen street route . swindon built a second batch in 1959/60 for use on services from glasgow to ayrshire .
