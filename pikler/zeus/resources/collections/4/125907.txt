Avignon Papacy     the avignon papacy refers to the 70 years when the popes lived in avignon instead of living in rome . this period started in 1309 and ended in 1376 when pope gregory xi returned to the pope 's residence in rome . there was an attempt to return the papacy to avignon , but it was not successful . the bishops of rome who lived in avignon were : 1. pope clement v : 1305–1314 1. pope john xxii : 1316–1334 1. pope benedict xii : 1334–1342 1. pope clement vi : 1342–1352 1. pope innocent vi : 1352–1362 1. pope urban v : 1362–1370 1. pope gregory xi : 1370–1378 two antipopes were based in avignon as well : - clement vii : 1378–1394 - benedict xiii : 1394–1423 ( expelled from avignon in 1403 ) related pages . - western schism other websites . - avignon tourist office website ;
