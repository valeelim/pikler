Pygmy marmoset     pygmy marmosets are the smallest marmosets and one of the smallest of all primates . they inhabit brazil , ecuador , peru and colombia . most pygmy marmosets lives in troops of two to nine individuals , with a dominant male , a breeding female , and up to four successive litters of offspring . the modal size of a standard stable troop would be 6 individuals . pygmy marmosets have tawny agouti fur ( the tip of the hair is a different color to the base ) with an indistinctly dark-ringed tail . they live in flooded forest near rivers , bamboo thickets and the edges of agricultural fields . pygmy marmosets mainly feed on gum ( tree exudates , sap ) , but they also eat fruit , nectar and insects . they gouge holes in the bark of trees , and revisit these holes daily to collect the sap . the marmosets hang on with specially adapted claws .
