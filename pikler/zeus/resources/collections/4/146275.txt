Charles Wood     charles wood ( born armagh , 15 june 1866 ; died cambridge , 12 july 1926 ) was an irish composer and teacher . charles wood was born in armagh , ireland . his father sang in the cathedral choir . charles had music lessons from the cathedral organist , then he went to the royal college of music where he studied composition with c.v.stanford and hubert parry . he also learned to play the horn and piano . after four years he went to selwyn college , cambridge , at first to study , then to teach harmony and counterpoint . in 1889 he got a teaching job at gonville and caius college , cambridge . soon he became their first director of music and organist . when stanford died , wood became professor of music at cambridge . wood is mainly remembered today for his anglican church music . he wrote music for the communion service and service settings of the magnificat and nunc dimittis . these are often sung today in cathedrals and churches . he wrote many fine anthems including `` o thou , the central orb '' . in cambridge he is remembered for the chimes he wrote for the clocks of gonville and caius college .
