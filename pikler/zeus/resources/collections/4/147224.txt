British Rail Class 103     the british rail class 103 diesel multiple units were built by park royal vehicles with diesel engines by british united traction ( but ) . ordered in the first half of 1955 , 20 of these sets were built by park royal at the crossley motors works in stockport of the acv group . they consisted of a power car and a driving trailer . standard but equipment was fitted , with 'a ' type engines . a two-car set with 16 first class and 100 second class seats weighed just under 60 long tons , representing 1,150 lb ( 520 kg ) a seat and had 5 hp per ton of empty weight or 4.35 hp per ton when full .
