Coat of Arms of France     the national emblem of france has been a symbol of the country since 1953. it is not officially the coat of arms but it appears on french passports . on it you can see : - a wide shield with the head of a lion and two letters , `` rf '' . this means `` république française '' ( french republic in `` french '' ) . - a laurel branch that symbolises victory of the republic . - an oak branch that symbolises perenity or wisdom . - the fasces is a symbol associated with justice ( from roman axes .
