Stoicism     stoicism was a school of hellenistic philosophy and was founded in athens by zeno of citium in the early third century bc . it concerns the active relationship between cosmic determinism and human freedom , and the belief that it is virtuous to maintain a will ( called `` prohairesis '' ) that is in accord with nature . stoic doctrine was popular in greece and the roman empire from its founding until all the schools of philosophy were ordered closed in 529 ad by the emperor justinian i , who perceived their pagan character to be at odds with his christian faith . stoic philosophers . - antipater of tarsus ( 210 bc129 bc ) - cato the younger ( uticensis 94 bc46 bc ) - chrysippus ( 280 bc204 bc ) - cleanthes ( of assos ) , ( 330 bc232 bc ) - diodotus , ( ~120 bc59 bc ) , teacher of cicero - diogenes of babylon ( 230 bc150 bc ) - epictetus ( 55 ad135 ad ) - hierocles ( 2nd century ad ) - marcus aurelius ( 121 ad180 ad ) - panaetius of rhodes ( 185 bc109 bc ) - posidonius of apameia ( ~135 bc51 bc ) - seneca ( 4 bc65 ad )
