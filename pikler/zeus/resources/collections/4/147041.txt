British Rail Class 24     the british rail class 24 diesel locomotives , also known as the sulzer type 2 , were built from 1958 to 1961. one hundred and fifty-one of these locomotives were built at derby , crewe and darlington , the first twenty of them as part of the 1955 british rail modernisation plan . this class was used as the basis for the development of the class 25 locomotives . the final survivor , no . 24081 , was withdrawn from crewe depot in 1980 .
