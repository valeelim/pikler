Conviction     in law , a conviction is the verdict that results when a court of law finds a defendant guilty of a crime . the opposite of a conviction is an acquittal ( i.e . `` not guilty '' ) . a minor conviction is a warning conviction , and it does not affect the defendant but does serve as a warning . in scotland there can also be a verdict of `` not proven '' , which counts as an acquittal . a convicted person is also called a convict .
