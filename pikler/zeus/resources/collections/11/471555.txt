Snake case     snake case ( or snake_case ) is the process of writing compound words so that the words are separated with an underscore symbol ( _ ) instead of a space . the first letter is usually changed to lowercase . some examples of snake case would be `` foo_bar '' or `` hello_world '' . it is commonly used in computer code . at least one study found that readers can recognize snake case faster than camelcase . if the first letter of each word is uppercase , this is known as train-case . programming languages that use snake case . - ocaml - c++ - erlang - perl - php - python - ruby - rust related pages . - camelcase
