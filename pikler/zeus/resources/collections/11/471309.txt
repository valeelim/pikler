Mollicutes     the mollicutes are a class of bacteria with no bacterial cell wall . `` mycoplasma '' is the best-known genus . they are very small , only about 0.2–0.3 μm in size , and have a very small genome . most move about by gliding across host cells , but `` spiroplasma '' are helical and move by twisting . mollicutes are parasites of various animals and plants , living on or in the host 's cells . many cause diseases in humans . `` mycoplasma '' and `` ureaplasma '' stick to cells in the lungs or sex tubes like the urethra . `` phytoplasma '' and `` spiroplasma '' are plant pathogens with insect vectors . bugs such as leaf-hoppers ( homoptera ) are the vectors . they deliver the parasites which they stick their mouth-parts into a plant to suck its sap .
