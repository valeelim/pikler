Thyroid-stimulating hormone     thyroid-stimulating hormone ( also known as thyrotropin , or tsh ) is a hormone which stimulates the thyroid gland . the thyroid gland secretes thyroid hormones , which control how fast the body 's chemical functions go ( the metabolic rate ) . thyroid hormones influence the metabolic rate in two ways : by stimulating almost every tissue in the body to produce proteins and by increasing the amount of oxygen that cells use . thyroid hormones affect many vital body functions . thyrotropin is a glycoprotein hormone synthesized and secreted by thyrotrope cells in the anterior pituitary gland , which adjusts the endocrine function of the thyroid gland .
