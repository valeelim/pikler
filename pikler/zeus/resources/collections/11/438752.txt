United States debt ceiling     the united states debt ceiling is a limit set by congress that tells how much money the government can borrow at any given time . its basic purpose is to try to lower the national debt or prevent excessive borrowing . the debt ceiling was created in 1917 under the second liberty bond act . it was designed to fund the us as it entered world war i. before the creation of the debt ceiling , the president determined how much debt could be incurred in a given time . this was changed in 1917 to prevent the president from having too much borrowing power . the debt ceiling in 2011 was 14.3 trillion dollars . < br >
