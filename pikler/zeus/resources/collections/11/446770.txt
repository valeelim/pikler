Economic Recovery Tax Act of 1981     the economic recovery tax act of 1981 , also known as the erta or `` kemp-roth tax cut '' , was a federal law signed into law in the united states in 1981. it was an act `` to amend the internal revenue code of 1954 to encourage economic growth through reductions in individual income tax rates , the expensing of depreciable property , incentives for small businesses , and incentives for savings , and for other purposes '' .
