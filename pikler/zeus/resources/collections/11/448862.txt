Brampton Bryan Castle     brampton bryan castle is a castle in the small village of brampton bryan in north-western herefordshire , england , 50m south of the river teme . the castle guarded an important road from ludlow along the teme valley to knighton and on into central wales . the castle is listed in the domesday book ( 1086 ) . in 1295 it was described as a `` tower with curtilage '' . the castle was almost entirely destroyed during the civil war . the ruins that are still standing include an outer gate house , parts of the inner gatehouse , part of the south wall of the kitchen , and the great hall . when bryan de brampton died in 1294 , his son-in-law , robert harley , inherited the castle . for almost 700 years since the castle has remained in the harley family . during the civil war , the castle was defended by lady brilliana harley , the wife of robert harley . harley was a puritan and a member of parliament .
