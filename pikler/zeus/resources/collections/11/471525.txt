Whoopee cushion     a whoopee cushion , also known as a razzberry cushion , is a practical joke device , used in a form of flatulence humour . it produces a noise resembling a `` raspberry '' or human flatulence . it is made from two sheets of rubber that are glued together at the edges . there is a small opening with a flap at one end for air to enter and leave the cushion . it is used by first inflating with air , then place it under a chair cushion for an unsuspecting victim to sit on . it is intended to embarrass the person . the roman emperor elagabalus was known to use an early version of a whoopee cushions at dinner parties . it is very similar to a pig-bladder used by court jesters in the middle ages . it would produce the same results . the modern version was re-invented in the 1920s by the jem rubber co. of toronto , canada . employees were experimenting with scrap sheets of rubber to produce the gag item .
