Turner syndrome     turner syndrome , also known as 45 x , is a genetic disorder of human females . it is usually caused by the loss of one of the x chromosomes . turner 's syndrome occurs in 1 out of 2000 , to 1 out of 5000 female births . the condition is much more common `` in utero '' ( that is , before birth ) . about 1–2 % of all conceptions are affected , of which only 1 % survive to birth . girls with turner syndrome often have webbed necks , short stature , broad chests and low hairlines . heart disease , hypothyroidism ( reduced hormone secretion by the thyroid ) , and diabetes are other problems that may be present . autoimmune diseases and infertility may also occur . these girls usually have normal intelligence but may have some trouble with vision and hearing . girls with turner syndrome sometimes receive human growth hormone ( hgh ) . human growth hormone regulates growth in the human body . human growth hormone helps girls with turner syndrome grow normally . many girls affected by turner ’ s syndrome also may experience learning disabilities . these are usually related to subjects that are not related to verbal subjects . a girl ’ s visual or motor coordination may be impaired , and she may have trouble with her spatial perception as well .
