Lady tasting tea     lady tasting tea is the name of a famous randomized experiment designed by ronald fisher in 1935. the experiment is the original exposition of fisher 's notion of a null hypothesis . fisher 's description is less than ten pages long and is notable for its simplicity and completeness regarding terminology , calculations and design of the experiment . the example is loosely based on an event in fisher 's life . the lady in question was muriel bristol , and the test used was fisher 's exact test . the lady in question claimed to be able to tell whether the tea or the milk was added first to a cup . fisher proposed to give her eight cups , four of each variety , in random order . one could then ask what the probability was for her getting the number she got correct , but just by chance . - the experiment provided the lady with eight randomly ordered cups of tea â€“ four prepared by first adding milk , the others prepared by first adding the tea . she was to select the four cups prepared by one method .
