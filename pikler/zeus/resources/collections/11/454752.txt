Politics of the United States     the united states is a federal constitutional republic , in which the president of the united states ( the head of state and head of government ) , congress , and judiciary share powers reserved to the national government , and the federal government shares sovereignty with the state governments . the executive branch is mostly about the president and is independent of the legislature . legislative power is rights in the two chambers of congress , the senate and the house of representatives . the judicial branch ( or judiciary ) , made of the supreme court and lower federal courts , focuses on the judicial power ( or judiciary ) . the judiciary 's main focus is to change the meaning of the united states constitution and federal laws and rules . this includes solving problems between the executive and legislative branches . the federal government 's layout is explained in the constitution . two political parties , the democratic party and the republican party . they have made a large difference in american politics since the american civil war .
