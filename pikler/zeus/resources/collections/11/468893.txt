Hunter River (Western Australia)    hunter river ( western australia ) the hunter river is in the kimberley region of western australia . the headwaters of the river rise below donkins hill . the river flows in a south-westerly direction . it empties into prince frederick harbour . then the river flows to the york sound and the indian ocean . the river was named by the explorer philip parker king in 1820. he was on the `` mermaid '' while making charts in the area . the river was named after the surgeon on the `` mermaid '' , james hunter . the river has two waterfalls . they are the hunter falls and the donkin falls . both falls drop . a large rock formation is at the mouth of the river . the area is popular with tourism companies . wilderness cruises operate in the area .
