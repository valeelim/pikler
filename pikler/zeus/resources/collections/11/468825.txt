Derwent River     the derwent is a river in tasmania , australia . it was named after the river derwent , cumbria , by british commodore john hayes who explored it in 1793. the name is brythonic celtic for `` valley thick with oaks '' . john hays placed the name `` derwent river '' only in the upper part of the river . matthew flinders placed the name on all of the river . the banks of the derwent were once covered by forests and occupied by aborigines . european settlers farmed the area and during the 20th century many dams were built on its tributaries . other websites . - derwent estuary program - upper derwent issues
