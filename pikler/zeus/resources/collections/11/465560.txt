Gall–Peters projection     the gall–peters projection is an equal-area map projection . it is a map style that has areas near the equator appear to be smaller than they are . those areas closer to the poles appear larger . it was named after james gall and arno peters . a controversy came up in the late 20th century about the political implications of map design . maps based on this projection are promoted by unesco . they are also widely used by british schools .
