Landmark decision     a landmark decision , or landmark court decision , establish new precedents that establish a significant new legal principle or concept . or it changes the interpretation of existing law . in commonwealth countries , a reported decision is said to be a `` leading decision '' . this is when it has come to be generally regarded as settling the law of the question involved . a landmark decision is `` a most important case which has establish a law firmly in an area , usually referring to a u.s. supreme court case . '' a landmark decision may have either long-term or short-term significance . politics , economics or other changes in society may reduce the effects of a landmark decision . a landmark decision is one that changes an entire area of the law during a period of time . the united states constitution did not provide for judicial review of laws and court decisions . it was a power the us supreme court assumed ( took ) for itself with its first landmark decision . in the decision `` marbury v. madison '' ( 1803 ) the court established its `` power to say what the law is '' . the court gave itself the right to interpret the constitution .
