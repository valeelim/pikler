Transglutaminase     a transglutaminase is an enzyme that forms a chemical bond between two protein molecules . the reaction also produces a molecule of ammonia . transglutaminases were first described in 1959. in 1968 it was discovered they form a role in blood coagulation . transglutaminase have many uses . they can be used as a binding agent to improve the texture of protein-rich foods such as surimi or ham . other examples of foods made using transglutaminase include imitation crabmeat , and fish balls .
