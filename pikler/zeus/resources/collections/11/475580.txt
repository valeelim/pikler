Edmund Wnuk-Lipiński     edmund wnuk-lipiński ( 4 may 1944 – 4 january 2015 ) was a polish professor of sociology . he was the founder and first head of the polish academy of sciences institute of political studies , rector of collegium civitas in warsaw . he was a fellow at the institute of human sciences in vienna , the university of notre dame , and wissenschaftskolleg in berlin . he is a member of the polish national council for civil service and the national council for european integration . wnuk-lipiński was born in sucha . he was the author of a social fiction dystopia trilogy , `` apostezjon '' ( `` wir pamieci '' , `` rozpad polowiczny '' , `` mord zalozycielski '' ) . he was the winner of janusz a. zajdel award in 1988 for `` rozpad polowiczny '' . wnuk-lipiński died in warsaw , poland , aged 60 .
