Tarsal formula     the tarsal formula is the number of segments on the tarsi of arthropods . the tarsus is the bottom part of an arthropod 's legs , see diagram . the formula has 3 numbers a-b-c , starting with the front leg ( a ) , then the middle leg ( b ) , then the hind leg ( c ) . for example , a tarsal formula of `` 5-5-4 '' means there are 5 segments in the fore leg 's tarsi , 5 segments in the middle leg 's tarsi , and 4 segments in the hind leg 's tarsi . this character is especially useful at family rank and higher . the characteristic is useful when identifying insects .
