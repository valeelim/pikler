Adze     an adze is an ancient type of `` edge tool '' . it dates back to the stone age . adzes are used for smoothing or carving wood in hand woodworking . they are similar to an axe , but with the head mounted perpendicular ( at right angles ) to the handle . the cutting edge was made from chipped flint in the mesolithic , polished stone in the neolithic , and metal later on . a very similar ( but blunt ) tool used for digging in hard ground is called a mattock .
