Lake Hindmarsh     lake hindmarsh , in western victoria , australia , is the state â€™ s largest natural freshwater lake . the nearest towns are jeparit to the south and rainbow to the north . it is a large shallow lake which is often dry . the lake filled after floods in 2011 , having been dry for nearly ten years . the area around the lake is the traditional country of the gromiluk , a group of the wotjobaluk people . explorer edward eyre camped at lake hindmarsh in 1838 while searching for an overland route from melbourne to adelaide . by 1845 europeans were settling near lake . in 1859 the moravian ebenezer mission was established nearby . lake hindmarsh is the first lake of the wimmera river terminal wetlands . its water comes directly from the wimmera river . when full , the lake covers , is deep and holds 378 gl of water . it is a wetland of national significance . if lake hindmarsh overflows , water flows on to the deeper lake albacutya , which is a ramsarwetland of international importance . because it is so shallow , water in lake hindmarsh evaporates quite quickly . when it is full , evaporation from the lake is around 140,000 ml per year . as the wimmera river usually only adds about half that amount , the lake is rarely full . it takes three to four years to evaporate entirely .
