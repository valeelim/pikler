Fire of Manisa     the fire of manisa refers to the burning of the city of manisa in turkey by the greek army . it happened during the greco turkish war of 1919-1922. the town was burned between 5 and 8 september 1922. more than 10,000 buildings were destroyed . this was about 90 percent of the town . thousands of people died in the flames or were shot dead by the greeks . turkish sources claim 4,355 died and 300 girls were raped . the town of manisa was part of the ottoman empire . turks and greeks were living there together . the greeks wanted manisa to become part of greece . in may 1919 a greek army occupied the town . then there was war between greeks and turks for three years . in the summer of 1922 turks defeated the greeks . the greeks fled to the aegean sea to be transferred to greece . during their retreat the greeks burned towns and villages of turks . manisa too was burned down . the local turks fled to the mountains . most of the town was destroyed and later rebuilt . some turkish authors wrote about their experiences in turkish literature .
