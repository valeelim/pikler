Apse     in architecture , the apse ( from latin `` apsis '' : `` arch , vault '' ) is a semicircular recess covered with a hemispherical vault or semi-dome . it is similar to another form known as an `` exedra '' . in romanesque , byzantine and gothic christian abbey , cathedral and church architecture , the term is applied to a semi-circular or polygonal termination of the main building . this is usually at the liturgical east end ( where the altar is ) . it is also regardless of the shape of the roof , which may be flat , sloping , domed , or hemispherical .
