Aydıncık, Mersin    aydıncık , mersin aydıncık is a district of mersin province on the mediterranean coast of turkey . it is from mersin and from antalya . aydıncık has also been called `` celenderis '' , `` gilindire '' , kelenderis ( κελενδερίς in ancient greek ) . in 1228 celenderis castle was captured from the armenians by the ottomans . the coast was settled by turkish peoples . the town 's name changed to gilindere . it continued to be an important port between anatolia and cyprus until the beginning of the 20th century . it was renamed aydıncık in 1965. aydıncık is near the mediterranean sea . there are a lot of beaches in aydıncık . there are yenikaş , hacıbahattin , karadere , karaseki , teknecik , yeniyürük , yeniyürükkaş , eskiyürük , pembecik and duruhan villages in aydıncık . the remains of ancient celenderis are very few . the ruins today are mostly covered over by the expanding modern aydıncık . fortifications may still be seen around the modern lighthouse near the harbor . there is a landlocked bay with its famous spring to the west at soguksu . here there are ancient ruins there is a bath at the head of the bay . there are also traces of archaeological debris on the peninsula at its mouth .
