Bradford Interchange     bradford interchange is one of two main railway stations in bradford , west yorkshire . the other is bradford forster square . it is a transport interchange in bradford , west yorkshire , england . it consists of a railway station and combined bus and coach station adjacent . the interchange was designed in 1962. at the time it was hailed as a showpiece of european design when it opened in 1971. it is served by the majority of bus services in the city centre . it is also served by national express coaches . the railway station , which is one of two in the city centre , is served by northern rail . it is also the terminus for grand central services to kings cross railway station . the station opened as part of the leeds and bradford railway in 1850 as `` bradford exchange '' it has been rebuilt since . the latest part to be rebuilt is the bus station in the 1990s . the station has an unusual layout with a concorse on the ground floor and both the bus and train stations on the first floor . they are accessed by escalators . the railway station is a terminus station .
