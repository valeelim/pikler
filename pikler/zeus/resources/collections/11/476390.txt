Herding     herding is the act of bringing individual animals together into a group ( herd ) . it is maintaining the group and moving the group from place to place . many thousands of years ago herding became a way of life for many nomadic people . today it is still a necessity for farmers , ranchers and those who raise domestic animals . some animals instinctively gather together as a herd . a group of animals fleeing a predator will herd together for . some predators , such as wolves and dogs , have instinctive herding abilities that come from primitive hunting instincts . but there can be a fine line between chasing and herding . dogs that show basic herding instincts can be trained to compete in herding and stock dog trials . it 's a sport that dogs and their owners can enjoy together . herding is used in agriculture to manage domesticated animals . herding can be performed by people or dogs trained to control the movement of livestock under the direction of a person . animals such as cattle , sheep and goats are commonly herded by humans and dogs . also herd dogs may be used to gather and herd turkey , geese , ducks and chickens .
