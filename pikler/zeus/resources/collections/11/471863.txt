Yonne (river)    yonne ( river ) the yonne is a river in the northern half of france . it flows mainly through the western part of the bourgogne-franche-comté region , giving its name to the yonne department and to several `` communes '' . it is the main left tributary of the seine river . the river was named during the roman empire as `` icauna '' , a latin word that likely came from the celtic name for the river . the yonne river has a length of and a drainage basin with an area of . its average yearly discharge ( volume of water which passes through a section of the river per unit of time ) is 91.70 cubic metres per second at courlon-sur-yonne . the source of the yonne is on the `` prénely '' mountain on the southern end of the `` morvan '' hills in the glux-en-glenne `` commune '' , nièvre department , at an altitude of about . the yonne flows , in general , from the southeast to the northwest and passes through the 115 `` communes '' , most of them in the bourgogne-franche-comté region ; the main `` communes '' are : - nièvre ( 58 ) : corbigny , clamecy
