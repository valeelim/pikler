RM4SCC     royal mail 4 state customer code ( commoly abbreviated to rm4scc ) is a two-dimensional barcode that was develooped by royal mail in england . it is used for marking the postcodes of letters and parcels in the uk . the system , or one of its variants is also used in other countries . the printed code is optimized for being read at a high speed . the barcode consists of a number of vertical bars . each bar has one of four possible states . four bars are necessary to code one of 36 possible characters . two of the four bars point upwards , the other two point downwards . rm4scc uses start and stop symbols . the rm4scc barcode includes a checksum , which is also encoded as a character . there are other barcode systems , which are similar . tnt post uses a system called kix which stands for klant index ( customer index ) . this system does not use the start and stop characters , and does not include a checksum . universal postal union has defined a standard how the system should be used . note that the code is read and printed at high speed , at which it is usually not possible to print straight lines ; for this reason , the lines being `` bent '' is a normal state .
