Aortic aneurysm     an aortic aneurysm is a general term for an enlargement ( dilation ) of the aorta to greater than 1.5 times normal size . while the cause of an aneurysm may be unknown , the end result is weakness in the wall of the aorta at that location . the aneurysm may occasionally cause pain , which is a sign of impending rupture . when rupture occurs , massive internal hemorrhage results , and , unless treated immediately , shock and death can occur within minutes .
