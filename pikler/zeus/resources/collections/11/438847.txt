Maria I of Portugal     maria i ( english : `` mary i '' ) ( 17 december 1734 – 20 march 1816 ) was the queen of portugal , brazil and the algarves . known as maria the pious ( in portugal ) , or maria the mad ( in brazil ) , she was the first undisputed queen ( in her own right ) of portugal . her court , then under the direction of her eldest son and heir dom joão , the , moved to the then portuguese colony of brazil . later on , brazil would be elevated from the rank of a colony to that of a kingdom . this led to the formation of the united kingdom of portugal , brazil and the algarves . maria was born at the ribeira royal palace in lisbon . on the day of her birth , her grandfather , king joão v , created her the princess of beira . she was the eldest of all her four siblings . when her father succeeded to the throne in 1750 as josé i , maria became his heiress presumptive and was given the traditional titles of princess of brazil and duchess of braganza . she was also an infanta of portugal from birth and thus entitled to the style of royal highness . she grew up during the dominating marquis of pombal
