2014 Hong Kong protests     the 2014 hong kong protests , also known as the umbrella revolution , are a series of protests in hong kong that started on 27 september 2014. they are protesting against the standing committee of the national people 's congress ' ( npcsc ) decision on the proposed electoral reform for the planned 2017 hong kong chief executive election . the npcsc would need a nomination committee to pre-approve up to three electoral candidates before they go on to a vote that involves the general population . after the election , the chief executive-elect still has to be formally appointed by the central government before they can officially take their post . the student groups hong kong federation of students and scholarism started protesting outside of the government headquarters on the 22 september , 2014. during the evening of 26 september , there were a group of several hundred demonstrators that broke through a security barrier and had entered the plaza in front of the central government complex . the police then closed off and blockaded it overnight . this caused more protesters to join and surround the police . the standoff between the protesters and police lasted for a day , during which the police started attacking unarmed protesters with shields , batons , and pepper spray . the movement occupy central with love and peace announced that they would start their civil disobedience campaign with starting immediately .
