Spur (zoology)    spur ( zoology ) a spur is a bone covered in horn that grows out of an animal 's body . while claws or nails grow from the toes , spurs grow on other parts of the foot . these parts are usually connected with joints . spurs are mostly found on the hindfeet , but some birds have spurs at the edge of their wings . not many mammals have spurs . the male platypus has spurs on the heels of its hind feet . the spurs are hollow and connected to a venom gland . this helps the platypus kick painfully . echidnas have spurs as well , but they are not venomous . fossils of some early mammals are found to have spurs also .
