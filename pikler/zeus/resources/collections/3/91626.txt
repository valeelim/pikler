Super Tuesday     in the united states , super tuesday commonly refers to the tuesday in early february or march of a presidential election year when the greatest number of states hold primary elections to select delegates to national conventions at which each party 's presidential candidates are officially nominated . more delegates can be won on super tuesday than on any other single day of the primary calendar , and accordingly , candidates seeking the presidency traditionally must do well on this day to secure their party 's nomination . in 2008 , super tuesday was february 5 ; 24 states held primaries or caucuses on this date , with 52 percent of all pledged democratic party delegates and 41 percent of the total republican party delegates at stake .
