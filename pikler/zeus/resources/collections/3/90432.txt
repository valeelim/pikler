Congo River     the congo river ( also known as zaire river ) is the largest river in africa . its overall length of 4,700 km ( 2,922 miles ) makes it the second longest in africa ( after the nile ) . the river and its tributaries flow through the second largest rain forest area in the world , second only to the amazon rainforest in south america . the river also has the second-largest flow in the world , behind the amazon , and the second-largest watershed of any river , again trailing the amazon . its watershed is a little larger than that of the mississippi river . because large parts of the river basin sit above and below the equator , its flow is steady , as there is always at least one river having a rainy season . the congo gets its name from the old kingdom of kongo which was at the mouth of the river . the democratic republic of the congo and the republic of the congo , both countries sitting along the river 's banks , are named after it . from 1971 to 1997 , the democratic republic of the congo was called zaire and its government called the river the zaire river .
