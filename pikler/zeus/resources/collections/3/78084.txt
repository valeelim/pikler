Boscobel House     boscobel house is a house on the shropshire/staffordshire border , near wolverhamptonand albrighton , england . it was built around 1632 when landowner john gifford of white ladies priory turned a farmhouse into a hunting lodge . charles ii famously hid in a tree at the boscobel house to escape discovery by parliamentary soldiers after the battle of worcester in 1651. the tree became known as the royal oak 42 days after the battle of worcester , charles escaped to france on a coal ship called `` surprise '' . nine years later , charles ll was invited back to england to become king . in 1660 , he came back to england to be the king and charles named that day `` oak apple day '' . other pages . - escape of charles ii other websites . - bbc on boscobel house - english heritage entry : boscobel house - roland film 's documentary on the restoration of boscobel house - postcard images from a personal site
