Erriapus (moon)    erriapus ( moon ) erriapus or saturn xxviii , is non-spherical moon of saturn . it was found by brett gladman , john j. kavelaars , et al . in 2000 , and given the designation s/2000 s 10. it was named in august 2003 after erriapo , a giant in gaulish mythology . later , the spelling was corrected to `` erriapus '' . erriapus is about 10 kilometres in diameter , and orbits saturn at an average distance of 17,300,000 km in 871 days . erriapus is a member of the gallic group of non-spherical , sharing a similar orbit and displaying a similar `` light-red '' colour , erriapus is thought to have been formed in a break-up of a bigger body . other websites . - david jewitt pages
