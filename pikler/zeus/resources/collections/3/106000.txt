Hyōgo Prefecture     hyōgo prefecture is a prefecture in the kansai region of japan on the island of honshu . the capital city is kobe . in the meiji period , hyōgo prefecture was formed by merging the provinces of harima , tajima , awaji , and parts of tamba and settsu . in 1993 , himeji castle was named a unesco world heritage site . it is in the city of himeji . in 1995 , the great hanshin earthquake damaged kobe and neighboring osaka prefecture . it caused the deaths of 5,000+ people . hyōgo has coastlines on two seas . the sea of japan is north of the prefecture and the seto inland sea is on the south . awaji island in the inland sea is part of hyōgo . hyōgo shares land borders with osaka prefecture , kyoto prefecture , tottori prefecture and okayama prefecture . there are twenty-nine cities in hyōgo prefecture , including national parks are established in about 20 % of the total land area of the prefecture . `` izumo daijinju '' is the chief shinto shrines ( `` ichinomiya '' ) in the prefecture . related pages . - provinces of japan
