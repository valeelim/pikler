Sula Sgeir     sula sgeir is a small , uninhabited scottish island in the north atlantic . one of the most remote of the british isles . the island is inhospitable to humans . there is a ruined stone house called taigh beannaichte ( blessed house ) on the east headland sgeir an teampall , the island is formed by pieces of rocks , where are often hard to walk on . sula sgeir is a national nature reserve since 1956. it is an important place for birds . the most famous part of island are the caves , which can be explored . there are 5,000 breeding pairs of gannets on sula sgeir , which they share with other bird species such as black-legged kittiwakes , guillemots , puffins and fulmars .
