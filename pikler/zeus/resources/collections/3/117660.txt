Flight recorder     a flight recorder is a recorder placed in an aircraft to help with investigating an aircraft accident or incident . for this reason , they have to be capable of surviving extremely rough treatment . they are usually built to withstand an impact of 3600 `` g '' and temperatures of over 1,000 Â°c ( as required by eurocae ed-112 ) , because a powerful impact followed by a very hot fire is what often happens in a plane crash . there are two types of protected flight recorder , flight data recorder ( fdr ) and cockpit voice recorder ( cvr ) . in some cases , the two recorders may be combined in a single fdr/cvr unit . the first combined flight recorder was invented by australian scientist , david warren . these recorders are often called `` black boxes '' . they can be used in other vehicles including trains and cars . the car 's recorder can monitor the speed of the car or the sharpness of turns . the train 's recorder can be used to upgrade after a crash to reduce the chance of another crash .
