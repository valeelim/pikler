Gigue     the gigue or giga is a lively baroque dance . `` gigue '' ( pronounce `` jeeg '' ) is the french word , and `` giga '' ( pronounce `` jee-ga '' ) is the italian word . the english word is `` jig '' and in england this refers to a folk dance . the gigue was one of the movements in a series of dance movements called suites written by baroque composers . the baroque suite normally had an allemande , a courante , a sarabande and a gigue to finish with . sometimes there were one or two other movements as well . the gigue usually has a compound metre such as 6/8 , 6/4 , 9/8 or 12/16 . they often have a contrapuntal texture and each phrase is usually four bars long . like all the dances in the baroque suite , the gigue is in binary form . the second half will usually start with the main tune in inversion ( upside down ) .
