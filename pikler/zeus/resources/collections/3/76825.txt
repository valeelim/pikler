Secondary source     a secondary source is a document or recording that writes or speaks about information that is one step removed from the original source . the secondary source depends on the `` primary source '' or `` original source '' of the information . secondary sources interpret , evaluate or discuss information found in primary sources . in historiography , a secondary source is a study written by a scholar about a topic . secondary sources frequently cite primary sources and other secondary sources . they rarely cite tertiary sources . a secondary source would be a book on 13th century politics , while the magna carta itself would be a primary source . many secondary sources use extensive citations in the form of footnotes or endnotes . the main use of secondary sources in law is to explain the law . they also explain legal concepts . they are used to analyze and describe laws as well as comment on them . judicial decisions and opinions by qualified experts are secondary sources . other pages . - source text
