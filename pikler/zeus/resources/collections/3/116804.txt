United States Army Air Force     the united states army air forces ( usaaf ) was the military aviation arm of the united states of america during and immediately after world war ii . it was the direct precursor to the united states air force , established in 1947. at its peak strength in 1944 , the usaaf had over 2.4 million men and women in service , nearly 80,000 aircraft and 783 permanent bases , and operated from more than 2,000 airfields worldwide . the air corps became the army air forces in june 1941 to provide the air arm a greater autonomy in which to expand more efficiently , and to provide a structure for the additional command echelons required by a vastly increased force . although other nations already had separate air forces independent of the army or navy ( such as the british royal air force and the german luftwaffe ) , the usaaf remained a part of the united states army .
