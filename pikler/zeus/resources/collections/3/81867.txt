Cupid (moon)    cupid ( moon ) cupid is an inner moon ( which makes it one of the closer moons ) of uranus . it was found by mark showalter and jack j. lissauer in 2003 using the hubble space telescope . it was named after a character in william shakespeare 's play `` timon of athens '' . it is the smallest of the closer uranian moons , estimated to be only about 18 km in diameter . this and the dark surface made it too dim to be detected by the `` voyager 2 '' cameras during its uranus flyby in 1986. the orbit of cupid is only 863 km from the orbit of the bigger moon belinda . amazingly , compared to mab and perdita , the recently found uranian moons , it does not seem to be perturbed . following its discovery , cupid was given the designation s/2003 u 2. it is also designated uranus xxvii . it should not be confused with the asteroid 763 cupido . other websites . - hubble uncovers smallest moons yet seen around uranus â€“ hubble space telescope news release ( 2003-09-25 )
