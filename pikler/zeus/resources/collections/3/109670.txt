Anglo-Saxon architecture     anglo-saxon architecture was a period in the history of architecture in england and parts of wales , from the mid-5th century until the norman conquest of 1066. anglo-saxon buildings in britain were generally simple , made from wood with thatch for roofing . there are few remains of anglo-saxon architecture . at least fifty churches are of anglo-saxon origin . all surviving churches , except one timber church , are built of stone or brick . other websites . - anglo-saxon architecture in early british kingdoms
