Hockey puck     a puck is a disk used in some games serving the same use as a ball in ball games . the best-known use of pucks is in ice hockey , a major international sport . hockey pucks are hard disks of rubber that are used to play ice hockey . a standard ice hockey puck is black , 1 inch thick ( 25.4 mm ) , 3 inches in diameter ( 76.2 mm ) , and weighs between 5.5 and 6 ounces ( 156-170 g ) . pucks are often marked with silkscreened team or league logos on one or both faces .
