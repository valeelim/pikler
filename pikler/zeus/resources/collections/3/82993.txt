Phonetics     phonetics ( from the greek word φωνή , `` phone '' which means 'sound ' or 'voice ' ) is the science of the sounds of human speech . someone who is an expert in phonetics is called a phonetician . phonetic theory regards the nature of sounds in speech ( called phones ) and how they are made , heard and thought of . phonology , which came from it , studies sound systems and sound units ( such as phonemes and distinctive features ) . phonetics is one of the two parts of orthographical linguistics , the other part being spelling , differing from grammar and lexis . phonetics has three main branches : - articulatory phonetics , regarding the place of articulation and the movement of the lips , tongue , vocal tract , and vocal folds , - acoustic phonetics , concerned with the traits of the sound waves and how the inner ear hears them , and - auditory phonetics , concerned with speech perception ( mostly how the brain thinks of what the ears hear ) there are more than 100 different phones recognized by the international phonetic association ( ipa ) and are shown in their writing system called the international phonetic alphabet .
