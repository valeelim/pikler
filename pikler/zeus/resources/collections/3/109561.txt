Province of Forlì-Cesena     the province of forlì-cesena ( ) is a province in the emilia-romagna region in northeastern italy , on the adriatic sea . its capital is the city of forlì . the province was created as `` province of forlì '' with the car plates `` fo '' . in 1992 , the southern part of the province was separated to form the new province of rimini and the name of the province was changed to the present of province of forlì-cesena and the car plates were changed in 1999 to `` fc '' . the iso 3166-2 of the province was also changed to `` fc '' in 2010. the province of forlì-cesena has an area of .. it is , together with the rimini province , the most southerly of the provinces in the emilia-romagna region ; it has a short coast along the adriatic sea . the provincial capital , the city of forlì , is along the montone river and at about southeast of bologna , the capital of the region . the province is bordered to the north by the province of ravenna , to the northwest by the province of mantua in lombardy , to the west by the province of florence province in tuscany , to the south by the province of arezzo , also in tuscany , to the southeast by the rimini and to the east by the adriatic sea .
