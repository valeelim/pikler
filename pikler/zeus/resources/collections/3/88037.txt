Topographic prominence     in topography , prominence , also known as autonomous height , relative height or shoulder drop ( in america ) or prime factor ( in europe ) , is a concept that is used in the categorization of hills and mountains , also known as peaks . it is a measure of the independent stature of a summit . the prominence of a peak is the height of the peak ’ s summit above the lowest contour line encircling it and no higher summit . related pages . - topography other websites . - http : //www.peaklist.org a website about mountain prominence , with lists and/or maps covering the entire world down to 1500m of prominence ( the `` ultras '' ) . - edward earl ’ s article on topographic prominence - mountain hierarchy using prominence islands
