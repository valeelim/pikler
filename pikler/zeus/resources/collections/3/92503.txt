Palindromic prime     a palindromical prime number is a prime number that reads the same when reversed . palindromical prime numbers include : 2 , 3 , 5 , 7 , 11 , 101 , 131 , 151 , 181 , 191 , 313 , 353 , 373 , 383 , 727 , 757 , 787 , 797 , 919 , 929 , 10301 , 10501 , 10601 , 11311 , 11411 , 12421 , 12721 , 12821 , 13331 , 13831 , 13931 , 14341 , 14741 , 15451 , 15551 , 16061 , 16361 , 16561 , 16661 , 17471 , 17971 , 18181 , 18481 , 19391 , 19891 , and 19991. out of the above list , 2 is the only number that is not an odd number . almost all palindromic numbers are composite , for any base . 11 is the only palindromic prime with an even number of digits because all palindromic numbers with an even number of digits can be divided by 11 , which means they are not primes . related pages . - palindrome - list of prime numbers
