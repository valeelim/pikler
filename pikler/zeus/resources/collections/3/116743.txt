Low Countries     the low countries is a term used to refer to a geographical region , especially during the middle ages . this region is around the deltas of the rhine , the scheldt and the meuse ( `` maas '' ) . today , the low countries consists of the netherlands , belgium , luxembourg , and the parts of lower-saxony ( in germany ) that are in the region . sometimes , the french netherlands ( nord-pas-de-calais ) are included as well . the dutch name of the netherlands is `` nederland '' , which literally means `` low country '' . the region was usually referred to as `` de nederlanden '' .
