Warsaw Uprising (1794)    warsaw uprising ( 1794 ) the warsaw uprising of 1794 ( also called the `` warsaw insurrection '' ) was started by a group of polish people who lived in warsaw . with help from the polish army , they tried to stop russia from controlling the biggest city in poland . it started on april 17 , 1794 , which was soon after tadeusz kościuszko 's victory at an important battle . although the russian army was larger , had better weapons , and killed many polish people , russian soldiers started to lose a lot of land and suffered from many deaths . the polish people had been preparing to fight in the uprising since the spring of 1793. they managed to become very popular : they formed a large army of volunteers who were led by a shoemaker , jan kiliński . apart from this army , the most famous group to take part in giving the polish people their freedom were the poles who had been forced to join the russian army . a person who watched the fighting was a french artist , jan piotr norblin , who drew pictures of the uprising .
