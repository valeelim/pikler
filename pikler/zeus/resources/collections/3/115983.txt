Barbour County, Alabama    barbour county , alabama barbour county , alabama is a county of the u.s. state of alabama . its name is in honor of james barbour , who served as governor of virginia . as of 2000 the population is 29,038. its county seat is clayton . barbour county was created on december 18 , 1832 from former creek indian territory and a portion of pike county . its borders were altered in 1866 and 1868. the election riot of 1874 occurred near comer . major highways . - u.s. highway 82 - u.s. highway 431 - state route 10 - state route 30 - state route 51 adjacent counties . - russell county , alabama - northeast - quitman county , georgia - east - stewart county , georgia - east - clay county , georgia - southeast - henry county , alabama - south - dale county , alabama - south - pike county , alabama - west - bullock county , alabama - northwest national protected area . - eufaula national wildlife refuge cities and towns . - bakerhill - blue springs - clayton - clio - eufaula - elamville - louisville
