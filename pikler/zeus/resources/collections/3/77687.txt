Egg (food)    egg ( food ) most eggs that are eaten by humans , including bird eggs and turtle eggs , have a protective , oval eggshell , the albumen ( egg white ) , the vitellus ( egg yolk ) , and various thin membranes , that are layers like skin . every part of the egg is edible , although the eggshell is usually thrown away . eggs are a good source of protein and choline . roe and caviar are edible eggs produced by fish . birds ' eggs are a common food source . the most commonly used bird eggs are those from the chicken , the duck , and the goose , but smaller eggs such as quail eggs are occasionally used as a gourmet ingredient , as are the largest bird eggs , from ostriches . most commercially produced chicken eggs intended for human consumption are unfertilized , because hens are kept without any roosters . fertile eggs can be bought and eaten as well . there is no difference in the food value of fertilised and unfertilised eggs . fertile eggs for eating do not have a baby bird inside . this is because eggs have to be kept cool before they are sold , and the cold stops the young inside from starting to grow .
