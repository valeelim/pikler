Black-capped chickadee     the black-capped chickadee ( `` poecile atricapillus '' ) is a passerine songbird of the family paridae . it lives in canada , alaska and the northern united states . this is a small , common bird which sings a simple song . the black-capped chickadee is about 12–15 cm ( 5–6 in ) long and weighs 9-15 g ( 0.32-0.49 oz ) . the male and female black-capped chickadees look the same . the black-capped chickadee has black and white on its head . the top of the head and throat are black , and the sides are white . it has a short , black beak . the back of the body of the black-capped chickadee is gray . the front of the body is white and light brown . the bird has short wings and a short tail . the song of the black-capped chickadee is a clear whistle . in the summer , black-capped chickadees eat many insects . in the winter , they eat seeds and berries . they will hide seeds in different places and return later to eat them . when two chickadees want to build a nest , they will use a hole in a tree .
