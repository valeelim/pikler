Buncombe County, North Carolina    buncombe county , north carolina buncombe county is a county in the u.s. state of north carolina . in 2006 , 222,174 people lived there . its county seat is asheville . the county was made in 1791 from parts of burke county and rutherford county . in 1808 the western part of buncombe county was made into haywood county . in 1833 parts of burke county and buncombe county were combined to make yancey county , and in 1838 the southern part of buncombe county was made into henderson county . in 1851 parts of buncombe county and yancey county were combined to make madison county . and , in 1925 a small part of mcdowell county was made into a part of buncombe county . buncombe county is part of the local land-of-sky regional council of governments . these are the most important roads in buncombe county : - interstate 26 - interstate 40 - u.s. highway 74 - u.s. highway 70 - u.s. highway 23 - u.s. highway 19 - u.s. highway 25 these counties are connected to buncombe county : - madison county , north carolina - north
