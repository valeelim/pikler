Louis-Nicolas Clérambault     louis-nicolas clérambault ( born paris , 19 december 1676 ; died paris , 26 october 1749 ) was a french organist and composer . clérambault ( pronounce “ clay-ram-bow ” ) came from a musical family . he learned to play the violin and harpsichord when he was young , and also learned the organ , composition and singing . he became the organist at the church of the grands-augustins and then at saint-sulpice . he worked for madame de maintenon who arranged concerts for king louis xiv . he also had a job at the royal house of saint-cyr , which was a school for young girls from the poor nobility . he directed the music there , played the organ and trained the choir . clérambault was the first important french composer of cantatas . they were often about greek and roman myths . he also composed for the organ , harpsichord and violin . he was thought of as france ’ s greatest organist . two of his sons took over jobs held by their father after his death . other websites . - groves dictionary online
