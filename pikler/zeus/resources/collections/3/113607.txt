Staphylococcus aureus     staphylococcus aureus is the most common cause of staph infections . it is a round bacterium , often living on the skin or in the nose of a person . about one third of the u.s. population are carriers of staph . aureus `` staphylococcus aureus '' can cause a range of illnesses from minor skin infections to life-threatening diseases . `` s. aureus '' can be treated with antibiotics , but they easily get resistance to antibiotics . when they are resistant , they are called mrsa ( methicillin-resistant s. aureus ) .
