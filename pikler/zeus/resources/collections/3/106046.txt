Spectacled bear     the spectacled bear ( `` tremarctos ornatus '' ) , also known as the andean bear is the last of the lineage of short-faced bears . locally , it is also called `` ukuku '' , `` jukumari '' or `` ukumari '' . the other short-faced bears are now extinct . they lived during middle to late pleistocene age . the spectacled bear is a relatively small species of bear native to south america . it has black fur with a distinctive beige-coloured marking across its face and upper chest . males are 33 % larger than females . males can weigh 130 – 200 kilograms ( 286 – 440 lb ) , and females 35 – 60 kilograms ( 77 – 132 lb ) . they are found in several areas of northern and western south america , including western venezuela , colombia , ecuador , peru , western bolivia , northwestern argentina , and eastern panama . spectacled bears are the only surviving species of bear native to south america , and the only surviving member of the subfamily tremarctinae . their survival has depended mostly on their ability to climb even the highest trees of the andes .
