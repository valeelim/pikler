Semitic languages     semitic refers to a group of languages including hebrew , arabic and aramaic ( the semitic peoples ) . the semitic languages are a branch of the afroasiatic language family , which originated in the middle east . semitic languages are spoken by more than 470 million people across much of western asia , north africa and the horn of africa , as well as in large expatriate communities in north america and europe . the name was taken from shem , a son of noah in genesis ( chapters 6-11 ) .
