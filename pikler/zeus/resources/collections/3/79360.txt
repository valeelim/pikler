1935 Labor Day Hurricane     the labor day hurricane of 1935 was the strongest tropical cyclone during the 1935 atlantic hurricane season . the second tropical cyclone , second hurricane , and second intense hurricane of the season was the most intense atlantic hurricane that affected the united states , and it remains the first of three category 5 hurricanes that struck the country . after forming as a weak tropical storm east of the bahamas on august 29 , it slowly proceeded westward , became a hurricane on september 1 , and underwent rapid intensification prior to striking the upper florida keys on september 2. after landfall at its peak intensity , it continued northwest along the florida west coast , and it weakened prior to landfall near cedar key on september 4. the large and strong hurricane caused severe damage in the upper florida keys , and a storm surge about 18 to 20 feet affected the region . the hurricane 's strong winds destroyed most of the buildings in the islamorada area , and many world war i veteran workers were killed by the storm surge . portions of the key west extension of the florida east coast railroad were severely damaged or destroyed . the hurricane also caused additional damage in northwest florida , georgia , and the carolinas . in total , more than 400 people were killed .
