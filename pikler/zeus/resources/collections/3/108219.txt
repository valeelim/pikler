Three Mile Island accident     the 1979 three mile island accident at the `` three mile island nuclear power plant '' in pennsylvania was `` the single most important event in the fifty-year history of nuclear power regulation in the united states '' . many people have seen the event as a turning point for the nuclear power industry in the united states . the three mile island power station is near harrisburg , pennsylvania . the accident began on wednesday , march 28 , 1979 , and resulted in a partial core meltdown in unit 2 of the nuclear power plant . unit 2 's pressurized water reactor was of 900 mwe capacity . the chain of events that led to the crisis at the tmi plant included several minor equipment failures that operator errors drastically compounded , resulting in a major accident . the three mile island accident is largely seen as a failure of crisis management . according to one reviewer of the book : reactor operators were not trained to deal with accident conditions , and the nrc had not established effective communication with utilities . moreover , once the accident occurred , the lines of authority proved to be ill defined . the public received conflicting reports that caused needless panic and evacuations . it was these systemic weaknesses in the regulatory system that allowed gifted people to make the mistakes they did .
