Nightingale     the nightingale ( `` luscinia megarhynchos '' ) is a small bird . it is also known as the rufous or common nightingale . it used to be classed as a member of the thrush family turdidae . it belongs to a group of more terrestrial species , often called chats . it is a migratory insectivorous species . it breeds in forests and scrubs in europe and south-west asia . the distribution is more southerly than the very closely related thrush nightingale `` luscinia luscinia '' . it nests on the ground within or next to dense bushes . it passes the winter southern africa . studies have shown that nightingales seem to choose places to breed that meet certain criteria ( wink 1973 ) : - less than 200 meters above mean sea level - mean air temperature during the growing season above 14 °c - more than 20 days/year on which temperatures are above 25 °c - less than 750mm of rain per year . - no closed canopy . the nightingale is slightly larger than the robin , at 15-16.5 cm length . it is plain brown above except for the reddish tail . it is buff to white below . sexes are similar .
