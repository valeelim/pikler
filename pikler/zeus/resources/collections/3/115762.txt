Betta splendens     betta splendens , also called the siamese fighting fish is a species of gourami commonly kept in aquariums . the betta comes from thailand , and lives naturally in large shallow rice fields in thailand . the betta has been bred to have large and color fins . these fish are rather aggressive towards other fish . other names for this fish are : - siamese fighting fish - fighting fish - plakad or plakat - betta - betta fish males appear more colorful and tend to have larger fins than the females . males come in all sorts of color patterns and fin types . female bettas have a more pointy shape at their stomachs . a popular fin type is the veiltail , which has long droopy fins resembling a dress . red and blue are the most common colors in betta fish . white , black , and green bettas are considered rare by some . the males will fight each other and can not be put together . they will also nip ( bite ) the fins of some fish . the females sometimes fight but can be kept in groups . though they can be kept with some species that do not look like the betta , such as the cory cat fish .
