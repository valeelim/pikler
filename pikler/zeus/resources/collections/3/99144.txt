Middle Eastern theatre of World War I     the middle eastern theatre of world war i was a large theatre during the first world war . battles here were fought between the allied powers ( especially britain and russia ) and the central powers ( especially the ottoman empire ) . this theatre was the largest of all the theatres of wwi . it was made of four main campaigns . in the sinai and palestine campaign , mainly british forces defeated the turkish army . in the mesopotamian campaign , british forces , mainly the army of india , also defeated the turkish army . in the caucasus campaign , turkey fought against the russian empire and other forces . in the dardanelles campaign the turkish army defeated british forces , mainly colonial . there were also minor campaigns in arabia and southern arabia , aden , and persia . fighting here began on october 29 , 1914 and ended on october 30 , 1918. a peace treaty was signed on august 10 , 1920 .
