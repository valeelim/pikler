Physical constant     in science , a physical constant is a number ( physical quantity ) that does not change . this is different from a mathematical constant , which is not based on a real measurement . there are many physical constants in science . some famous ones are the speed of light , planck 's constant , the gravitational constant , and avogadro 's number . some scientists think that physical constants may decrease as the universe gets older . there has been no evidence to support this yet . constants that do not have units ( like length or speed ) are called fundamental physical constants . some believe that if the physical constants were different , there would be no life in the universe . they say that the universe seems to be fine-tuned for intelligent life . other pages . - scientific constants named after people - fine-tuned universe - physical law - codata - kooyman constant
